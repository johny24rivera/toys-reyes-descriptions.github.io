Sweeps (Botcon 2009 Attendee-Only Exclusives)

Allegiance: Decepticon
Size : Deluxe (comes sold in sets of three)
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dull teal, silver, and some transparent amber-yellow, white, and yellow
Rating : 9.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron Sideways. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron Sideways here .)

Given that the Sweeps are meant to essentially be "extensions" of Scourge's will, it makes sense that not only do they use the same mold, but they're colored similarly. All of the silver paint apps on the Sweeps here are the same shade of color and in the same exact same places as the silver on Botcon 2009 Scourge , and the white plastic is the same on the Sweeps as on Scourge as well. There are only two differences between the Sweeps and Scourge, though they are major-- the darker blue on Scourge has been replaced with a paler, more teal blue on the Sweeps, and the red & transparent red on Scourge is a yellow & transparent yellow on the Sweeps. This gives the effect of making the Sweeps look like less-powerful, less-evil versions of Scourge-- which they are, so it works. That said, I think the yellow and pale blue don't quite as well together as Scourge's red and darker blue. The Sweeps also come three-to-a-pack, and though there are three different bio cards for them, they're all identical, which I think isn't a particularly cool decision for something like Botcon exclusives, I would've preferred if they had just allowed you to purchase one, but make more of them then the other exclusives so people could "troop-build" if they wanted to. (Still, at least for Botcon exclusives they're cheap-- they're only about twice retail price if you divide the price of the set into thirds, which is honestly rather impressive.)
The Botcon Sweeps have the same tiny head mold as Scourge, so my comments on his head are reflected on the Sweeps' heads as well.
The Sweeps are a nice idea, and their color scheme comes off quite nicely (though not quite as nicely as Scourge's). I wish that they differed a little more from Scourge in terms of their paint apps, though, just to help differentiate them more. I also think it would've been a better idea if there were some slight differences between the individual 3 Sweeps since you can only buy them in sets of three, just so you know which is which (either that, or sell them individually). I don't think the Sweeps are overall quite as good as Scourge, but you're probably going to find them for cheaper on eBay, and it's still an excellent mold with good colors, so if you're in the market for Botcon exclusives, you might wanna check out these.


Sweep Two Bio :
FUNCTION: Close Combat Specialist
MOTTO: "Those who oppose us will be broken with my bare hands!"
Created from Decepticon wreckage, Sweep Two is the hand-to-hand combat specialist of the Sweep legion. While his fellow Sweeps generally prefer using energy and ballistic weaponry, he prefers to use his knowledge of Cybertronian martial arts to enforce Scourge's will. He is extremely disciplined and believes that following orders is paramount to everything else. He represents the most brutal aspects of Scourge's personality and fulfills his role with no remorse. Scourge often relies on him to keep the other Sweeps in line, and his mere presence is often sufficient to reach this goal. Sweep Two is an expert in the use of his dual electron blades. He is often seen using them to dispatch his enemies instead of utilizing his array of energy weapons. This is both a strength and a weakness as he will sometimes opt for close combat when ranged combat would be the more tactically sound option.

Sweep Six Bio:
FUNCTION: Tactician
MOTTO: "Action without thought will crush any hope of victory."
Forged from the remains of fallen Decepticon warriors, Sweep Six is the most skilled tactician among the Sweeps. He is always quiet, calm and self-spoken. This disposition should not be taken as a sign of weakness, however. Underneath his muted exterior is a strong, calcuating mind. He sees the Autobot/Decepticon conflict as a carefully planned series of tactical decisions. Scourge often consults him on planning missions for the Sweeps, and it is a role he executes with grim determination. Utilizing finely tuned sensors and communications equipment, he often creates three-dimensional battlefield images and transmits them to other warriors as he flies over the combat zone. Sweep Six lacks patience for those who he deems his intellectual inferiors and has been known to lash out against them. This sometimes causes friction with other Decepticons.

Sweep Seven Bio :
FUNCTION: Weapons Officer
MOTTO: "Weapons defeat warriors, ideas defeat armies."
Like all the other warriors in the Sweeps army, Sweep Seven was created using parts from the bodies of deactivated Decepticons. Decepticons often have trouble maintaining conversations with Sweep Seven due to his tendency to walk off, deciding his time is better spent developing some new weapons system for the Sweeps. Scourge relies on him to keep the weaponry used by the Sweeps the most current and lethal available. He sees weapons development as an intellectual challenge and grows quickly bored once a new weapon has been developed. Scourge often feels Sweep Seven is an extension of both his own intellect and creativity and uses him accordingly for the benefit of the Decepticon Empire. When called into battle, Sweep Seven is often eager to test his new weapons, but they sometimes backfire on him.
