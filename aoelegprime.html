Optimus Prime (Legion; AoE) {Wal-Mart Exclusive}

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legion
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Light milky bluish gray, black, and some moderately light red and silver
Rating : 7.5

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original RotF Legends class Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original RotF Legends class Optimus Prime here .)

Well, I guess with Wal-Mart exclusives Hasbro wasn't planning on making any new molds, because this definitely isn't Optimus' Age of Extinction design; it's his design from the previous three movies. Heck, in vehicle mode it's even DONE UP in the same color scheme that he had in the previous three movies, with the front section being red with the blue flames instead more of him being blue like in his new design. It's quite obviously Optimus' regular color scheme, mostly being re-released for those who weren't able to get it the first couple of times. There are some minor differences between this one and the initial release; namely, a little less paint! Well, okay, that sounded a little too negative, even though he DOES have a little less paint; the robot chest specifically should have more red and/or silver on it. The paint on the lower arms is blue now as opposed to red, and the robot knees are now unpainted gray plastic, with an Autobot symbol on them instead. There's also fewer flames on the front in truck mode. On the upside, the upper legs are painted black now, so that's something. As far as plastic colors go, the gray has a slight blue tinge to it, though it's still in the same "realm" of colors as with the gray on most other Movie Optimus toys. The blue is also just the teensiest bit brighter. Aaand... that's it. This is more of a variant than your usual themed redeco.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Optimus.
AoE "Legion" Optimus Prime certainly isn't going to satisfy fans looking for a teeny version of the AoE design, but if you haven't gotten the nifty RotF Legends class Optimus Prime yet, here's your chance, and with a relatively accurate color scheme too (though not QUITE as accurate as the usage of the mold that comes with the Wal-Mart exclusive Grimlock 2-pack).

Review by Beastbot
