Bumblebee (Gold)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark metallic gold, black, glossy dark blue, and some silver and metallic blue
Rating : 7.8

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the "G1" Universe 2.0 Legends class Bumblebee toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the "G1" Universe 2.0 Legends class Bumblebee toy here .)

"Gold" Bumblebee here is actually an homage to G1 Goldbug, who was himself an "upgrade" of the Bumblebee character. I imagine why he's simply called Gold Bumblebee here is both for copyright reasons and for name recognition. Anyways, Gold Bumblebee certainly lives up to his name, as his main color is a dark, metallic shade of gold. If I were to be absolutely nitpicky about this I would've liked the gold to have been just a TAD lighter, but it still looks magnificent as a main color. The black used as the color for the windows and wheels serves a dark neutral color, while in robot mode a dark glossy blue is revealed that goes quite well with the gold AND the black. However, those are pretty much the only colors for Gold Bumblebee-- his face and headlights are painted silver, which looks quite nice, but I wish there was a little more of said silver simply to get enough of a real "light" color in there. Because he's a "Reveal the Shield" toy, Gold Bumblebee has an allegiance symbol on his chest/roof, which appropriately shows an Autobot symbol if you rub it with your finger.
No mold changes have been made to Gold Bumblebee-- as much as I would've liked for him to have gotten that faceplate that Goldbug had...
Gold Bumblebee is a pretty nice-looking redeco of the Legends "G1" Bumblebee mold, both in terms of being a nice homage to Goldbug while still having a variation of Bumblebee's main color so that kids who didn't have the original wouldn't have had much trouble using this toy as "just" Bumblebee. His colors are definitely an improvement over the original's, though I wish his gold had been a tad lighter (or he had had a few more silver paint apps) to make everything pop just that much more.
