Autobot Stripes (Titans Return)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation to Vehicle : Very Easy
Difficulty of Transformation to Beast : Easy
Color Scheme : Milky orange, black, dull mustard yellow, and some metallic orange, silver, and dark metallic gunmetal gray
Rating : 5.7

Stripes is a pretty awesome-looking smartphone in alt mode. (As an aside, I'm glad they went with a more obscure, previously Japanese-only character for Blaster's tiger buddy than doing another Steeljaw -- not only for variety's sake, but because we already have another yellow smartphone bot.) There's no extras from Stripes' other modes in smartphone form as well. However, like the other Titans Return scout smartphone "minions", he's slightly oddly-propotioned as a smartphone so he can fit in TR Blaster's chest, being a bit thicker and smaller than your average smartphone, with the screen not taking up quite as much as space as you'd think. There's some nice tiger stripes all over the "screen" of the smartphone (in actuality stickers), and some very nice stripe paint apps on the sides of the smartphone, as well. Regarding the stickers, beyond the stripes there's a nice big ol' Autobot symbol in the middle, with some nifty circuitry lines on the "screen" and various symbols on the top and bottom that you'd expect on a smartphone, from a battery life signal to an e-mail icon to a wifi symbol. However, these stickers are rather flimsy, and given that you have to move these pieces around during transformation, the edges can peel a little, which is REALLY unfortunate-- I'm not sure why painted details weren't used here. There's also some black down the middle, which contrasts exellently with Stripes' other major color in this mode-- a rather medium shade of milky orange, which is pretty much exactly the shade you'd expect for a tiger. Finally, there's a couple of nice dark metallic gunmetal gray paint apps near the corners-- so dark they're essentially black, for all intents and purposes.
Stripes' jet mode is actually semi-decent for a tertiary mode. The front is a bit blocky for a jet, but otherwise pretty decent-- the halves of the screen become rather square but fairly nice-looking wings, while the jet nosecone and landing gear/flight stand flip out from the bottom. It's here that we see a noticeable addition to the color scheme-- a dull mustard yellow shade of plastic used on the nosecone and the underside of the wings, which complements the orange quite well in the amounts it's used. I'm glad it's not too bright, or it might've tipped the color scheme a bit too far in that direction. There's some nice mold detailing on the jet nose (given that, what with the smartphone mode, Stripes is fairly sparse in that department on most of his other pieces), and even the tiny little windows are painted black. The weak part of this mode comes towards the rear-- two of the tiger legs are just folded up in a rather obnoxious way. I guess the're SUPPOSED to imitate tailfins, but they do a really poor job of looking like tiger legs. On a lesser note, if you look straight-down in this mode you'll see the other two tiger legs and a bit of a visible gap near the edges of the body, breaking the illusion of the whole middle section being "solid". On a final note, there's two little pegs on the top middle of this mode for a Titan Master to stand on that are also there in the tiger mode.
Speaking of Stripes' tiger mode, it is definitely NOT impressive for what should be his secondary mode. He's got all the necessary "parts"-- a fairly tiger-shaped body, four legs, a tail and head-- but so much of it is FAR too skinny for a tiger. The body isn't quite bulky enough, and the legs in particular are way too lean. Add to this his little yellow stubb of a tail and the two smartphone screen halves that stick out from above his butt very obviously without any real attempt to "integrate" them into this mode, and you have an underwhelming tiger. On the plus side, though, the amount of mold detailing on a few parts-- such as the head and hips-- is quite impressive, though something seems a bit "off" about Stripes' head. The molding is a bit too detailed to the point where he looks a little ugly (though the silver paint on the eyes and teeth helps separate out at least SOME details). Also, oddly, his whole head is painted-- when you think it'd just be milky orange plastic, it's actually a metallic orange. It's generally "close enough" to the milky orange, but it's still a weird decision. I also do like that the main body slopes up a bit near the front like an actual tiger's, and Stripes does have pretty decent articulation for an animal-former of this size; he can move up-and-down at the neck and tail, as well as at the shoulders (ball joints) and back-and-forth at the hips, both sets of knees, and both sets of ankles.
Stripes has a pretty nice color scheme and a pretty great smartphone mode, but that's where most of his positives end. His jet mode is... okay, though very iffy in the back, and his tiger mode has so many proportional maladies it's honestly hard to take it seriously. If you want a beast minion for Blaster, this is really your only option... but that doesn't mean it's necessarily a good one; in fact, I think it's one of the worst molds out of the entire Titans Return line. Too many sacrifices were made to make Stripes have three modes.
