BotCon/OTFCC Exclusive Toys

Every year in the United States, a convention is held that deals exclusively with transformers. In each one of these conventions, the toymakers repaint an original Transformers figure(s) different colors to create a limited set of an all-new transformer. For example, in BotCon 1996, they repainted Optimus Primal(Bat) into Onyx Primal. BotCon transformers are all repaints, but have a low production run, and thus cost quite a bit of money.
In 2003, BotCon underwent a temporary name change-- "The Official Transformers Collectors' Convention" (OTFCC for short). In 2005, however, the name BotCon was regained again, as the convention came under new management and regained the name. (The exclusives for Botcon 2004 are not reviewed here, because that was a different convention.) Non-transformable exclusives, such as PVCs, are not reviewed, as well as custom class exclusives.
In addition, starting in 2005, a special Transformers Collectors' Club (by the same people) was introduced to those willing to pay for it, and every year, a special toy is made available to club members. Every so often, exclusive toys that only Club members would be allowed to purchase would be added to the Club Store, too. In 2013, the Transformers Club began offering an annual "Transformers Subscription Service" (TFSS for short), where you preordered a set of toys to be released slowly throughout the year, and which would be made to order. These-- along with some Japanese eHobby exclusives also made available through the Transformers Club Store via a special partnership between the two companies-- are reviewed below as well.
Sadly, in 2016 Hasbro pulled the plug on Botcon, with the last TFCC exclusives coming out in the first half of 2017.
