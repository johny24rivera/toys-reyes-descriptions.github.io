Bumblebee (Titan Changer) [The Last Knight]

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Orangish "cheesy" yellow, black, and some silver, metallic bronze, and light blue
Rating : 3.4

Bumblebee's Chevrolet Camaro vehicle mode is pretty accurately represented here on his Titan Changer form; the proportions are pretty accurate all around, and the only robot mode extras are the little slanted bits on his back end which become his robot feet. Beyond that, there isn't much to complain about in this mode design-wise. The mold detailing is also pretty accurate as well, with both the basic and the more intricate details present. The curved grill with the large angular bits on the sides are present, keeping this toy accurate as far as the model of Camaro 'Bee is in the movie. He's also got those nice triangular bits on the sides, in front of the car doors; the several vents on the hood; and all the necessary molded-in details on the back end, like the exhaust pipes on the lower sides, all those angles on near the sides, and so on. Many TLK toys have paint detailing problems, and Titan Changer Bumblebee is no exception. There IS a fair amount of black paint used in this mode to contrast against all his yellow (specifically, on his front grill, front hood, and windows), along with the usual black plastic on the wheels. (Due to the transformation, a crooked "U" of yellow pokes out from the black on the front window and hood.) There's also a spot of metallic bronze on that all-important Chevrolet symbol on the front. However, there's a lot of major details still unpainted in this mode, particularly on the front grill and back end, like the headlights/taillights. Still, you get what you pay for.
To transform Bumblebee, just fold his legs down from the back end, unhooking them at the sides, and then rotate his arms out and down from the front portion of the body, and you're done. Bumblebee is very much a "shellformer" toy in this mode, with many of his details just molded into shell portions of the car mode. This doesn't look all that bad for the legs honestly, as there's only the very sides of the car that go out beyond what the mass of his legs should be. However, his arms are absolutely awful, being way too big in the shoulders and ridiculously tiny in the lower arms. His head is also simply molded into the front of a panel that forms part of his front hood, and his chest and waist are molded onto the underside of parts that form much of the top hood. Because of the sizes of the parts these details are molded on, Bumblebee is too skinny at the chest, and because Titan Changers are made to be as large as possible given the budget, his proportions in general are a bit stretched vertically, here. He's also very 2-D; he's extremely flat from a side view, though he's still relatively stable, at least. All of this said, the mold detailing on him IS remarkably intricate and accurate to the show model as much as can be given the proportions. He's got the angular, faux grill on his chest, his "gas cap pelvis", some angular and spring details on his legs, and some wiry details on his tiny arms and hands. Paint-wise Bumblebee especially suffers in this mode. He's got some black on his chest and stomach, and his face is silver with light blue eyes. That's it, and that's not nearly enough given the multitude of detailing on him, with nearly all of it being in yellow; only the tires and small parts on his shoulders and knees are black plastic in this mode. For articulation, Bumblebee can move at the shoulders and forward at the knees. (You can technically move him at two points at each knee, but one just completely breaks the illusion of it being a solid leg and just looks extremely weird, so it's not recommended.)
Bumblebee is the weakest of three TLK Titan Changers, and that's saying a lot given how incredibly budget-minded this subline is. He does have a pretty nice car mode, albeit one that could use a bit more paint, but MAN does his robot mode pay for that. Wacked-out proportions everywhere, almost no paint, everything just detailed onto a shell portion of his vehicle mode... just ugh. Get one of the many other TLK Bumblebee toys instead, even if you have to pay a little more.

Review by Beastbot
