Lockdown (Flip 'n Change) [AoE]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Very dark milky semi-metallic brown, black, silver, and some moderately light blue,Â  and dark metallic blue
Rating : 7.1

Lockdown's vehicle mode for his Flip 'n Change version is a Lamborghini Aventador, just like for his other AoE toys. For this version, though, vertically this mode seems to have just a bit more "bulk" proportionally when compared to his length. It's not a huge difference-- just enough to be noticeable, but it is worth noting. Otherwise, the proportions in this mode are pretty darn spot-on, with the mold detailing being particularly impressive-- all the angles of the Aventador are replicated very accurately on what is AoE Lockdown's largest toy. The "grills" around the rear taillights and front bumper, the ridged rear blue section, the triangular designs on the side doors and the front hood-- it all makes for a mean-looking car, and a fitting one for Lockdown. Like his other toys, Lockdown's main color is a rather dark, milky shade of brown-- I would've preferred black, but this version has a semi-metallic sheen to it which actually looks somewhat similar to a car finish, so I don't mind it as much as I otherwise would. The silver paint on the windows and headlights and the dark metallic blue paint on the rear section also look great, with the silver adding some "light" coloration to this mode and the metallic blue just looking nice regardless. He is missing some paint on some important places, though, such as his taillights and wheel hubs. This being a Flip 'n Change carformer, Lockdown also has the same issues as Flip 'n Change Bumblebee in this mode-- that is, rather obvious rectangular bits sticking out just slightly below where his rear view mirrors are (their blue coloration makes them even more obvious), and the fact that the front third or so of the roof doesn't QUITE sit flush with the rest of the vehicle mode, leaving a noticeable gap that gets worse as you get closer to the bottom of this "seam". It's not a deal-breaker, but a simple shallow peg would have solved this-- I'm not sure why Hasbro did it this way.
Just like other Flip 'n Change carformers, you take apart Lockdown's car mode from the back end, folding out the arms and then flipping the entire toy around said arms a few times either one way or another (depending upon which mode you're transforming him into) and then the transformation's "magically" done. Because Lockdown shares the exact same transformation scheme as Bumblebee, he shares the same issues-- namely, the roof and front of the car mode merely hang behind this mode-- out of the way, but very noticeable and more and more of an eyesore the more you look at him from a side-on view. If yours doesn't "lock" into robot mode position solidly enough, this backpack can also cause him to fall backwards if left standing by himself. He also has the car doors as shoulders-- completely inaccurate, but they still function well enough as said shoulders. His arms are also a tad too long proportionally due to the transformation gimmick, and his articulation is almost nil-- he can move at the shoulders (at four points; one of them is shared between both shoulders, meaning if you move one shoulder up and forward the other will follow due to the gimmick), elbows, and slightly at the wrists. There's no articulation whatsoever at the head or legs. That said, the mold and paint detailing for his robot mode bits-- the head, chest, and legs in particular-- is just awesome. He's got movie-accurate mold detailing all over the place, like the tubes/pistons on his chest and abdomen, the "folded metal" on his abdomen and legs, the fake car front-chest, the relatively large hinged toes, and a pretty good replica of him with his mask down over his face for a headsculpt (the mask, sadly, cannot be flipped up). There's plenty of paint on these parts to lighten up this mode as well-- silver as well as some dark metallic blue, and some metallic green for his face-mask. These look even better given that much of Lockdown's robot bits are black, which I think looks better than the dark brown of his vehicle mode. I wish I could say the same about his arms, which are sadly unpainted. The blue plastic used for Lockdown's shoulders, upper arms, and some other minor transformational parts helps to add some more contrast to this mode, even if it's completely movie-inaccurate.
Flip 'n Change Lockdown has mostly the same weaknesses as the rest of the carformers in this subline-- namely a bunch of kibble behind his back in robot mode, very little articulation, and a vehicle mode with a front section that doesn't sit quite flush with the rest of it. That said, he LOOKS pretty good in both modes, and his colors are largely pretty good, with some excellent mold detailing and some pretty good paint detailing as well. As for my copy, out of all my Flip 'n Change toys this is the one that has the hardest time "locking in" to the robot mode configuration firmly-- meaning that he can fall over onto his back easily-- though I'm not sure if this is just my copy. Still, if you're looking for a simpler-to-transform or a large AoE Lockdown toy, this is the best one.
