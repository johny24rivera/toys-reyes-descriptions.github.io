Slog (AoE) ["G1" Redeco; also covers Platinum Edition*] {Amazon Exclusive}

Allegiance : Autobot
Price : $35 (U.S.)
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation : Hard
Color Scheme : Silver, chrome silver, moderately light red, black, and some moderately light milky gray, dark blue, metallic gold, very light milky gray, and clear plastic
Rating : 8.6 (Amazon Exclusive); 8.5 (Platinum Edition)*

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original AoE Slog toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original AoE Slog toy here .)

Although the rest of the "G1-ish" Dinobots got a G1-themed redeco in an SDCC exclusive boxset , poor ol' Slog was left out, so this was rectified by this Amazon-exclusive single-pack, which came in the exact same packaging as mass-retail Slog, oddly. Anyways, like with his SDCC-exclusive-- and similarly premium-priced-- brethren, Hasbro went pretty much all-out on the G1 redeco. This isn't just a bit of silver and gold paint, folks; for starters, there's LOADS of silver chrome on Slog-- his shoulders, feet, rear lower legs, stomach, and sides of the tail are all chrome, along with parts of his waist. Additionally, as of this writing (12/17) it hasn't even shown a hint of chipping, so it seems to be bonded pretty well. Now, if Slog were ALL silver chrome-- or close to it-- this might get a bit gaudy, but thankfully that's not the case. Most of the other gray plastic on Slog has been coated in a silver paint-- a nice "in-between" the chrome and regular plastic in terms of brightness. The only places unpainted gray plastic is used are on some bendy parts that tend to bend a lot during transformation, where obviously it would be a problem to cover them in paint-- namely, the top tail/robot "skirt" piece and the lower front part of Slog's dino neck. There's also a touch of pearly light gray plastic, but it's only used for a few small connector pieces. He's got some nice gold paint-covered parts on his robot feet and chest, but what's especially cool is that his upper dino neck and head are clear plastic, but then are painted gold on only the inner side of the plastic-- this gives the part a very unique feel, much like the G1 toy that had clear plastic on the outside but gold chrome on the inside. It's a VERY nicely done effect, and something I didn't expect for the premium price. The dark blue eyes also contrast with the gold extremely well. The black and red plastic bits on this version of Slog are the same color-- and largely have the same placement-- as on the mass-release version of this toy, though much of red paint has been removed (there are a few red paint apps on the ends of the tail and-- most notably-- on parts of the robot chest). The parts where the red and gold paint meet on the chest are the only portions of Slog's color scheme I feel don't look all that great-- together they're a bit too light, without silver or black paint to help break up those colors. Although they still look nice, I also wish Slog's guns weren't the same red as on the mass release version-- I would've preferred gold guns to differentiate things a little more.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Slog.
The "G1 Redeco" of Slog looks fantastic, with loads of gold and silver paint and a good amount of silver chrome that looks great without going "overboard" on the shininess. The black helps break up all the shiny a bit as well, and the clear/plastic combo on the dino neck and head looks utterly stupendous. The few places where the red and gold paint directly meet are my only real complaints about the color scheme-- if you're up for the premium price, I'd definitely recommend this over any other versions of this toy, easily.


Slog (G1 Redeco; Amazon Exclusive) Bio :
Slog is low on brainpower, even by Dinobot standards. He's slow, lumbering, and absolutely gigantic. When you're that big, most problems get stamped out pretty quickly, sometimes before you even know they're there. But what Slog lacks in brains he makes up for in brawn. Anything he tramples snaps under his massive feet. The Dinobots know that he can clear a path through any enemy line... as long as they point him in the right direction first.

*A "Platinum Edition" of the G1-colored AoE Dinobots-- all of those in the SDCC exclusive boxset [the toys only, no Ark] as well as Slog, this time-- was released as an online retail exclusive in 2015. This release of Slog is identical to the Amazon exclusive version described above except for one detail-- namely, the robot headsculpt has been changed to look much like G1 Sludge's, though with a bit spikier of a headcrest. It's an okay sculpt, but I don't think the G1 head really fits the movieverse body, so I recommend the Amazon exclusive version over the Platinum Edition version.
