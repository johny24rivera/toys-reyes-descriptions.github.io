Bard of Darkmount (Shattered Glass) [Botcon 2012 Primus Preregistrant-Only Exclusive]

Allegiance : Decepticon (Shattered Glass)
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme: Dull glittery yellow, black, light lime green, and some silver, red, and metallic gold
Rating: 8.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Generations Darkmount. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Generations Darkmount here .)

The "Bard of Darkmount"-- i.e. Shattered Glass Straxus-- has a color scheme that, at first, appears random, or perhaps a "polar opposite" of his regular color scheme. However, it's actually based off a mis-colored version of the character from a UK comic back in 1990. Talk about obscure! Anyways, the most prominent color for the Bard here is rather odd-- it's meant to be gold plastic, but it's actually much more of a mustard yellow with glittery flakey bits in it. By itself it doesn't look bad, but the gold paint right next to it (such as on the bottom of Straxus' feet, which are in plain view in vehicle mode) makes it look really off. That said, the light green plastic complements the mustard yellow and black exceedingly well, and makes the Bard's color scheme a bit more "cheery", as befits a Shattered Glass Decepticon. There's also a few nice red and silver paint apps to help round things out, though these definitely accent colors, no more.
No mold changes have been made to the Bard of Darkmount. Of note is that in addition to being a Botcon 2012 exclusive, the Bard of Darkmount was only available ("free") if you bought the expensive preregistrant Primus package AND picked the set up in person at the convention-- so this toy is no doubt worth quite a lot of money, even for a Botcon exclusive.
The "Bard of Darkmount" seems a bit random-- after all, I don't think anyone was particularly begging for this recolor to be made-- but most of the color scheme works well. The mustard yellow really should've been a gold shade, however-- it that had been the case, I think this whole scheme would've looked much better and the bottom of his feet wouldn't have clashed with the rest of the turret in tank mode. It's definitely my least favorite of the color schemes this mold has had.


Bard of Darkmount Bio :
MOTTO: "The losses of war are cruel, crueler still if no one remembers."
The Bard of Darkmount is the most renowned poet and playwright of Cybertron. His masterpiece, "Xal Unbound" is regarded as the turning point of philosophy and theater for Cybertronian kind. When the war came, he withdrew to his home beyond Darkmount. He did not interfere with the war and the war, bizarrely, returned the favor. It was only when the wall between worlds was breached that he stirred from his sanctuary. His involvement in recent events is a baffling curiosity to most and a dire warning to the wise.
The poet's sonic cannon shatters steel and his pick can lay even the most terrifying Dinobot low, but his voice is his true power. His songs and poems dispel his allies' fears and crush his foes' morale. He is full of joy and wit, to the point that rumors circulate that he has supernatural insight that sets him beyond worldly concerns.
