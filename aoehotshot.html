Hot Shot (AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Black, glittery dark blue, tinted clear plastic, dark milky gray, and some white, metallic gunmetal gray, and red
Rating : 8.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original AoE deluxe Crosshairs toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original AoE deluxe Crosshairs toy here .)

The toy is named Hot Shot, and it may bear a slight resemblance to Hot Shot's Cybertron form in that the alt mode is a blue car-- but really, this is an homage to Tracks. Why didn't they just call him Autobot Tracks? Beats me-- maybe they think it's too likely they'll use that name for a character actually in the movies in the future? Regardless, Hot Shot's main colors are a dark blue and various shades of dark gray/black. The dark blue is a pretty nice shade of the color, having a little glitter in the mix-- not quite enough to make it look outright metallic, but just enough to make it pop a little. The dark blue plastic and paint match each other perfectly, so no worries there. The clear plastic is tinted this time, which works better with Hot Shot's generally darker color scheme-- plain clear plastic would've looked a bit ugly with all the dark colors on this toy, and the tinted plastic helps hide some of the robot parts behind the windows in car mode. Most of the black and dark milky gray is on the robot parts, with the dark gray making up much of Hot Shot's legs, waist, and upper arms. The legs could've used a bit more paint, but otherwise it serves as a decent neutral color for the blue to play off of. The black is mostly reserved for the feet/front grill, the guns, and the wheels-- I would've preferred the black in place of the dark gray in robot mode given the greater contrast, but this is a minor quibble. The best parts of Hot Shot's color scheme are definitely the red and white paint apps used on parts of his robot mode-- namely, the red on his lower arms and the white on his shoulders and crotch. They really contrast extremely well against the otherwise dark color scheme, and look great. That said, the white head with a painted red face just looks weird, especially for a "more realistic" movieverse ultra-detailed headsculpt. It doesn't look good, and not much like either Tracks OR Hot Shot. The vehicle mode, sadly, is basically bereft of either red or white paint apps, with only an Autobot symbol on the hood and some gunmetal gray paint on the headlights. No paint on the front or back bumpers at all, or on the sides-- just blue and black (or tinted clear plastic, in the case of the windows). Given that this is supposed to be a Tracks homage, why not some flames on the hood? SOMETHING to make the toy stand out a bit more in car mode...
No mold changes have been made to AoE Hot Shot.
AoE Hot Shot is an okay redeco taken by itself, but it's not nearly close enough to Hot Shot in terms of overall color scheme to really qualify as a "new version" of that character, and although he looks like Tracks, he doesn't have iconic Tracks bits like flames on the hood-- in fact, his vehicle mode looks very boring overall, with only bits of white and red in his robot mode really saving his entire color scheme from being rather dull. I'd recommend Crosshairs slightly over Hot Shot, mass-release version of the former or otherwise.
