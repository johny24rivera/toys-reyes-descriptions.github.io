Megatron (Animated; McDonald's)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Happy Meal Size
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Light milky gray, black, and some light red
Rating : 2.5

Megatron's vehicle mode is.... um... his robot mode laying down with propellers. Really, I'm not exaggerating that much, LOOK at it. It's pathetic, even for a Happy Meal toy. The cockpit covers up the head pretty well (though the windows could really use some paint), but other than that there's not even an attempt to reshape the robot mode into a vehicle mode. Really, I don't have much else to say about this mode, it's just bad all over. Well, I guess the propellers spin pretty well... but that's it.
Megatron's robot mode is certainly much better than his alt mode, and the proportions are pretty good-- he's very "Animated" in his stance, and the mold detailing is done pretty well, especially on the arms and head. Most of the necessary paint details are filled in-- such as on the head, legs, and waist-- but a couple of red paint apps on his shoulders relaly would've made him look a bit more complete in this mode. So he LOOKS pretty good-- however, that's not to say there aren't some major downsides to this mode as well. For one, he can barely move at all-- he can turn his head and his shoulders can move back and forth SLIGHTLY, but that's it. Also, the propellers that unceremoniously hang off his arms don't have any locking mechanism, so they just swing around even if you move him a little. Finally, the cockpit piece just sticks out of his back almost horizontally. Even making a SLIGHTLY better notch for it in the back would've helped it collapse into the back of the chest pretty well, but nope-- it sticks out there for no real reason, sadly.
Happy Meal Megatron has a decent, if brickish, robot mode that pulls off the "Animated" style quite well, but MAN is his "vehicle mode" irredeemably bad. Just.... get another version of him. ANY version.
