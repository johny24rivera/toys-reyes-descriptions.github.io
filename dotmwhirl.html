Autobot Whirl w/ Major Sparkplug

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Human Alliance Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation to Weapon Mode: Easy
Difficulty of Transformation to Robot Mode : Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately dark brown, light milky gray, black, and some white, red, bright orange, silver, and transparent pale blue
Rating : 8.8

Whirl's alt mode is a sort of personal helicopter, as it's a little "skeleton-y" compared to most other helicopter Transformers. Considering that, though, the proportions are pretty good on this, and the robot mode extras are pretty minimal. The back end obviously has the robot hands molded into them, and on a lesser note, the parts of the helicopter to the sides are kind of obviously his robot legs, but the latter's not a big deal at all, and understandable given the scale. The mold detailing is decent enough, though not outstanding when compared with most other toys of a simliar size-- there's the occasional really detailed bit like where his rotors converge or on the sides, but generally he's got what is needed in terms of mold detailing and not a whole lot more. The color scheme is a bit on the unique side for a Transformer-- there aren't too many mostly brown TFs. The light milky gray is a blah color and I wish it wasn't used even as much as it is as a secondary color, but having some of the silver painted gray was a nice touch, and the black helps provide another dark color to contrast against the gray. The paint apps of bright orange, red, and white also look quite well against the brown and/or light gray, so no complaints there. As you'd expect from a Human Alliance figure, Whirl has a peg hole in his cockpit area for Major Sparkplug or some other Human Alliance human figurine to fit in, and there's also handles for that figure to grasp the "controls" in the cockpit. (As for Major Sparkplug, he's your typical soldier human figurine-- nothing special, here. He has all the standard points of movement that pretty much every other human figurine in this subline has.) As far as other features, Whirl's rotor rotates quite well, offering little resistance. He also has two machine gun emplacements on C-clips, which can be used on four different places on his rotor tail. In addition, those weapons also have two additional bars on each to allow for more C-clip weapons to be attached to THEM, so you can give Whirl quite a lot of firepower if you so desire.
Whirl's weapon mode is essentially his helicopter mode turned on its rear end with the rotors combined together to look like a large railgun and the side pieces and cockpit slightly rearranged so that it looks like a genuine "protection cage" for the human figurine piloting him. The rotor-rail gun is a really creative usage, and the missile packs and guns on the sides definitely give this mode firepower, but otherwise it's a pretty weak weapon mode. The tail rotor section hanging down doesn't even try to look like anything, as do some of the parts on the sides of the vehicle. Like most weapon modes from the subline, it's best ignored.
All of the pieces come together quite well in robot mode, which is arguably Whirl's best mode. The rotor on his back is the only real vehicle mode extra in this mode, and it enhances the silouhette of the figure-- plus it can still rotate in this mode, and if you REALLY dislike it, it can slide off and be put aside. The proportions are a tad off, with his arms being a little small and short, while his hips stick out way too much from his body. (His arms also can't hold any weapons, either, which is a bummer.) On the plus side, the skis on the feet really help to give his design some more uniqueness, and are a throwback to G1 Whirl's feet as well. The cockpit bits compress quite impressively to form a chest that doesn't stick out TOO much from the head-- and the head itself is a completely new design and looks great, particularly with the orange and bronze paint apps on the forehead and sides. The black faceplate paint does mesh a little too well visually with his pale blue transparent visor, so they're rather indistinguishable from each other in most light. (The visor's light piping doesn't work too well, for some reason.) As far as weaponry, Whirl also has C-clip points on his shoulders, so his rotary guns can still be mounted in a good position this mode. His articulation is pretty good for the size, too; he can move at the neck, shoulders (at three points), elbows (at two points), hips, knees (at three points), and ankles (at two points). Given his wide stance and lack of back-heavyness, this means he can take on a pretty good number of poses.
Dark of the Moon Whirl is one of the better molds in the Human Alliance deluxe line, with a pretty solid robot mode and a vehicle mode with only a few minor kibble problems. His weapon mode is pretty crummy (railgun aside), but it doesn't impact the other modes and can be easily ignored. Mildly recommended.


Autobot Whirl Bio :
Autobot Whirl prefers to observe what occurs around him and base his strategy on the information he gathers. His human partner Major Sparkplug agrees, having spent much of his life behind enemy lines and deep undercover.
