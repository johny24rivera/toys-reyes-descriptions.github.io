Autobot Hound (Power Battler; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Dark military green, black, dull grayish brown, and some silver, light sky blue, and orange
Rating : 7.7

Hound's alternate mode is a military transport, and is captured pretty accurately as a Power Battler. The proportions are near-perfect; no issues there at all. The colors are generally fitting for Hound, too, taking the accurate dark military green and adding a fair amount of grayish brown, which contrasts and complements the dark green while also just being a fitting color for a military alt mode, even it's not used on the vehicle in the movie to near the extent it's used on this toy. There aren't any large patches of only one color, which is always good-- that said, given the sheer amount of outstanding mold detailing on this guy, there's lots of details that are begging for some paint. The front windows are painted silver, but the side windows are not; other details like handlebars, spare tire detailing near the center of the figure, and bumpers are all bereft of paint, as well. The abrupt transfer from dark green on the sides of the rear end to grayish brown in the middle of the back end also looks rather odd. In general this mode is robot extra-free from most normal angles, but some robot parts show up from a head-on or side-on look at eye level. Hound's gun and fists do stick out below the bottom of the vehicle a bit to the point where it interferes with him rolling on a level surface slightly, though that's small potatoes compared to his head just being RIGHT THERE underneath the front section, between the two tires; I wish there was a panel or something to cover that up. On a very minor additional note, you can juuust make out the tips of Hound's (unpainted) wheel-heels on the upper back end of this mode, but again-- small potatoes.
Hound's transformation mostly consists of turning his vehicle mode inside-out via a couple of simple panel flips. This results in a robot mode that is a bit more square than you'd think-- the panels that make up the sides of his main body are a bit too flat and the wheels that come off the front of his chest certainly aren't movie-accurate, and his legs are merely molded onto the inside of the panels that form the back half of the vehicle mode (though they are, at least, detailing that sticks out a fair amount so you see them as legs BEFORE you see them as part of two large panels-- that said, the legs are definitely still the weakest part of this toy). The four wheels sticking out the front of the legs are unsightly, as well. Still, legs aside this is a pretty good robot mode given the restrictions of the Power Battler price point and complexity. The mold detailing continues to be darned impressive in this mode, with pistons and the like on his legs all detailed, along with (non-removable) guns and painted wheel-feet. He also has a great, fairly accurate chest sculpt, even if his gut from his movie model isn't really present for transformation purposes. His headsculpt is pretty spot-on, sporting a dark green military helmet and a scraggly black "wire" beard that really helps give this design a lot of character, as do the ammo clips on the arms. The paint detailing in this mode is also thankfully much more evident-- much of it is for black coloration on his arms and legs, though his chest also has a fair amount of dark green paint and his head is wonderfully painted, with a silver face and the usual Autobot bright blue eyes. Hound's Power Battler gimmick is "quick draw", and it's pretty cool without being all that obtrusive-- pulling back on the small tab at the top of Hound's right shoulder will cause that arm to swing forward a bit, the gun that's normally slung underneath that arm rotating forward so it looks like he's ready to shoot whatever's ticking him off currently. It's one of my favorite Power Battler gimmicks, is addictive, and the orange paint looks great against the black of the gun. For articulation, Hound can move at the left shoulder (at two points), right elbow, and I suppose he can rotate a little at the hips, though the further you rotate his legs out the more awkward they look given how all the leg detailing is only on one side of those leg panels. (All of the articulation in his right arm is spring-loaded-- if you let go of the tab, it automatically retracts the arm back to its default position.) It's not much, but given the transformation I'm not sure how they could have fit in anything more than head rotation.
Power Battler Hound is one of my favorites from the subline. Yes, he has very restricted articulation in robot mode and some obvious panel issues in said mode, but his downsides are somewhat minimal. His "Quick Draw" is cool, his transformation is simple yet effective, he's got great mold detailing and decent paint detailing, and he's got good proportions in both modes. He's not a super-articulated, poseable 'bot, but he's not meant to be. A fun, basic figure that's a good substitute if you can't find/afford the voyager version.

Review by Beastbot
