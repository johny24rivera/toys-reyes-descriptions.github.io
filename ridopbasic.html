Optimus Prime (Basic) [RiD]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Basic
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Japanese Name : (Super) Fire Convoy
Color Scheme : Light gray, sparkly red, silver, sparkly dark blue, black, and some dull yellow, chrome silver, flat red, and dull orange
Mold History : Although based off the super version of the same character, the basic version of Optimus Prime was a mold made exclusively for RiD.
Rating : 4.9

Vehicle mode is a fire truck. This mode is nearly right-on when compared to his Super version . He's very accurate in nearly all aspects. The ony aspect that he could use something, though, is in paint detailing. There's some of it- under the ladder platform and on the windows- but that's really it. Some silver detailing on the top front of the middle would have been appreciated. Also, even though Prime has six tires, only four of them are real, spinning ones. Two of them are just molded on, and without any paint detailing on them to even disguise them like the other ones! Kinda took the cheap way out there. Prime's "super mode" extras are NOT in the middle of the truck this time, though, unlike the Super version which is good. The ladder is pretty much the only moving piece in this mode- the base can rotate, and the ladder can slide back and forth.
Optimus Prime's robot mode is very similar to the Super version's "Super" robot mode, even though the transformation is totally different. His Super version's "regular" robot mode is completely skipped in this toy, due to its size. The detailing on this mode is much better than in the vehicle mode, but it still needs a little more on the chest area. This mode wouldn't be that bad if it wasn't for two major things. First off, Prime's back third of the fire truck just hangs off his upper back, and not only hampers the look of the toy a lot in this mode, but also gets in the way quite a bit. Secondly, Prime's feet are curved upwards towards the back, as they're the front parts of his fire truck mode. Because of this, he falls over very easily, even when his fire truck ladder adds a bit of support. Without the ladder, it's impossible to stand him up by himself, with gets really annoying if you want to use the end of the ladder as a gun of some sorts that he has to hold. His articulation is also rather limited in this mode- his head can move, as well as his arms at the shoulders. His legs can also move from side to side, but that's it. And it wouldn't have been that hard to give him some knee articulation or something, either, which makes it that much more annoying. Oh, and this version of Optimus Prime cannot combine with the basic version of Ultra Magnus , so don't expect to have a little Omega Prime or anything.
Although it's certainly cheaper than the super version, so less features are expected, Optimus Prime just doesn't measure up in this version. His truck mode, although good-looking, lacks paint details, and his robot mode falls over way too easily and has a large backpack. Even though he's much more costly, I'd recommend the Super version of Optimus Prime instead unless you're a completist.
