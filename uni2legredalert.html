Red Alert [Universe 2.0]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Series : Classic/Generation One
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : White, red, and some dark red, light dull metallic silvery blue, and black
Rating : 5.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Legends of Cybertron Hot Shot. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Legends of Cybertron Hot Shot here .)

Red Alert here, like many repaints in the Universe 2.0 line, is an homage to the G1 character of the same name in terms of his color scheme and general alt mode. While the original Red Alert had more of an emergency vehicle bent, the Legends Hot Shot mold does the job fairly well with the right color scheme. The white, black, and red color scheme is hardly original by this point, but that doesn't mean that the colors don't go well together-- and it's very fitting for an emergency theme, of course. Red Alert has the same "fire department" symbols on the sides of his vehicle mode that the G1 toy had as well, which is a nice touch. The silvery-blue paint is also a nice color to be used for the windows. Still, that said, there are some things about Red Alert's paint job that I don't like. For one, he really could use a tad more variety, like maybe one more secondary color. The color scheme is just a bit old now, even if it is a direct homage. I'm also not fond of how large the Autobot symbol is on the front hood of his vehicle mode-- a bit more discreet, perhaps? Painting his face red in robot mode also just looks rather ugly and is a bad move. And finally, in robot mode he has a bit too much white showing-- a bit more black on his legs and/or shoulders would have helped to break that up a bit more.
No mold changes have been made to Universe 2.0 Red Alert.
Universe 2.0 Red Alert is a decent enough homage to the G1 version, but the color scheme is hardly original by now, and this mold has been repainted enough where there's been a better color scheme for it released ( Excellion ). Only recommended if you really have a thing for the tiny Legends class Transformers-- otherwise, Red Alert's an easy skip.
