Hardtop (Movie) [Target Exclusive]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Robovision Code for Target.com website : BULLSEYE
Color Scheme : Black, dull pale greenish blue, moderately dark military green, and some silver and yellow
Rating : 7.9

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron Hardtop. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron Hardtop here .)

Hardtop's been repainted for the Movie line, and like most of the other movie toys, he has a more subdued, realistic color scheme than most Transformers released in the past few years. With the exception of a few spots of yellow, there are absolutely no bright colors on Hardtop whatsoever-- it makes him look very military, which is of course appropriate for a Sector Seven vehicle (he has S7 logos on both sides of his vehicle mode, as well as one on his Cyber Key). And, although none of the colors really contrast with each other because they're all rather dull colors, there's no large spot of only one color, so the color variations keep Hardtop from looking too boring.
With the exception of a different pack-in Cyber Key, no mold changes have been made to Hardtop.
Hardtop is an excellent military-themed repaint, with a very realistic color combo that fits in with the movie universe well, even if the mold itself doesn't. Some may be put off by the fact that there's very little color contrast on him, but Hasbro certainly accomplished what they wanted to accomplish with this scheme, that's for sure. My personal favorite of the three paint jobs this toy has received so far.


Hardtop Bio :
As an elite tracker and spy for the Decepticons, Hardtop has come into conflict again and again with Autobot spy Bumblebee. What began as a professional rivalry defined by the limits of their allegiances on opposite sides of a war became a full-fledged vendetta over the years. The two robots have played a punishing game of cat and mouse across the breadth of the galaxy; it is a little known fact that it was a shot fired by Hardtop that took away his Autobot enemy's ability to speak without pain.
