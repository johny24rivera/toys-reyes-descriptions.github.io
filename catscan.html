CatSCAN (Botcon 2002 Exclusive)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : White, milky red, transparent dark red, glossy black, and some chalky dark gray, silver, metallic sky blue, glow-in-the-dark paint, and chrome gold
Rating : 8.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Night Slash Cheetor. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Night Slash Cheetor here .)

CatSCAN is a very interesting repaint, to say the least. It's an Autobot... but it's an mecha-cat. So would it be a Maximal, like the person who supplied his essential programming ( Apelinq )? I guess not... Anyways, the white-black-and-red color scheme, with its silver and blue accents, is a pretty neat color scheme, but I don't know if it's really... fitting... for Night Slash Cheetor's mold. It's hard to put my finger on exactly, it just... doesn't fit. Night Slash Cheetor's mold needs a darker color scheme than this. CatSCAN has a few nice quirks, though, that Night Slash Cheetor doesn't. For one, a very slight mold change was made- CatSCAN has an Autobot symbol emblazoned on his chest, a nice touch. Secondly, CatSCAN is the only Botcon 2002 Exclusive to have brand-new paint apps. The chalky dark gray is one of them- there's a nice fade on the jetpack, but the presence of it on the face only looks like CatSCAN managed to stick his face in a chimney or something. The other new paint app is the taking away of Night Slash Cheetor's "spots" and the inclusion of nice "glow-in-the-dark" detailing on some of his shoulder, hip, and ankle panels. It's VERY nicely done, and almost looks like some kind of cyber-virus has infected CatSCAN at those points. It looks especially cool in the dark. (For some reason, the detailing on mine doesn't glow in the dark much at all- but I've been told that this is not a common problem.) The "swirly" gimmick that took place in Night Slash Cheetor's chest when you activated his sword-slashing gimmick isn't prevalent on CatSCAN. Mainly because his transparent plastic is a darker plastic, and the transmetal gold plastic used for the swirlygig under it has white stripes that don't stand out and reflect that well. Meh. Also, the Maximal spark crystal has been replaced with a solid ball- now the allegiance symbol is on the chest, not in the middle of the back.
Other than the new Autobot symbol, CatSCAN has no mold changes, so he retains all the flaws and features of his predecessor in that area.
CatSCAN has some pretty neat gimmicks, and a very nice robot mode, like his predecessor. The glow-in-the-dark detailing is also a very original and neat feature, but the overall color scheme (which is definitely important) doesn't measure up to Night Slash Cheetor's. As such, I'd recommend the original (and cheaper) version of this mold. If you're a sucker for exclusives, though, he's alright.


CatSCAN Bio:
FUNCTION: Medic
CatSCAN is in many ways like a lost child in the wilderness -- he knows to some degree where he came from, but is unsure as to where he will eventually end up. Brought to life by a bizarre combination of Apelinq and his digitization powers and the artificial intelligence of a medical diagnostic computer, this curious creature has found a permanent home with the Wreckers team. Armed with two battle blades of ancient Cybertronian design and a tail-mounted plasma torch, CatSCAN could easily be considered a lethal one-robot army. This fact notwithstanding, his fearsome appearance belies an inherently gentle and nonviolent personality -- it is simply not in his programming to cause harm to other beings. Lacking a Spark of his own, CatSCAN has a deep wish to understand the true nature of his self-awareness. He looks up to Apelinq as a mentor and "creator figure" and often acts as a voice of conscience among his teammates.
