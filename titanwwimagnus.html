Ultra Magnus [War Within] (6" Titanium)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : White, blue, and some black, dull yellow, silver, metallic light blue, and red
Rating : 8.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Titanium War Within Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Titanium War Within Optimus Prime here .)

As is quickly becoming a trend (albeit one I don't particularly mind), War Within Optimus Prime has been repainted into Ultra Magnus. Granted, War Within Ultra Magnus in the comics looked much more like the traditional G1 Magnus and not a white Prime repaint, but eh, it still looks nice. The white certainly differentiates him from Prime's darker colors, and the blue looks superb next to the white. Unlike on many other versions of Ultra Magnus, the blue isn't a particularly light color, so it provides some great contrast-- there's also more of it in comparison to the white than on other white Prime-Magnuses as well, which I think looks better. The bits of red and yellow used mostly on parts of the robot mode also provide some great contrast against the blue and are just good secondary colors all-around. The silver doesn't provide very good contrast agains the white, as both are light colors (which is why I would've preferred a blue faceplate, not a silver one), but it does go well against the blue.
No mold changes have been made to War Within Ultra Magnus, so unfortunately, his joints are just as loose as War Within Optimus Prime's, maybe even a little moreso. That's Magnus' biggest flaw, right there.
War Within Ultra Magnus is an excellent repaint with some very nice color choices, especially for the accent colors. He's not QUITE as good as War Within Prime in terms of color schemes, but he's pretty darned close-- not to mention he doesn't have the paint chipping problem on the legs like Prime does. His floppy joints are his only particularly annoying flaw.

The hope of a rally was just over the horizon when Megatron pulled the Autobot leader's spark core from his ruined body. To hear that Optronix had been passed the mantle of leadership - made Optimus Prime - was simply another, greater shock. The archivist?
Ultra Magnus is on the other side of Cybertron, literally a world away from his new leader. As the Decepticons continue slaughtering their way around the globe, he and his men wait for word from Optimus Prime, hoping that the new leader proves equal to the challenge of the diabolical will to conquest of Megatron.
