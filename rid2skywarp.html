Skywarp (RID2015)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Warrior
Difficulty of Transformation : Hard
Color Scheme : Black, metallic purple, and some semi-metallic milky purple, dull purple, light milky gray, moderately dark red, silver, and light pale yellow
Rating : 9.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the RID2015 warrior version of Starscream. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of RID2015 warrior Starscream here .)

Welp, I'm pretty sure you guys are familiar with Skywarp. Redeco of Starscream in black, purple, and silver? Yep, they did it again for RID2015, and by and large they haven't really changed much from the usual Skywarp redeco. He's mostly black, with a dark purple plastic used on the feet, lower arms, and guns. The purple plastic is a rather nice, semi-metallic shade with a good amount of glitter mixed in, so it looks rather eye-catching. Surprisingly, most of the purple on Skywarp is actually paint-- a really nice metallic shade is used on the main wings and shoulders. Surprisingly, a different, duller shade of purple is used on the kneecaps, tailfins, and a stripe down the center of his forehead. Not really sure why they didn't just use the metallic shade instead there-- it's a great shade. Anyways, although Skywarp has SOME silver on small stripes on his wings and on his face, his pecs and chest are actually a blah light milky gray plastic shade, which doesn't look NEARLY as good. Still, at least it's not used much, but man, he really could've used some more of that nice silver coloration to help spice up all that black a bit more. There's some pale light yellow used for his cockpit window in vehicle mode and the window on his chest, and given how light it is, it contrasts exceedingly well with the black. Finaly, there's just a titch of some fairly dark red paint, used for Skywarp's eyes.
No mold changes have been made for RID2015 Skywarp.
RID2015 Skywarp is a largely by-the-numbers redeco of a Starscream toy, with the only surprises being some slight negatives, such as having a bit too much black plastic, and using milky gray plastic on his body instead of silver paint. He still looks better than Starscream color-wise, but I think Hasbro made a few minor bad calls that kept his color scheme from really shining.

Review by Beastbot
