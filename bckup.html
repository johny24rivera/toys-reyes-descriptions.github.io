Kup (Botcon 2009 Exclusive)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe (comes in a 5-pack with Flak , Landshark , Scourge , and Thunderclash )
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Sky blue, pale light sky blue, black, transparent dark blue, and some metallic silver, metallic bronze, and yellow
Rating : 7.9

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron deluxe Red Alert. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron deluxe Red Alert here .)

Kup is one of the oddest mold choices used in recent Botcon years for an homage. His alt mode isn't a truck. It's not even truck-ish, it's just a van with four doors. Granted, I'm sure Fun Publications had in mind molds before this one-- which apparently didn't pan out-- but still. Kup... isn't any sort of pickup. Yeeeah. Still the color scheme is quite good-- the duller sky blue is the absolute PERFECT shade of color to match his G1 toy, as is the lighter sky blue-- and despite them both being shades of blue, there's enough contrast between the two to really make the toy look good. (It should be noted, however, that the painted pale blue and plastic pale blue look virtually indistinguable from each other-- the obvious difference between the two above is only from the camera picture lighting.) Black gives some nice dark contrast to the toy, though, and the dark transparent blue used for the windows looks very nice in this respect, too. My favorite paint apps on Kup have to be the nice orange stripes, which really just make the toy pop in vehicle mode. I love how the come down to triangles at the ends, too-- a very nice touch. The bits of copper and silver help to round off an all-around stellar color scheme. I don't like quite everything about Kup's colors, though-- the Elite Guard symbols on his doors are oddly partially transparent, and given that part of the orange stripe goes through them it kind ruins the look of those symbols. He also could use some taillight paint apps.
Kup has a new head mold-- pretty G1-accurate, but given that this is supposed to be a young version of Kup, he doesn't have any wrinkles and looks quite youthful. Unfortunately, it goes even further than that-- many of the details, particularly on the face, are a little "fat" and indistinct and make Kup look kinda baby-ish and chubby. Given that, due to the transformation, the top of Kup's head pokes out of the vehicle mode, it also REALLY sticks out. At least with Red Alert he had a smooth forehead and "siren"-ears to help it blend, but not with Kup. It really sticks out. The Cyber Key is also a standard Autobot Cyber Key, but with the Elite Guard "wings" added to the Autobot symbol, which is pretty nice.
Botcon 2009 Kup has a GREAT color scheme, great paint apps-- but unfortunately it's just a poor homage to Kup. The mold doesn't fit him at all in either mode, and his head goes beyond young to looking chubby and overly smooth. If you're gonna drop a mint on him, get him for the color scheme, not the homage.


Kup Bio :
FUNCTION: Interstellar Patrol
MOTTO: "Every experience is worth savoring."
The youngest Autobot assigned to the Eight Track, Kup hides his eagerness and inexperience behind adherence to the command structure and protocol. Kup seeks to emulate his Captain and mentor, Thunderclash, but also looks up to Landshark due to his unorthodox approach. He can't help but be in awe of both his comrades and all the new worlds he has visited serving aboard the Eight Track. A manufacturing glitch gave Kup's joints the ability to detach and re-attach without causing permanent damage to his superstructure. This gives him surprising resilience and makes him exceptionally easy to repair. This ability also lets him swap out his hand for a top-of-the-line laser musket and other useful tools. His lack of experience often leaves him vulnerable, but he is as brave and loyal as they come.
