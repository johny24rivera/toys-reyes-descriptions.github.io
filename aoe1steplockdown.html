Lockdown (1-Step Changer; AoE)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Very dark grayish brown, gray, silver, and some black, moderately dark glossy blue, light milky gray, and light lime green
Rating : 7.0

Lockdown's Lamborghini Aventador alt mode makes the transition to a one-step changer quite well, with his vehicle mode being about as reasonably close as you can expect a toy of this size to be to an officially licensed high-end car. Proportionally he's pretty much perfect, and his robot mode extras in this mode are very, very minor-- you can see just a titch of his robot mode details inside the (open) side windows, and on the back end you can see his front toes poking out the bottom, but that's it. The mold sculpting is pretty accurate-- as you'd expect for a dude with a licensed car mode-- with the unique teeth-like grill and vented back end looking pretty nice. The overall color scheme is accurate to the movie, but in general this is bad thing when it comes to Lockdown-- having almost all of the car mode be a rather dark grayish brown-- even if it's semi-metallic-- just is not an exciting, eye-catching color. Luckily the front windshield is painted silver, and the headlights and rear vents are painted a rather nice shade of blue, both of which contrast with the main color pretty well and the latter of which adds some much-needed color to this mode. However, the paint app placements are also a victim of Lockdown's 1-step transformation gimmick, as they make even more obvious the gaps in the windshield and between the two halves of the rear. In fact, this is my principle issue with this figure; in vehicle mode the parts often don't quite tab together firmly, with the parts wiggling about slightly here and there even during light play, transforming the figure a fraction without you actually pressing the button on the windshield, which is what actually triggers his transformation to robot mode. (To transform Lockdown back, you simply push his roof back together again.)
For robot mode, Lockdown looks quite simple, and he is-- his doors and front grill move downwards and out a bit, and his legs lengthen a bit while his roof moves back a little further behind him so it doesn't get in the way as much. As one-step transformations go, there's definitely more impressive ones out there. However, the positive for this is that Lockdown doesn't really suffer from proportional maladies in robot mode like some of the other toys in this subline do; his arms are a bit slim and merely molded into the sides of his doors, sure, and his vehicle kibble (his tires and shoulder-grill halves, most obviously) could be tucked away a bit better, but he generally looks like he does in the movie, with his face molded in the "mask down" position. The amount of mold detailing on Lockdown is incredible, and definitely the highlight of this mode; movie-accurate vents, pistons, and all sorts of robotic detailing are everywhere. For articulation, he can only move back-and-forth at the elbows, but articulation really isn't the point of these toys, so I'm willing to give him a partial pass on this due to how good the rest of this toy looks for a one-step changer. As far as colors go, the coating of his main body in silver, the addition of several blue paint apps on his body, and the lime-green on his mask really help to give this mode considerably more color and make it more eye-catching than his vehicle mode, with no large area looking dull.
AoE "One-Step" Lockdown may not have a very exciting transformation for a toy from the subline-- and I really wish his parts fit together more firmly in vehicle mode-- but, by and large, he's one of the best one-step changer designs in the Age of Extinction toyline. He's pretty proportional in both modes, well-detailed, and although he's barely poseable at all, the one-steps are for kids who are young enough to generally not care about that kind of stuff anyways. If you want a simple-changer of this design and don't want to spring for the more-expensive, harder-to-find Flip 'n Change version, this toy makes a nice substitute.

Review by Beastbot
