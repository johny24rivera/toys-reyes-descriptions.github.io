Optimus Prime (Battle Blades) [Autobot Alliance]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Hard
Color Scheme: Light milky gray, dark glittery blue, moderately light red, and some silver, black, transparent dark blue, metallic light gold, milky gray, and light milky tan
Rating : 9.7

This is Movie Optimus Prime's vehicle mode. He's had a lot of toys made of him and had a lot of high-budget screen time, so you've no doubt become fairly familiar with it. This version's vehicle mode doesn't have any particularly noteworthy deviations from Movie Prime's standard proportions or colors, so I don't really see much need to elaborate on most of the details here-- you've heard (well, seen) them all before. There are a few minor points I have to knock against this version's vehicle mode, though-- for one, the light milky gray is a particularly blah shade even for that color, and a few paint details like headlights or the flame details usually on top of the roof are missing on this version. You can also see a LITTLE bit of the robot legs under the rear section of the vehicle, and because of his transformation, the halves of the cab's "nose" do tend to noticeably seperate a little towards where they meet in front of the windows. The top of the smokestacks are also different pieces from the bottom and, due to the transformation, can swivel, so it can be a little difficult to get them looking "just right" and completely aligned. Still, all of these are pretty minor complaints, given how accurate this toy generally gets to the actual truck in terms of proportions, the color scheme (with the really nice gold among the front flame decals), and the like.
Battle Blades Optimus Prime's transformation is almost identical to RotF Leader Class Optimus Prime's , with only a surprisingly few simplifications made for the smaller size. As such, he shares many of the same positive and negatives of that mold-- which is to say, it's almost all positives in this mode. The vehicle mode kibble that doesn't integrate in some essential way to the robot mode design is almost non-existent-- simply consisting of the folded-up tire covers behind his lower back. Pretty much everything else is 98% movie-accurate. Being able to pull this kind of transformation off at a Leader-class scale was an engineering feat; being able to pull it off at a class that's half the size and price is simply jaw-dropping. Unfortunately, like with the Leader-class toy, it comes with a price; Battle Blades Prime is pretty darned hard to transform, particularly back into vehicle mode. This is not one for the younger kiddies, folks, despite what it says on the box. That aside though, because there's no electronics, this version of Optimus Prime also has a few tricks that the Leader class one doesn't (and visa versa, obviously, but we'll focus on the positives here). For one, part of the lower middle section of his truck mode can come off and flip out to become hand-held weapons, just like they were used in the movie (though neither of them fire any actual missiles on this toy). He also has a Matrix of Leadership with incredibly finely-sculpted detail inside his chest. Just like with the RotF Leader-class toy, he also has flip-out swords in this mode, though you have to manually flip them out yourself. They're also made of that rubbery type of plastic for safety purposes, what with the smaller scale and whatnot. Honestly though, they can get in the way a bit, particularly during transformation. I would've slightly preferred if they had forgone that little feature. As for articulation, Prime's got it in spades; he can move at the neck, shoulders (at four points), elbows (at two points), hips (at three points), knees (at two points), and ankles (at two points), so he can get into a TON of awesome poses, and he's quite stable, too.
Battle Blades Optimus Prime is an incredibly-engineered design, particularly at this scale-- he has great proportions in both modes, great screen accuracy, great mold detailing, great articulation, great features (for the most part)-- just about great everything, really. He's quite difficult to transform, so I wouldn't recommend this toy to younger or less patient Transfans, but if you like complex transformations and don't want a super-big or expensive Movie Prime toy, this one's your guy.


Optimus Prime (Battle Blades) Bio :
Optimus Prime isn't famous for being a particularly skilled fighter-- he relies mostly on raw power to secure victory. Unfortunately for Decepticon Banzaitron, all that raw power actually masks decades of training. Optimus Prime is one of the most accomplished fighters ever produced by Cybertron. He may not be as flashy as the Decepticon martial arts master, but he's got more than enough skill to crush Decepticon Banzaitron.
