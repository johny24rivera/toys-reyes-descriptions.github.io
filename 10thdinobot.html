Dinobot (Beast Wars 10th Anniversary Edition)

Allegiance : Maximal
Size : Deluxe ($15 U.S.-priced, however)
Comes with : Transmutate's left arm; DVD containing the Beast Wars episode "Code of Hero"
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dark brown, moderately light bronzish brown, flat sky blue, and some light sky blue, silver, flat red, and metallic jungle green
Rating : 7.5

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Beast Wars Dinobot. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Beast Wars Dinobot here .)

10th Anniversary Dinobot is definitely the 'bot who has the most color scheme changes when compared with the original version's paint job-- literally none of the colors on this toy are the same as on the original version. And overall, this version of Dinobot looks more show-accurate than the old version-- the light bronzish brown color used for the dino flesh is dead-on accurate with the TV show, the face is painted correctly, and the dark brown used on the stripes is also accurate to the show. However, this is a toy that definitely fits into the category of "so close, yet so far". For some reason I can't fathom, Dinobot's legs have different paint apps this time, which, at least in their placement, is very show-accurate-- however, the "bone" paint apps are GREEN, not bone white like they should be. The blue coloration on the inside of his tail-spinner is also not show-accurate AND doesn't look that good to boot. Plus, his sword (which, surprisingly, isn't short 'n stubby like the last time this mold was used for Transformers Dinobot Grimlock ), should be silver, as well as some other parts of his legs, and his upper arms should be the same color as his lower arms. His beast mode eyes are also blue, when they should be white. Dinobot DOES sport a nifty new Maximal allegiance symbol paint app on the left side of his chest, though.
No mold changes have been made to 10th Anniversary Dinobot.
10th Anniversary Dinobot is really a missed opportunity-- some colors are spot-on show-accurate, while a few others are completely wrong, and you have to wonder what the designers were thinking when they made the 10th Anniverary toy look this way when the entire POINT of this line was to make the old toys more show-accurate! Taken by itself, the color scheme isn't bad, but it really aggravates me that such an opportunity was passed up. Still, I think the toy does look better than the original Beast Wars Dinobot.


Dinobot Bio :
A warrior to his core, Dinobot lived only for honor won in combat. Disgusted by the greed and dishonesty of Megatron, and with a life debt owed to the Maximal leader, he joined Optimus Primal. Though Dinobot was never fully trusted by some Maximals, his strong honor code and philosophical nature led him always to the path of victory. He was one of many to fall in the Beast Wars, sacrificing his life in a heroic battle against overwhelming Predacon attack.
