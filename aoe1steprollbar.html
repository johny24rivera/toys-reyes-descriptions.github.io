Rollbar (1-Step Changer) [AoE}

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : White, moderately dark purple, and some black, dark metallic grayish blue, silver, dull yellow, and dull gold
Rating : 7.0

Rollbar is the sportscar that Shane drove briefly in the movie-- apparently for rally racing, and it was really decked-out to the point where I was surprised it WASN'T a Transformer in the movie. Well, Hasbro has rectified that oversight in the movie by making a toy that transforms into said car! As you'd expect from a licensed Chevrolet alt-mode, the proportions here are pretty good, with only a few minor compromises made because of the transformation-- most notably at the front end, where the windshield doesn't slope up as quickly as it should and is a little narrow. There's also the unsightly hinge lines along the front hood that are a byproduct of the transformation. Other than that, and obviously not having seats inside when you look inside his open side windows, Rollbar is extra-free in this mode. The mold detailing is fairly sparse on most of the car-- as is accurate to the speedy rally car used in the movie-- but there are parts that are appropriately detailed fairly intricately, such as the back end (what with the license plate, taillights, front bumper, exhaust pipes and all), the front end, and the tires, especially. The general color scheme of white and purple looks really good and is a bit abnormal for an Autobot, but it still looks good, and the bit of dark metallic grayish blue used for the windows also contrasts excellently with the white. The way the purple swoops down from the spoiler to the side and then "fades out" in a checkered pattern is quite unique, and makes Rollbar stand out on a movie shelf. (That said, the bit of purple plastic in the middle of the front grill looks kinda weird.) There's a bit of silver on the headlights and gold/yellow used on the Chevrolet symbols on the front grill and the back end, but there still definitely needed to be more paint on the front and back end. The grill on the the front is unpainted, and with the exception of the Chevy symbol, the back end is COMPLETELY unpainted, which is a crying shame given all the mold detailing back there just begging for some paint. There's also no fake sponsor logos either, but I consider that a minor complaint compared to the aforementioned oversights.
Rollbar uses the nifty "switchblade" style of one-step transformation first used in toys like Drift , where you pull out and rotate the sides of the mode down, and the rest of the toy automatically changes into robot mode. As such, he shares nearly all of the same pros and cons in his robot mode design that the other "switchblade one-step" Transformers have. On the plus sude, Rollbar looks pretty good from the front proportionally, but from a side view looks quite odd, with his legs far too "deep" and actually going almost all the way up to his head, concealed in the front by the chest flap of "fake" robot detailing. Said legs are also hollow from the side, and just plain look bad. Just... don't look at the toy from the side in this mode. That major downside out of the way, Rollbar has a pretty good look in this mode, with his chest having several nice asymmetrical mold details on it, like a gas cap on the right side of the chest and what look like angled fragments of his car mode on the left side, and with what looks like the hood "air vent" in the middle of his chest-- a nice look. His headsculpt is also really well-done-- again, slightly asymmetrical what with looks like a (literal) headlight on the right side of his forehead, but otherwise a silver faceplate for a mouth below deep-set eyes and a round purple helmet. The mold details on his arms and legs are sadly rather generic with only a few pistons 'n such visible, though the backside of this mode-- the underside in the car mode, so it's almost never "seen"-- has some angular robotic detailing, as well. Rollbar's also armed with daggers that apparently come right out of his knuckles, Wolverine-style, on each hand. The white and purple are broken up pretty well in this mode, with the purple being a bit more evident here, particularly with the purple paint on the chest, hips, and head. The silver in the middle of the chest and head also helps to break up the white and add a bit more variety to the color scheme, and the black "V" shape behind the head is a nice little accent as well-- no complaints here. Unfortunately, like all 1-step changers with this transformation scheme, Rollbar can only move at the shoulders at two points and outwards sideways slightly at the legs (the latter due to his transformation)-- otherwise he's a brick.
Rollbar is a pretty good-looking one-step changer, all things considered, though he suffers from the same side-view issues in robot mode that all AoE TFs with the "switchblade" transformation scheme have. Mildly recommended if you want a toy of the character that isn't "just" a redeco of someone else but a brand new mold, of if you don't want to purchase an expensive giftset to obtain some version of him-- he's one of the more unexpected additions to the AoE line.

Review by Beastbot
