Landquake (2006 Collectors' Club Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Basic
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Transparent cherry red, dull periwinkle, dull light milky gray, glossy black, and some white and metallic gunmetal gray
Rating : 8.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Energon Kickback. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Energon Kickback here .)

Landquake is the second part of the Club-exclusive combiner that will take until 2009 to complete, with one component being part of each years' membership. Like the previous combiner component Skyfall , Landquake is composed primarily of transparent plastic. He keeps the same general color scheme as Skyfall, but the amounts of the colors and their placement have been changed. Now, a transparent red is by far the primary color scheme, comprising the tank body, the Energon weapon and star, and the lower robot legs. In fact, I think there's a bit TOO much of the transparent red-- another transparent color should have been used to give the figure a bit less uniformity in vehicle mode. The dull bluish periwinkle, black, and dull gray make excellent contrasting colors to the red, and contrast amongst themselves pretty well too. They're very Autobot-ish colors, though, so it's pretty weird that they're on a Decepticon. (And speaking of which, considering Skyfall is an Autobot-- it looks like this'll be the first multi-faction combiner, folks!) Landquake certainly has more paint apps than Skyfall did (who barely had any at all), but he still has less paint apps than the mold's previous two uses as Kickback and Blight . All of the paint apps do look very nice and contrast well on the colors they're next too, however, and there is one new paint app that was on neither of the mold's previous versions-- Landquake's forehead vent is painted black. It's a pretty minor paint app, to be sure, but still worth noting.
No mold changes have been made to Landquake-- however, many of Landquake's joints are a less tight than on previous versions of the mold-- the knee joints, especially.
Landquake has a nice, if not very Decepticon-y, paint job that looks pretty cool if you have a strong light shining from behind him. However, his color scheme doesn't match up to the awesomeness of Blight or Kickback, and he doesn't have as many paint apps as them either. If you live in the U.S. or attend Botcon regularly, than the Collectors' Club subscription is worth the money and thus you'll get Landquake regardless, but if you aren't a member of the club I can't recommend going out of your way to hunt Landquake down.


Landquake Bio :
FUNCTION: Warrior/Energy Specialist
MOTTO: "Respect the power you wield and it will serve you well."
SPECIALTY: Energon refinement; Energon Plasma physics; Energon Structure Dynamics
WEAPONS: Smoke dispensers, twin heavy-repeater auto shotguns, indepentently targeted EM pulse cannons, jump jets, Energon Plasma Weapon
UNIQUE QUALITY: Can refine and shape raw Energon in the same manner as an Omnicon
Landquake has served Megatron since the very beginning of the Cybertronian Civil War. As an Energon flow analyst at the Kalis refineries, he had become obsessed with the idea that his destiny held a greater purpose. With the first attacks on Mini-Con settlements, he threw in his lot with Megatron, seeing in the Decepticon cause a means to achieve this greater destiny. Centuries of association with the Decepticons have proven to him that the movement he once believed in is little more than a vehicle for its leader's ego. He undertakes the missions he is assigned with less than total dedication. Preoccupied as he is with self-doubt, and desiring the rigid structure of a real army, he often finds his duties for Megatron almost as boring as his old refinery duties. Landquake is a terrifying opponent in battle. His EM pulse cannons can scramble the circuits of even hardened targets, and the blast from hi Energon plasma weapon is formidable enough to destroy many small to medium-sized targets at close range. Rocket-assisted leaps and glides can offer a devastating surprise to an unwary enemy. His long exposure to raw Energon has given him Energon refinement and shaping capabilities superior to even the Omnicons. With a sufficient supply of raw Energon, he can create an array of formidable weapons.
