Bumblebee (Animated; McDonald's)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Happy Meal Size
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Black, yellow, and some sky blue and light gray
Rating : 3.2

Bumblebee's vehicle mode is fairly good for a Happy Meal toy, with the proportions being pretty darn good overall. Heck, in some respects he actually looks LESS chubby/kiddy than the mainline toy, such as in the slightly lengthier proportions here. All of the required mold detailing is here, though the paint detailing is merely adequate-- at least the windows and Bumblebee's characteristic stripe is painted (though all black-- the stripe almost blends win with the window), but the headlights and siren, both rather major details on Animated Bumblebee, are left unpainted here. So he's pretty much just all black and yellow with no real accents in this mode, which makes him look pretty bland. It's also worth nothing that Bumblebee's wheels are merely molded in-- there's tinier wheels under them that actually move the toy along. Bumblebee has one extra in this mode, and that's that his robot head prety blatantly sticks out the back end, just staring out into space. I wish his neck could have at least turned around so you've have the back of his head facing outwards. On a more minor note, there's a gap in the front bumper due to his transformation.
Bumblebee's robot mode certainly isn't as good as his vehicle mode, though compared to some other McDonald's Animated Happy Meal toys it's not the worst. His head is teeny tiny proprtionally, as are his arms, which are merely molded on the side of his car doors. Granted, this is how it is on the show model as well, but the arms are really 2-D here and aren't as obviously arms, looking more like just long car panels instead. His chest is a bit boxy, but that's a minor complaint at this scale-- I really don't like how he only has one uni-leg, though, as it would've been pretty easy to seperate them-- practically just a slide down the middle of the piece, and you would've gotten individual knee articluation due to the transformation. Instead, you can't rotate his legs back at the knee without making it impossible for him to stand up. The windshield-feet look good, though. As for the rest of his articulation, he can move at the shoulders, the waist, and his head can look up a bit due to the transformation.
Happy Meal Animated Bumblebee is one of the better toys from this promotion, though honestly that's not saying much. His robot mode, though it has some positives like the chest and general leg shape, is still pretty awkward with a tiny head and arms. His vehicle mode is pretty decent given that he's a Happy Meal toy, though.
