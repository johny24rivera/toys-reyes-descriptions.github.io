Flak (Botcon 2009 Exclusive)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Voyager (comes in a 5-pack with Kup , Landshark , Scourge , and Thunderclash )
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : White, gray, black, and some red, metallic gunmetal gray, light green, transparent pale light green, and metallic copper
Rating : 9.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron Defense Scattorshot. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron Defense Scattorshot here .)

Flak here has a bit of an interesting history. He was originally going to be released in colors that were similar to Universe 2.0 Overload , who himself was a redeco homage to G1 Flak. However, at the time he was conceived, Fun Publications didn't know about the Overload release, so when they did, they change Flak's deco to a more "arctic/urban camo" color scheme that really isn't an homage to Flak at all. So Flak here is a homage to the G1 character in everything BUT the color scheme, while Universe 2.0 Overload is an homage to Flak only IN the color scheme. Mmmkay... regardless, I've always liked these arctic/urban camo paint jobs, and Flak is certainly no exception. The white-and-gray stripes color scheme looks utterly fantastic, and the dude's COVERED in paint apps-- no boring big patches of plastic here. Black becomes more apparent in robot mode due to his treads, arms, and head, and serves as a nice dark contrast color, but what REALLY makes this figure pop are the small uses of a few minor colors. The red looks quite nice as a "connector piece" color, especially against the black and gray. The copper tips to the missiles look REALLY nice, and the transparent green plastic used for the targeting reticle in robot mode is an interesting choice, but surprisingly it looks really good. Honestly, I don't have any problems with this dude's color scheme at all.
Flak has a new head mold, based on his G1 version's. It's very well-sculpted, and the green visor looks quite nice against the other colors, similar to how the transparent green looks. His Cyber Key is the same mold that comes with Cybertron Defense Scattorshot, though it has Elite Guard wings painted around the Autobot symbol in a rather cool effect.
Botcon 2009 Flak is a fantastic mold with an utterly fantastic paint job, even if the homage may be a little screwed up. He's got paint apps everywhere and all these colors mixing together is fairly original and just really catches the eye. Definitely my favorite of the color schemes this mold has received-- my favorite exclusive from the "core" Botcon 2009 boxset, and my 2nd-favorite exclusive from that year overall. Even if you already have the mold, if you have the money to be hunting down Botcon exclusives, I'd recommend considering this guy.


Flak Bio :
FUNCTION: Shock Trooper
MOTTO: "Always be prepared. If you can be lucky, do that, too."
An experienced "alien fighter" who forged his career in the Cybertronian Revolution, Flak is a fearsome opponent. Space pirates, mutants, transorganics... Flak has fought them all, first as a security officer, then as an explorer, and finally as a shock trooper under Thunderclash's command. He does not yearn for battle, but it is his only marketable skill. Off the battlefield, combat doesn't interest him; it is his job, he does it when needed and he does it well. Each of his weapons carries a unique feature: incendiary missile launchers conceal an acid burst cannon and an electron blaster. He carries a negative energy freeze rifle and has concussive force blasters concealed in his optics. An expert tactician and long-range combatant, he lacks finesse as a melee brawler. Flak sometimes has difficulty adjusting his tactics for use against Cybertronian foes.
