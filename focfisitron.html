Fisitron (FoC) [TFCC 2014 Subscription Service Exclusive]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Creamy pale tan, dark periwinkle, and some silver, light milky brownish gray, light orange, pink, light metallic silvery blue, and seafoam green
Rating : 7.5

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Generations FoC Swindle. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Generations FoC Swindle here .)

"Fisitron" is another version of Ironfist. Admittedly FunPub has already done this character as a BotCon 2011 exclusive , but that version was in the Animated style and had more saturated colors-- this version is closer to the "traditional" Transformer style and color-wise is MUCH closer to his original toy. (Plus, the original Ironfist's alternate mode was a jeep, so using FoC Swindle's mold makes sense if you're going for a Cybertronian version of him.) Fisitron's main two colors are a creamy tan and a rather dark shade of perwinkle (the latter much more apparent in robot mode). These two colors contrast against each other EXCEEDINGLY well, and both just look good even by themselves. It's actually a rather realistic color scheme for a character from the early '90s. Without the chest there's a bit too much unbroken periwinkle on the upper body in robot mode-- nearly everything from the knees up-- but, thankfully, there IS that big chest flap to help vary the colors-- though it may not help much from a back angle... Fisitron also has some light brownish gray plastic used for both his wheels and his combiner hands, and it's honestly an ugly shade of the color that looks a little "dirty" but not enough to actually look like dirt's on the wheels. Thankfully, it's not used much. There's some silver on the engine pistons on the front, the tailpipes, and the wheel hubs, though the silver is so close to the brownish gray it doesn't stick out enough, especially for a FoC toy, if you ask me. The addition of some light orange on the kneecaps and some seafoam green on the lower legs both contrast quite well with the tan and hearken back to his original toy as well, and help to vary up Fisitron's colors more. There is just a LITTLE bit of that early '90s color madness in Fisitron's color scheme, with his headlights being pink (again accurate to the original toy).
Fisitron has the FoC Roadbuster head, but not the FoC Roadbuster weapon, unfortunately. Thankfully, Fisitron's head is painted quite nicely, with a silver faceplate and metallic silvery blue eyes, though the mouth "gas mask" area does look a bit too bulky/"mean" compared to Fisitron's original toy, which had a more straightforward faceplate.
FoC Fisitron is a nice "normal" update of the character, and mildly recommended if you liked the character in the IDW comics. His colors are top-notch (light brownish gray excepted), and contrast and complement each other very nicely. That said, the mold is rather medicore, which does dampen my enthusiasm a bit-- but hey, if you like the character or want another character compatible with the mass-retail FoC Wreckers, here you go.


Fisitron Bio :
FUNCTION: Autobot Weapons Specialist
MOTTO: "Everyone has a talent. The trick is knowing yourself well enough to find it."
Ironfist was an assembly line worker in the factories of Polyhex, known only for a modest and agreeable personality that brought people together around him. None would have suspected that his profession was a front for his true passion as an underground weapon designer, for which he was known across the infosphere by the alias "Fisitron". When Ultra Magnus appeared at the factory one day and asked for him by name, Ironfist immediately thought that his illegal hobby had gotten him arrested, but the truth was even stranger: Unknown to him, a routine physical examination had shown the origins of his mechanical genius to be a "combination spark"-- a rare genetic mutation giving him the potential to innately bond and interface with other machines. Welcomed by Magnus as a new member of the Wreckers, the days of "Fisitron" having to conceal his true nature are long past, as he proudly discovers new ways to lend his ever-growing abilities to the Autobot war effort.
