Color Scheme : Red, dark blue, black, and some silver, pale metallic gold, and light milky gray
Rating : 8.2

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Titanium War Within Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Titanium War Within Optimus Prime here .)

Optimus Prime gets repainted in Optimus Prime colors as... Optimus Prime! Wow, what an amazing redeco! All kidding aside, although he still shares the basic color scheme as this mold's original deco, the colors are mixed around and used in amounts more akin to Movie Prime's color scheme (sans the flames). That is, there's considerably more blue and a bit less red, with some more gray creeping in. The paint scheme does an exceptional job of mixing all this up so that no part of this version of Prime's deco job gets too boring, however. Using some nice silver, black, and dull gold paint apps here and there-- particularly on the waist, the shoulders, and the front of the lower legs-- really helps to achieve this. Heck, with the silver and gold Optimus Prime looks almost royal in places, given the inherent glossy-ness of paint over Prime's many diecast metal parts. The actual plastic gray-- mostly used for connector parts-- is pretty dull, but given hwo little it's used overall this is a fairly minor quibble. Especially when you consider that, unlike the original, this guy's paint isn't sloppy and doesn't chip!
No mold changes have been made to this version of Prime, so unfortunately he still suffers from the loose, dangly joints that plague this mold. Also, he doesn't come with a stand like most other 6" Titaniums.
War Within "Movie Colors" Optimus Prime has a great deco job-- the best that this mold's gotten, in my opinion, with the accent colors of gold, black, and silver really helping to make Prime's base colors stand out. A shame about the inherent looseness of the mold's joints, and when you consider the lack of a stand and the $5 U.S. price jump, it makes me a bit more hesitant to recommend him even though he has the superior color scheme. Still, if you don't mind the slightly increased price and really want this mold, this guy's your one to look out for.
