Optimus Prime (Power Armor)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately light gray, very dark navy blue, dull pale red, black, and some silver
Rating : 7.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original RotF Legends class Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original RotF Legends class Optimus Prime here .)

There's not much "Power Armor-y" about Power Armor Optimus Prime. He's basically in the same colors as his original Legends class toy-- with nearly the same paint apps-- but everything's been dulled down and darkened a bit. The red is a lot less eye-catching and just looks really blah, almost rusty. The gray plastic has been also dulled down a tad, but I think the biggest negative about Power Armor Prime's color scheme is that his blue has been darkened and dulled to such an extent it looks almost black-- which of course, doesn't make it contrast well with the ACTUAL black plastic. The flame paint apps on the front of the vehicle mode have also been darkened to a similar color. The silver paint used on places like his windows, face, and shoulders is still a nice color, but it's too little to make this guy stand out from other versions of Prime.
No mold changes have been made to Power Armor Optimus Prime.
Power Armor Optimus Prime takes Optimus Prime's traditional color scheme and makes everything more blah, like he's being viewed under a dim light or something. And more dull colors is not something that the movie aesthetic needs. Given the multiple other releases of this mold, all with better color schemes, there's really no reason to get this version of him unless you're a big Prime completist.
