Darksteel (Prime) [Target Exclusive]

Allegiance : Predacon
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme: Dark dull navy blue, moderately light gray, moderately dark gray, and some black, dark perwinkle and bright orange
Rating : 8.5

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Prime Grimwing. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Prime Grimwing here .)

This is one of the very few times in TF history where the original release of a mold-- Grimwing-- never appeared in official fiction, but the redeco is actually in the show, having a role in the "Predacons Rising" mini-movie that is the epilogue to the Prime series. Unfortunately, his model on the show only shares the "big strokes" in terms of the design, with the toy being much bulkier and more round than the show model-- though the show model was created later, so I can't really fault the toy in this instance. As far as the new color scheme goes, Darksteel lives up to how name by having a lot of both light and dark gray paint and plastic on him, to give him a more colder, "steelier" look. Thankfully, neither of the shades of gray are of the particularly milky, blah shade-- both are fairly flat in their appearance. This is alright, but adding a bit more of a metallic shade to the gray really would've helped more here. Thankfully, the gray is broken up by a lot of dark flat navy blue, which contrasts with all the other colors excellently, and also helps to tie in Darksteel thematically with is Predacon "brother", Sky Lynx. Oddly, just the tail is a slightly different shade of dark blue, being more of a dark perwinkle compared to navy blue. There's also a bit of black on the figure, such as on the beast heads and sides of the chest, but it blends in so much with the dark blue that it's not really noticeable. No, what really stands out on this figure are the excellent bright orange accents, which both highlight many of the "Cyberglyphs" on his wings and arms and also helps his heads (in both modes) and his weapon-head stand out quite a bit. The color just goes especially well with the dark blue, and upgrades what would be a fairly dull color scheme to one with a bit more of a punch to it.
No mold changes have been made to Darksteel.
Darksteel is generally a pretty decent redeco of the Grimwing mold in colors that are definitely more pleasing overall. I wish Darksteel had a bit more of that bright orange for accents, and that his grays were a bit more metallic for shade, but overall he's pretty nice-looking and a solid addition to your Prime Predacon collection. (That said, he's not all that accurate to his show model in terms of the details, but unfortunately as far as Prime Darksteel toys go this is all you've got.)
