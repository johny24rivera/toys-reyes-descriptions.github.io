Chopsaw

Allegiance: Decepticon
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately dark red, light pale milky greenish tan, and some black, metallic copper, light pale blue, metallic silver-gold, and dull yellow
Rating : 9.1

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Autobot Alliance Brimstone. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Autobot Alliance Brimstone here .)

Chopsaw seems to be a much more fitting name for this mold, even though Brimstone itself was a cool name. Regardless, this redeco takes the roughly opposite approach of Brimstone, with the main color being a dull red instead of blue. The neutral color-- instead of a blah milky gray-- is now a sickly greenish tan color. Honestly, I've never been impressed with the light milky gray plastic on a lot of Transformers, but I still prefer it over this weird shade of green-tan. It doesn't look good at all, and it just makes it look like the figure's eternally under bad lighting (or has the flu). Instead of Brimstone's flame paint apps, Chopsaw has more straightforward yellow racing stripes across several of his red parts. These contrast pretty nicely with the red, though in terms of catching the eye I think Brimstone's flames work a little better. (That said, the extra little yellow stripes on Chopsaw's head do look really nice here.) A few other colors like metallic copper and silver-gold are also here and there as accents and help to add a bit more to the color scheme, and look cool enough. Since Chopsaw is in the "Reveal the Shield" subline, he has an allegiance rub sticker on the left side of the top of his motorcycle mode-- rub it to reveal he's a Decepticon.
No mold changes have been made to Chopsaw.
Chopsaw is a decent enough redeco-- not outright bad by any means, though I wish that sickly greenish tan was a more interesting color-- or heck, even a boring color like light gray, as opposed to the slightly ugly shade it is. The red and black plastic and paint apps all look nice, but overall I think the original release of this mold as Brimstone looks a bit better and I'd recommend that more (not to mention Brimstone's a lot easier to find).


Chopsaw Bio :
Most Decepticons are motivated by power or speed or ambition, but Chopsaw just likes to cut stuff in half with his saws. That's really all the reason he needs to follow Megatron. He'll follow any leader who indulges his maniacal tendency towards destruction. Everywhere Chopsaw goes, he leaves behind a smoking trail of bisected rubble.
