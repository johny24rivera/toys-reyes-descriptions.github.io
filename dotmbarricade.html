Barricade (Deluxe; DotM)

Allegiance: Decepticon
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Black, white, gray, and some silver, light red, dark purple, clear plastic, transparent red, transparent dark blue, light metallic pale purple, and pale orange
Rating : 9.1

Although he has a new-mold toy for Dark of the Moon this time around, Barricade's vehicle mode is still the same-- namely, a Mustang police car. He looks as cool and proportionally accurate in this mode as he ever did, with the white bits on the doors and the rear spoiler helping to add some contrast against all the black plastic, while other little details-- such as the orange headlights, light red taillights, purple Decepticon "badge" in front of the car doors and transparent red-and-blue siren-- provide a few more bits of contrast elsewhere. (And, of course, as is basically required of a Barricade toy that isn't teeny tiny, he has his "To Punish and Enslave" motto near the back end on each side as well.) That said, the windows bug me a little. The front and car door windows are clear plastic-- which would be fine if you couldn't see any parts inside besides a molded cockpit, but there's just robot parts inside there, and the only extras visible in this mode to boot. It looks a bit out of place, particularly when compared to the rear windows, which are unpainted black plastic-- they definitely clash. As for Barricade's Mechtech weapon, it's a rather thick (non-firing) machine gun that, when you push back on the gray tab on the side, transforms into an almost ludicrously large claw weapon, which actually suits Barricade just fine. I wish it could stay in claw mode, but unfortunately due to the spring-loaded Mechtech gimmick that's impossible. Barricade's only Mechtech port is in the middle of his siren in this mode.
In robot mode, Barricade's almost an entirely different story depending upon whether you're talking about him from the waist up or the waist down. From the waist down, he's passable, but a little weak in places-- his legs proportionally look great, but the back ends of the police car mode hang off the upper legs rather unceremoniously and can get in the way of articulation a bit. His hips also come out from the sides of his waist too much to the point where they look a bit awkward, and the waist joint itself-- while I'm grateful that Barricade has one-- is rather fat, so if you're looking at him from head-on or a view that you can see underneath his chest, it looks a bit odd as well. However, from the waist up Barricade is practically perfect, particularly for the size-- his headsculpt and paint apps are spot-on; his arms are skinny but not quite as 2-D as on his initial movie toy and thus more movie-accurate; the claw-hands are accurate and not overly large; the car door panels slide outwards a bit so that Barricade's arms don't appear to "break in half" when he bends his elbows; and there's even a panel in the middle of his chest that flips around to give him the more movie-accurate "crushed fender" look in this mode. The mold detailing on his robot parts is also pretty spectacular overall, particularly on his hands and arms, where all sorts of little hinges and the like can be seen. When it comes to articulation, Barricade's pretty good in that area, too-- he can move at the neck, shoulders (at three points), elbows (at two points), at the wrist, at the base of each hand (with all four fingers on one joint), and at the waist, hips (at three points), knees (at two points), and ankles. Thus, pretty much any point you can reasonably expect on a deluxe-sized toy is here, and with a lack of back-heaviness, only the couple of pieces behind his upper legs limit the number of poses he can take.
It took me a while to decide which version of deluxe Barricade I liked better; this one or the original movie mold. The first try was larger, had better robot legs, and came with a little Frenzy partner. This version, however, has better articulation; a slightly easier, but still satisfying, transformation; and better arms, head, and chest in robot mode. I think the edge goes to this version due primarily to the increased amount of articulation and poseability-- though if you're looking for a "definitive" version of the character and money isn't a factor, I'd still get the Human Alliance figure.


Barricade Bio :
Barricade doesn't make friends, he finds potential victims. His seemingly benevolent police car vehicle mode has been known to trick potential targets into trusting him. Barricade has the ability to stalk and eliminate nearly any Autobot he wishes. Humans and Autobots alike should take cover when they notice police sirens in the distance-- it could be the last thing they ever hear!
