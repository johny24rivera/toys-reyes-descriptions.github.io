Nightbeat (2008 Collectors' Club Exclusive)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size: Deluxe
Price (in addition to membership): $48 (U.S.) {comes with exclusive comic}
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme: "Cheese" yellow, moderately light blue, black, and some silver, clear plastic, light orange, white, and moderately dark red
Rating : 8.4

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Energon Hot Shot. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Energon Hot Shot here .)

Another fan-favorite character gets redone from an existing mold, and this time it's the daring Autobot detective Nightbeat! Energon Hot Shot's vehicle mode was already heavily reminiscent of Nightbeat's G1 vehicle mode, so redecoing it up in Nightbeat's colors wasn't much of a stretch. The colors in general are a nice match- the light blue and yellow work together well, though I think using a bit less of an orangey-yellow would have looked a bit better, it's a tad loud compared to the other colors. I'm also not too fond of his gun being made of clear plastic, though I guess if they had to make the car windows normal clear plastic this was unavoidable. The black serves as a very nice contrast, however, and Nightbeat was certainly spared no expense when it came to the paint applications-- he's got lots of 'em. The flames on his doors look cool, but he's also got a lot of minute details on his chest and lower legs-- including even a bunch of nifty black lines on the tops of said lower legs-- that really give him a lot of variety.
Nightbeat also has a mold change-- a new head! Unfortunately... it doesn't look like it should. It SHOULD have been a spot-on representation of G1 Nightbeat's head... but the final product has the large antennae on the sides of his head-- his head's defining characteristic-- completely removed from his body. Apparently, they didn't meet "clearance issues" for the transformation, and someone at the factory in China chopped the antennae off of the mold and didn't tell the TF Club guys until it was too late. A real shame, and I'd be lying if I didn't say it subtracted from the look of the toy a bit, but you can hardly blame Fun Publications for this.
Overall, if you're a fan of Nightbeat, and the removed antennae doesn't bother that much, than this is a must-buy, and it comes with a cool detective comic story. But otherwise, given the prices of Club exclusives, your money is probably better spent elsewhere, as this isn't exactly the best mold in recent years.


Nightbeat Bio :
FUNCTION: Detective
MOTTO: "Tackling the mysteries of the Universe... one planet at a time."
When Grimlock approached Nightbeat about joining his crew in the search for Bludgeon and the rest of the Decepticons who had fled Klo, he naturally jumped at the opportunity. It was a mystery to solve, and Nightbeat was ready for the challenge. Along with Muzzle and their trusted (and rather loud) comrade Siren, the three headed off with Grimlock and the rest of the crew aboard the Graviton.
Now, after deciding that even the remnants of war was just something he no longer wanted to be a part of, Nightbeat finds himself commanding a crew of his own: one made of comrades, new and old, together mapping out different star systems to explore and finding new mysteries to solve!
