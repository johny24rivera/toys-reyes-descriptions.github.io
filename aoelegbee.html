Bumblebee (Legion, AoE; Solo-Packed) [Wal-Mart Exclusive]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legion
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately dull yellow, black, and some light sky blue and off-white
Rating : 7.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original RotF Legends class Bumblebee. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original RotF Legends class Bumblebee here .)

Guess what? It's Bumblebee! In a very Bumblebee-like color scheme! If you're expecting this exclusive redeco to have a vastly different color scheme from most other movie 'Bee toys, well... keep looking. That said, beyond the usual "yellow and black" color scheme-- and the inaccurate robot mode design and still-inaccurate-but-less-so vehicle mode design-- there's a few minor things different for this redeco. The yellow is a bit lighter than it is on most Bumblebees-- it's still a little dull, but the "orange" part of the usual orangish-yellow has been removed from the equation for this release; I think the purer yellow looks quite good, as it contrasts against the black better (it actually looks more yellow in person than in these pics, which tint it to more his usual orangish-yellow. Huh). And speaking of the black, Bumblebee has more black paint apps on this version than on most of his other Legion-class toys; beyond the obvious big stripe down the middle of the vehicle mode with little stripes around it, there's also nifty stripes down the sides of the vehicle mode, angling up and over the rear tires, and his front grill and headlights are black too. There's also a black Autobot symbol on the right side, and in robot mode some black paint around the middle section of his lower legs. Particularly in vehicle mode, this added deco looks nice. The light blue windows are really what make this redeco stand out, though, as they contrast nicely enough against the yellow but REALLY well against the black, and just give the whole scheme a "brighter" feel (but not in an obnoxious way). Bumblebee's face in robot mode is also painted a rather inaccurate-- but still cool-- off-white. It makes it look like his face is a skull, which I like, as it gives him just a bit of unintended creepiness. (Now I really want a "ghost Bumblebee" redeco...) The only important details that don't have paint on this version are the taillights.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Bumblebee.
If you have any previous version of this mold (or the superior Dark of the Moon legion-class Bumblebee ) that's colored like "normal" Bumblebee, there isn't much to recommend here, given how similar this release is to most other releases of 'Bee. That said, if you don't have one, this is one of the best-looking "normal" paint jobs this mold has had, with the added black paint apps, light blue windows, and a "yellower" plastic all making Bumblebee look better than he usually does.

Review by Beastbot
