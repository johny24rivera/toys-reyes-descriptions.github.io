Grimlock (Legion; AoE*; blacker paint job) [Discount Store Exclusive]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legion
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Black, brown, and some red, metallic bronze, silver, and light sky blue
Rating : 7.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Prime legion Rippersnapper toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Prime legion Rippersnapper toy here .)

Yes, the Prime Rippersnapper mold gets another re-do as "Grimlock"-- and actually released about the same time as the Wal-Mart exclusive AoE version , to boot! However, though this discount store-exclusive version and the Walmart-exclusive version are both the same mold, both named Grimlock, and generally have brown and black as their main colors, they're about as different as they can be while still fitting those descriptors. Their paint masks are completely different, and black actually dominates this Grimlock's color scheme considerably more, especially in beast mode. The brown on this version is a more straightforward, "muddy" milky brown, compared to the rather orangish brown shade on the Wal-Mart exclusive that was more similar to other Grimlock toys in the AoE line. Additionally, this version of Grimlock has a fair amount of red on him, too-- mostly on the middle of the beast "fin" and around the "jaw gear" detailing, with a bit of red on the robot chest, as well. These three colors go together exceptionally well-- black works better in the greater amount that it's used, the more traditional shade of brown just looks better, and the red paint contrasts excellently against both colors. There's also a bit of metallic bronze used on this version, mostly on the top of the beast mode-- and it's a really nice shade of the color as well, quite closely matching the brown but being just a tad lighter and metallic, both contrasting against the black and complementing the brown. For some more accent colors, both the robot and beast eyes are light blue, and the robot face has some silver on it, though said colors aren't used anywhere else and thus don't contribute to the overall scheme much, though they do look nice.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Grimlock-- like the other AoE legion Grimlock, he unfortunately doesn't come with any weapon.
This legion class release of "Grimlock"-- with its increased usage of black-- makes it look even less like the AoE version of the character, much less any version of Grimlock, but honestly I'd recommend you ignore that. It's a great mold in a great scheme with just the right amount and shade of each color, and just looks great, regardless of who he's supposed to be. Recommended over the Wal-mart exclusive version of AoE Grimlock, and recommended that you just name him something else and accept the fun little guy into your collection.
