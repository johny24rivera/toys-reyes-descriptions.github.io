Transformers Kids' Meal Toys

This section of the site reveals all transformable McDonald's and Burger King pack-in Meal toys released since 1996.
The first line, the 1996 wave, featured five toys. These toys had no real names, and were just called names like "Beetle" and "Rhino". These iincluded Beele, Manta Ray, Panther, Rhino, and the toy for children under three.
The second line, the 1998 wave, featured three toys. These toys had real names, and were names taken from other Beast Wars toys. These included Dinobot, Blackarachnia, and Scorponok. All were supposedly " Transmetals " even though they had no third modes.
The third line, the 2000 wave, featured nine toys. These toys had their respective show names, and in some cases, they were even more show-accurate than the "actual" Beast Machines toys themselves! These toys included Optimus Primal, Rattrap, Blackarachnia, Cheetor, Nightscream, Jetstorm, Tankorr, Thrust, and Megatron.
The fourth line, the 2002 wave, featured eight toys. These were McDonald's versions of Armada Optimus Prime, Smokescreen, Hot Shot, Red Alert, Megatron, Starscream, Demolishor, and Cyclonus. Besides having Powerlinx ports and their own gimmicks, all four Autobots and all four Decepticons could "link up" into a gestalt version.
The fifth line, in 2005, which was exclusive to Burger King, featured four toys-- miniaturized, "kiddie-fied" versions of Cybertron Optimus Prime, Red Alert, Jetfire, and Megatron.
The sixth line, in 2007, also exclusive to Burger King, were representations of the Movie bots, but since they didn't really transform, they are not reviewed on this site. Similarly, in 2009, Burger King representations of the Revenge of the Fallen Movie bots were released, but they also didn't really transform and thus are not reviewed; Also, in 2010, McDonalds' released non-transformable figures of some more movie 'bots and 'cons.
The seventh line, in 2008, was exclusive to McDonald's and was a Transformers Animated promotion, featuring Happy Meal versions of Optimus Prime, Megatron, Starscream, Bumblebee, Ratchet, and Lugnut from that series.
