Cindersaur (Botcon 2010 Attendee-Only Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe (comes in a 2-pack with Rapido )
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Moderately light blue, royal violet, transparent dark blue, light lime green, and some light milky gray, light red, white, and silver
Rating : 9.3

NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Beast Wars 10th Anniversary Megatron. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Beast Wars 10th Anniversary Megatron here .)

Hooboy, now THIS guy is an obscure homage. Cindersaur, in G1, was one of three Firecons, in addition to Sparkstalker and Flamefeather. The latter two Firecons got re-released in G2 in Europe only; both of them had purple and light green as main colors in their robot modes, while their beast modes were either transparent yellow or transparent red. Cindersaur's original mold was never re-released in G2, unlike his Firecon brethren, so now he gets a sorta G2 toy himself! Granted, he was a monster in the original, not a dinosaur, but given the "saur" part of his name, using a toy with a t-rex alt mode isn't too far off the mark.
Anyways, blue works surprisingly well for a dinosaur-- particularly this nice shade of moderately light blue-- sorta like some sky-dino or something. And the transparent blue parts really help to make the toy a bit more stand-out, given the lack of a new mold or anything of the sort. The light red and silver both go extraordinarily well with the blue. In robot mode, the purple and light green are more apparent and give Cindersaur an appropriate "lizard-esque" color scheme-- but they also blend in quite well with the blue in a fruity (but NOT "so-bad-its-good) kind of way. All of the right details painted, it all works together surprisingly well. I don't like the dull light gray-- it's way too overused and just looks bad-- but it's only used in very small amounts, so that's a pretty minor complaint.
No mold changes have been made to Cindersaur, though the Cyber Key has been changed to the standard Decepticon Cyber Key with the G2 Decepticon symbol painted on the front.
Cindersaur is a pretty darn imaginative homage, and though he has loud colors, unlike many of the other updated G2 characters in this years' set, I think they actually work together really well even ignoring the "kitsch" factor, and is in fact my favorite of the color schemes this mold has been released in-- not to mention color-wise my favorite toy of the Botcon 2010 exclusives. Still, he is admittedly a bit of a stretch when it comes to looking like Cindersaur, given the head that's so obviously a differently-colored Megatron and the different alt mode, but I still think he looks pretty darned cool.


Cindersaur Bio :
MOTTO: "Fire...burn...smash...crush!"
There is no nuance within the mind of Firecon Cindersaur. He is a simple creature whose whole purpose of existence is focused on the destruction of anything around him. This is not cruelty or some perverse enjoyment; to Cindersaur it is pure instinct to destroy and that is what he does best. His fusion powered flame breath can be used in both narrow and wide streams. His recent forestonite fueled upgrade has made this weapon more powerful. The constant stream of energy leaking from his joints causes him to leave a path of destruction everywhere he walks. A curious side effect of this energy leak is that he no longer needs to take baths in lava, as he was once so fond of, in order to soothe his aching joints. This has made Cindersaur both a faster and more efficient engine of destruction, but no more intelligent than before.
