Predaking (Beast Hunters; Cyberverse)

Allegiance : Predacon
Size: Commander
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme: Black, orange, and some yellow, moderately light milky gray, dull orange, red, and silver
Rating : 7.5

Predaking's Cyberverse-scaled form is certainly a winged, "traditional" dragon, but it has considerably differerent proportions from his other toys (and especially the show model). His body makes up considerably more of his mass on this toy due to his tranformation, while his wings are outright tiny compared to the huge, sweeping things they are on the show. His four appendages are also a bit short-- especially the back legs, as those are so tiny proportionally that they just look kind of ridiculous; it's hard to imagine him moving much on those stubby little things. His silouhette also slopes upward considerably more, with the neck molded into a raised position and his head far above the rest of his body. His tail is fairly short as well due to his tranformation, but not quite enough where it looks ridiculous like his back legs. There's also one fairly obvious robot mode extra in this mode-- it's obvious that the back two-thirds or so of his body are made from his robot legs, given their rather distinct shape and detailing. His robot head is also slightly visible underneath his chest, but that's small bananas, particularly on this small of a figure. However, despite all this, there are certainly positives about this mode. For one, his mold detailing is very well-done-- among the most detailed out of the Prime Cyberverse toys. The intricate armor plating/scales on his neck, tail, and accurate dragon headsculpt all look absolutely ace, and there's a pretty good amount of detailing on his legs and wings, as well. (His wings have a feathery look to them, though, which is definitely a departure from all other toys of this figure, not to mention the show model.) Predaking's general color scheme takes a hint from his G1 gestalt counterpart and is primarily the "Halloweeny" colors of orange and black, both of which contrast against each other very well. There's also a fair amount of milky gray (more obvious in robot mode), and while I'm not fond of the color, it's not of the super-bland very light variety, and there's a bit of swirly look to it that I like. A few accent paint apps of silver and yellow help to round out the color scheme-- I particularly like the addition of yellow, which both contrasts with the black and complements the orange. There's a bit of orange paint used on Predaking's head, but it's noticeably duller than his orange plastic-- I'm not sure why they didn't use a lighter shade to match, there. Other than Predaking's wings and legs (which, again, are small little things), he's pretty well-covered in terms of paint. For articulation in this mode, Predaking can move at where the wings meet the body, as well as at the front shoulders (at two points), front elbows (at two points), and SLIGHT side-to-side movement on the hips of his back legs; it's not the most articulated beast mode.
Predaking's transformation is only a slight variation on the "stand me up and do a headflip" beast transformation, with the variation being that his dragon back form his legs, not his dragon rear legs, which are tucked mostly away behind his lower robot legs and stay out of the way--- that's ONE positive for those back legs being so small, at any rate. Predaking looks better and a bit more imposing in this mode, though his wings still definitely should've been able to fold out wider or something. His core body is proportioned quiite well, with a fairly wide chest and powerful form. His head is just a tad tiny and has rather ambiguous mold detailing (Does he have a mouth or just a big chin? Why does the back end of the top of his skull point upwards? Etc.). The mold detailing on the rest of the robot bits is quite good, though, with some really nice alternating jagged/smooth details on his chest that give him a bit of a "royal" appearance with all the orange and yellow paint detailing on him; his head also has a good amount of paint detailing on it. The rear legs are minor kibble issues, but Predaking does have one major kibble issue in this mode, and that's his dragon head. It hangs down behind his back, and the instructions SAY to split them into two (pictured above); I generally don't like to do this, as it doesn't look that good and makes those pieces more obvious. I prefer to just leave it un-separated on his back; it gets in the way less that way. For articulation, Predaking can move at the base of the wings and the neck, shoulders (at two points), elbows (at two points), hips, knees, and slightly at the ankles-- about average for a Commander-class figure. He's a little back-heavy because of his dragon head and wings, but you can still get him into some decent poses. As for a weapon, Predaking can hold his tail in either of his hands, which has a push-missile function, and is a decent little weapon for such a small toy.
Cyberverse Predaking certainly has some positives-- his mold detailing is great (if at times inaccurate to the show model), he's got decent articulation and great proportions in robot mode, and a nice color scheme. However, he has plenty of proportional issues in beast mode, and a few kibble issues. As such, this is my least favorite toy of the character, and I recommend getting one of the larger versions of him-- it'll have a much more commanding presence over your Predacon army, anyways.
