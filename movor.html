Movor (Ruination Part)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Basic
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Japanese Name : Shuttler
Color Scheme : White-gray, very light gray, black, dull yellow-green, shiny orange, and some shiny black, grayish silver, and metallic purple
Mold History : Movor's mold dates all the way back to 1986, when the mold was released as the Combaticon Blast Off, part of the original Transformers line.
Rating : 5.4

Vehicle mode is a space shuttle. Although a bit simplistic in its design (there's not much mold or paint detailing in this mode at all), the overall design and proportions are nice, with no extras whatsoever. There's also nice Decepticon symbols on the wings. Speaking of the wings, their color is a couple shades lighter than the very light gray used for the main body of the space shuttle, and it makes it look rather odd in that way. A closer shade of color could surely have been chosen. What I really don't like about this mode, however, is the use of the dull yellow-green fade on the middle of the body. I realize that it's supposed to give the space shuttle a "worn" look, but it just makes it look like the plastic's yellowing. :\ Oddly, Movor is the only basic Ruination component that can't hold all his weapons in his alt mode in a convincing manner. Although his two larger guns can be plugged onto his backside (see above), his smaller gun can't be plugged in a hidden-away area. You can plug it into the top engine hole on the backside, but it sticks out VERY obviously there and just looks odd.
Although Movor's robot mode has more pleasing overall proportions than, say, Rollbar , his doesn't really have any arms. They're very obviously just the front halves of his space shuttle mode, and they look more like giant oven mitts than actual arms and hands. Also, Movor's articulation is rather limited in this mode- he can only move his arms at the shoulders (in two places) and at the "hands". This makes him very limited in his posability. At least the orange adds a bit of much-needed variety to the color scheme, although a bit more paint detailing is still needed.
Forms either an arm or a leg of Ruination, depending upon the configuration.
Movor is a rather uncreative and boring transformer, overall. He's worth getting if you want to form Ruination, but as an individual toy, he just doesn't measure up.
