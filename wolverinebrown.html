Wolverine (Brown) [Marvel TF Crossover]

Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dark brown, black, dull brownish gray, yellow, and some silver, white, and dark red
Rating : 8.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Marvel TF Wolverine. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Marvel TF Wolverine here .)

As opposed to most redecos nowadays, brown costume Wolverine is more of a straight pallette swap than an actual, completely different Transformer with new paint apps and the "works". His black and yellow parts are still all exactly the same and in exactly the same places compared to the original Wolverine TF, along with the white, yellow, and silver paint apps. The main difference is that the original Wolverine TF's blue has been changed to, obviously a dark brown. It's a straight color-swap, which makes it a bit more bland and unimaginative than it otherwise would have been, unfortunately. Less obvious changes are that the tires are a bit more of a brownish gray compared to a straight gray-- which of course complements the main color better-- and the interior of the wheels is now painted black, not silver, which works just as well, in my opinion. The black is also used a bit more liberally on the chest stripes.
No mold changes have been made to the brown-costumed version of Wolverine.
I wish a bit more had been done to brown-costumed Wolverine besides just turning the blue to brown and a few other minor changes, but it IS a signficant color swap, so he easily looks different-- and of course, it's accurate to Wolverine's brown costume design. I like it a bit more than the original release of this toy, but if you're gonna get one version, get the X-Force redeco instead.


Wolverine (Brown Costume) Bio :
Built to match the toughness and ferocity of Wolverine himself, this battle suit is fast, scrappy, and able to take a pounding that would turn other mechs into scrap. Advanced repair systems fix most damage done almost instantly. In addition, the mech mode pops out claws sharp enough to rip through almost any armor.
