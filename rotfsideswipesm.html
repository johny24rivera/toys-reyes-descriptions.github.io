Sideswipe (Strike Mission)

Allegiance: Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation: Medium
Color Scheme : Silver, moderately light milky gray, moderately dark gray, transparent dull red, and some metallic light red, clear plastic, and black
Rating : 9.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original deluxe RotF Sideswipe toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original deluxe RotF Sideswipe toy here .)

Aaand here's Sideswipe's mainline "slightly-repainted-just-to-keep-him-on-the-shelves" toy. The basic-- and rather bland-- Sideswipe color scheme of gray and black is intact, though thankfully with that sweet, sweet silver paint all over his vehicle mode. There are only two major changes made to Sideswipe's paint job this time around. First, three black stripe designs have been added to his vehicle mode (one on each side and one in the center of the roof), which add a bit more contrast to the vehicle mode and look quite good-- the side stripes also have the N.E.S.T. Global Alliance logo on them, which is a nice touch too. The only other major change is that Sideswipe's transparent plastic is now red instead of blue. Red, of course, works with silver and black, since the latter two are neutral colors, but I think the blue transparent plastic on the original deluxe is a tiny bit better-- more "Autobot-y", if you will.
No mold changes have been made to Strike Mission Sideswipe.
Strike Mission Sideswipe is an okay repaint, but I don't really have strong feelings towards the slight color changes one way or the other. I like the black racing stripes a little, but dislike the change of the transparent plastic to red pretty much equally. So whichever version you can find which fits your personal fancy more, get, though this version isn't nearly different enough from the original to justify getting both.


Strike Mission Sideswipe Bio :
Sideswipe is used to working alone, but he's enough of a team player to recognize that younger Autobots can benefit from his experience. What Mudflap thinks is a simple race is actually a training exercise. Sideswipe intends to put the young warrior through his paces, teaching him everything there is to know about offensive driving.
