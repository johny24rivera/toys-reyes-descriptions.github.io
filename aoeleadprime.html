Optimus Prime (Leader; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Leader
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dark glittery blue, light milky gray, dark flat red, and some black, chrome silver, chrome red, chrome dark blue, translucent yellow, silver, and light red
Rating : 7.8

Optimus Prime's leader class form's vehicle fits his on-screen appearnace quite well; by and large, the proportions are pretty on-the-mark, though the back end of the cab looks just a TAD longer than it actually is in the movie, though this just a very minor "stretching"-- he still looks just fine. There's also no robot mode extras visible in this mode at all, always a good thing. (That said, the back end of the cab is a bit hollow, given the sliders to shorten it during the transformation-- you can see through a good amount of it from a top view.) By and large the mold detailing in this mode is pretty sparse-- as is keeping with Prime's new Western Star truck mode, which is rather windswept for such a large vehicle-- though some parts have some more intricate detailing, such as the front grill, the side "steps" up to the driver windows, and the wheels, which have little Autobot symbols molded in the hubs-- a nice little extra touch. The color scheme is classic movie Prime, though in this mode less emphasis is given to the gray and more to the blue. The red flame paint apps on the front and middle of the mode look pretty ace, though being against so dark a color already they don't contrast quite as much as I wish they would have-- the blue "bleeds through" the red a bit. In place of (most of) the gray in this mode is a nice silver chrome-- I haven't seen any major chipping issues on mine, and the chrome is used in appropriate places (such as the smokestacks, front grill, and the like), and I quite like it; it gives this mode a bit of a visual flair, when color-wise it would've looked a tad plain otherwise. (The top of the truck mode and the back end in particular could've used some more paint apps.) There is also a touch of translucent yellow used for the windows-- honestly a rather odd color for windows, but it does contrast nicely against the surrounding colors. Optimus has two weapons-- a translucent yellow sword with a painted handle, which slides in neatly into a slot on the bottom of this mode and thus out of the way. However, it does tend to wiggle around in that slot a little, so I wish the fit was a bit more firm. Optimus' other weapon can double as either a shield of a gun, depending on how you orient it-- a pretty ingenious idea, I must say. It stores on the top of the back end of this mode, which may look a little odd, but this does help to mask all the gaps in said back end. Although I like the red paint apps on this weapon, the rest of it is of that blah light milky gray plastic that I rather despise is being used so much on TFs these days when a more metallic-looking shade of the color would look much better.
Optimus' transformation to robot mode definitely takes some major design cues from his First Edition figure , but takes a few things a couple of extra steps further, resulting in slightly better compression/hiding of his vehicle mode parts in robot mode. SLIGHTLY being the operative word here-- there's still a large backpack made up of the back third or so of his truck mode on his back, the shoulder pads are definitely overly large, and the wheels and bits on the back of his feet are also pretty obviously truck parts, though at least the latter bits stick to his general silhouhette. In a new twist, parts of the sides of his truck mode rotate out a bit to become sort of "skirt armor". It's not movie-accurate, but it's a better idea than simply sticking even more onto Prime's backpack. Optimus' "core" robot mode proportions are pretty good in this mode-- granted, his chest could be a bit beefier and his arms a bit thicker, but being realistic when looking at his movie design, that would be pretty darned hard to implement. His color variation is much better in this mode, with all the blue being broken up much better with red, silver, and blue paint apps, along with more-dominant light milky gray (bleh). Part of his chest is also chrome, with both red and dark blue chrome, which looks mighty nice, even if the details on them aren't completely movie-accurate. Speaking of the details, Prime's sculpting is very well-done, with (mostly) movie-accurate mechanical, segmented armor all over him, which generally gives him a more "solid-looking" appearance than his previous movie design-- and given that it's movie-accurate, I can hardly fault Hasbro for that. Some parts like the fingers are incredibly intricately detaile,d with little knuckle hinges molded in and the like. The headsculpt is also pretty spot-on, and has his faceplate molded in the down position. For articulation, in this mode Optimus can move at the neck, shoulders (at three points-- six if you include his shoulder panels), elbows (at two points), rotation at the thumb, and movement at the hips (at two points, with one of them being nice, thick ratchets to support his weight), knees (at two points-- again, one with a heavy ratchet), and ankles (at a point that not only allows them to rotate some, but also swivel a bit to keep Optimus' feet flat on the ground if he's in an "A" stance). Optimus can also move at the waist, but you have to lift his skirt piece to do so, which just looks weird, so that's pretty unfortunate. When it comes to equpping his weapons, Optimus can hold his sword in either hand by rotating the thumb open and slotting the sword in firmly at the handle before closing up the thumb again-- Optimus' grip is iron-tight this time, which I wholeheartedly approve of. The sword can also sheathe behind his back, using the same slot used for storage in vehicle mode. As for his shield-gun, he has a port on the side of his right lower arm (why only the right?) where you can position it forward for a gun or vertically for a shield. Unfortunately, he can't hold his shield in his actual hands, though.
Age of Extinction Leader class Optimus Prime is the best design you're going to get of this character in the line, but that doesn't mean he's flawless. His truck mode is largely pretty darned good, albeit lacking a bit in paint apps and having a hollow back end. His robot mode is decent, but suffers a lot from all the vehicle kibble (albeit less so than his First Edition version), much of which can interfere with his otherwise good amount of articulation. Recommended, but more because this is the best transformable representation you're going to get of this intergral character as opposed to the awesomeness of the toy itself.


Optimus Prime (Leader) Bio:
Optimus Prime has long believed that freedom is the right of all sentient beings. He has never wavered from his belief, no matter what has befallen him in battle. Once again ready to defend the people of his adopted planet, the unyielding Autobot Commander prepares for the battle that will allow the Autobots to reclaim their role as Earth's honored protectors.
