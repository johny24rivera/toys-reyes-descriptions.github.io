Optimus Prime (First Edition) [Age of Extinction] {Online Retailer Exclusive}

Allegiance : Autobot
Price : $60 (U.S.)
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dark blue, red, silver, transparent pale yellow, moderately dark milky gray, chrome silver, and some black, chrome red, chrome pale blue, and transparent blue
Rating : 7.3

Optimus Prime's new form got a star appearance in the Age of Extinction line, with this toy actually being released to some online retailers early in 2014, before the main line hit store shelves. In vehicle mode Optimus Prime is surprisingly large-- more akin to the "old" Leader-class size from the mid-2000s than the newer Leader class size. He's now a more windswept, aerodynamic truck cab compared to his previous movie form, and this mode looks pretty near-perfect when compared to the actual customized Western Star truck it's based on. The lower section in the back may be a TAD short, but only just. There is one obvious robot mode extra in this mode, and that's the chest easily visible behind the front window. (There's also a gap in the back end, but it doesn't reveal anything.) This wouldn't be so bad if the chest weren't chrome, but it is, so it sticks out like a sore thumb. The side windows aren't transparent plastic so at least the chest doesn't show from the sides, but on the other hand this means that the windows mismatch. The color scheme is pretty much classic Optimus Prime-- you know what you're getting by now, dark blue and red with some silver/gray. The red used for Optimus' rather extensive flame paint apps looks like it's a bit duller of a color than usual, but it still looks great against the dark blue (and in fact is lighter than it appears in this mode-- the dark blue plastic "bleeds" through the red paint in this mode, whereas the red paint on Prime's lower robot arms is the same shade, yet looks lighter). Nearly all of the gray in this mode-- except for the wheel hubs and the back side of the truck-- is either painted a rather eye-catching shade of silver, or is chrome-- something we haven't seen much of lately at all, and in this mode it's mostly visible on his front grill. As of this writing about three years later after release (3/17), the chrome is still holding up very well, so there doesn't seem to be much cause for concern when it comes to chipping. The various paint apps and chrome do break up Optimus Prime quite well in this mode, though another few flame apps on the top or the back end wouldn't have hurt. I will say that this mode is a bit light on the mold detailing, however-- granted, it's a streamlined truck, but even things like the "bolts" on the side seem a little fewer in number than usual. That said, there's still some parts-- like the smokestacks and steps on the side-- that are quite intricately detailed. Optimus Prime has two weapons-- a rather long sword and very large shield. The shield is the cooler of the two accessories, having a lot of extremely nifty "energy arc" detailing on the transparent pale yellow parts, like the silver-painted parts are "projecting" a larger shield-- it's stored on the top of the rear in this mode. Not exactly an inconspicious place, but I've seen worse. The sword slides into a slot on the underside (it can't store on the back in robot mode, unfortunately). On a small side note, the tires look like they're rubber, but they're not-- they're plastic, though they still roll quite well. The paint detailing on the sword isn't as impressive, though having a silver hilt while the rest of the blade and hilt are yellow make it look sort of like an energy sword (even though it's not in the movie). The yellow color in general seems a little "off"-- I think a clear transparent blue like on Prime's front window would've looked a little better.
Optimus' transformation is surprisingly simple for such a large toy, mostly consisting of folding up his truck panels and connecting his waist and shoulders to his main body. As far as general proportions go in this mode, the "core" robot mode is decent; there's plenty of that impressive mold detailing we've come to expect from movieverse TFs in their robot modes, with little panels and bolts everywhere, though Optimus Prime's new "knight" status in TF4 makes his core proportions smoother and curved and more like layered armor, rather than with many obvious vehicle parts on the core robot. (His head is sculpted with his mouthplate retracted, FYI.) The chest is just a touch flatter, the arms just a bit skinner, and his feet smaller than his movie model, but honestly given what the designers had to work with-- little of Optimus' truck mode is visible AT ALL on his movie model's robot mode-- these are very small sacrifices. What IS a big deal is just the absolute ton of truck kibble hanging off of Optimus in this mode. Pretty much his entire truck mode is on his back, with most of the lower sides of said truck hanging down down to his knees in a very unsightly manner. Granted, much of the mass on his back is folded up, but it still gets in the way of movement, particularly at the shoulders and knees. Optimus' legs fare a bit better; there's still large sections of the truck hanging off the sides, but parts of the roof and sides at least fold behind his feet to provide better support. Optimus IS surprisingly stable in this mode despite his back-heaviness because of this support, as well as the fact that his ankles are ball jointed. Optimus' other points of articulation are at the neck, shoulders (at two points, three if you include the blue "flaps"), elbows (at three points), hips (at two points), and knees (at two points). His hip "skirt" piece can also move forward a bit to get out of the way for forwards leg movement. Thus, he's able to get into a decent number of poses, considering how big his backpack is. Either of his fists can hold his sword, though there's only a hole for his shield on his left arm, oddly. Finally, as for colors, considerably more chrome is visible in this mode, and I generally like it on the chest and upper legs, and the red and blue chrome paint look pretty nice against all those details, but I wish the blue was applied a bit thicker or was a bit darker, as it doesn't quite stick out as much as it should on a few places like the shoulders. Between the chrome and the paint apps on the appendages, this mode is well-covered when it comes to paint.
First Edition Optimus Prime is a bit of a tough sell at $60 U.S. His truck mode is large and pretty fantastic and his weapons are nice, but his robot mode has oodles and OODLES of truck mode parts hanging off of it that just look bad. He's also a touch simplistic for the size and price, given that he's not aimed at little kids like the 1-step changers. I understand the constraints the designers were under, but in robot mode this toy just doesn't cut it as well as the regular mass-release Leader class AoE Optimus toy does. Only recommended if you're some sort of Optimus completist, if you value size and/or lots of pretty chrome over movie accuracy, or if you want a version of this mold that's more difficult to transform than the 1-steps and simplified toys, but not as difficult as the mainline Leader release.

Review by Beastbot
