Silverbolt (Beast Machines)

Allegiance : Maximal
Size : Basic
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Yellow, fiery red, transparent light blue, transparent dark blue, pale yellow, silver, shiny fuchsia, and black
Similarity to Counterpart : !
Rating : 5.0

Beast mode is a condor- sort of. The main, inexcusable problem I have with this mode is that the condor has friggin' ARMS. Birds don't have arms, at least not in this sense! It makes this look more like some kind of wacked-out feathered monster than an actual condor. Second, the color scheme just wakes your eyes water. Not only is it extremely bright and just plain looks like whoever picked it was color-blind, it is not accurate to the show version. The show version has a primarily purple and yellow color scheme, which contrasts much better, in my opinion. There is absolutely no reason Hasbro couldn't have put a show-accurate color scheme on this toy to make it more appealing. Third, in this mode, Silverbolt has his robot head sticking out of his butt, making it look like he's giving birth- kinda sick, really, I wish Hasbro would have tried to hide the face better. Also, this mode is not very show-accurate at all, with the exception of the head. Another oddity is that they used transparent plastic for both the beast and robot heads, a bad move- it just looks wrong. Posability is also very limited in this mode. On the upside, the fiery detailing on the legs is pretty neat, as is the feathered detailing on the wings. If you hold Silverbolt up the the light, his wings look really neat. This toy is also quite big for a basic- which, of course, is definitely not a bad thing. His spark crystal is pretty difficult not to spot, as it's right on top of his head.
Robot mode is actually pretty show-accurate- with the exception of the color scheme, of course. Except for the aforementioned color scheme, I actually don't find this mode all that bad. His posability is excellent in this mode, and he has a much better overall look to him in this mode than in his beast mode. The small feathers on his back also add a nice touch as well. One of the flaws, however, is that Silverbolt's condor head sticks out of his stomach a bit too much, giving him a kind of potbelly. This figure also has a bit too much transparent plastic, which is a bad habit running through the Beast Machines Maximals line. He has a very large weapon that consists of his bird wings and a small part attaching them. This small part has a small dagger on the end of it, but it's so small, it doesn't look like it can do much damage, especially with the large wings attached to it always getting in the way. There is some kind of action feature on this weapon, but it doesn't seem to serve any kind of obvious purpose. If you bring his wings together and then press the red button they swing out again. Some kind of weird flapping action for beast mode, I guess. His weapon can be stored on his rear in this mode, but it tends to get in the way too much, and it also falls off a lot, so I just leave it off in this mode.
An absolutely horrible beast mode, and an okay (but not great) robot mode. In my opinion, the worst of the basic Beast Machines Maximals.

Formerly a warrior with the Maximals, Silverbolt became the victim of the worst kind of crime. His spark stolen and reprogrammed by Megatron, Silverbolt was remade into the evil Vehicon aerial general, Jetstorm. With no memory of his Maximal past, it took Blackarachnia to finally bring him back to his former Maximals self. Reformatted into a technorganic condor, his wings create violent turbulence that topples Vehicons. In Robot Mode, eyes can fire static pulse lasers that paralyze Vehicons instantly. Silverbolt struggles to shake his Vehicon past and rediscover his noble Maximal ways.
