Autobot Whirl (Classics)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Baby blue, charcoal gray, and some dull greenish yellow, dull red, and dark navy blue
Rating : 7.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Legends of Cybertron Evac. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Legends of Cybertron Evac here .)

Classics Whirl is, like most others in the line, an homage to the G1 character in the same name. Granted, since he's just a straight repaint of a mold designed for a completely different character, he's only an homage in terms of the color scheme-alt mode combination, but he's still an homage. All of the colors go together surprisingly well-- I never thought baby blue would be such a fitting color for a Transformer, but it works pretty well against the charcoal gray. (This is one of the few instances where I feel a charcoal gray color works better than just a plain black.) The yellow lines in robot and vehicle modes also look really nice against the blue, though making it more of a plain yellow instead of a yellowish green would've looked a tad better. The dark blue paint apps and dark red optics in robot mode help to individualize the head a bit more from the rest of the body, since they're used in very few other places besides the head.
No mold changes have been made to Classics Whirl.
Classics Whirl is a great repaint of the LoC Evac mold-- beyond perhaps a minor change in the tone of the yellow, I wouldn't have changed a thing regarding the color scheme. Easily recommended over the rather loud LoC Evac toy.
