Sunspot (Autobot Alliance)

Allegiance: Decepticon
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme: Bleached white, yellowish orange, gray, and some silver, moderately dark rusty brown, and black
Rating : 8.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen Skystalker. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen Skystalker here .)

Sunspot certainly lives up to his name. The white on him is bleached, and pretty much exactly the shade of sand you'd find on a tropical beach. The yellowish orange paint apps add color contrast a fair amount with this white, and the sweeping lines in vehicle mode really make those paint apps look like rays playing off the surface. However, in vehicle mode there isn't really any significant amount of a dark color to offset all the lighter colors-- and honestly, this color scheme doesn't fit a stealth drone at all, which is probably my biggest beef with it. In robot mode, gray/silver and dark rusty brown-- along with a tad of black-- help to break up all the light colors a bit more, though only the brown seems to contrast and fit in with the color scheme particularly well.
No mold changes have been made to Sunspot.
Autobot Alliance Sunspot certainly has a "sunny" color scheme, but his colors really don't fit his alt mode at all, and his dark colors needed to be a bit more promiment (particularly in vehicle mode) to contrast a bit more with all his light colors. I'd definitely recommend RotF Skystalker's color scheme over this one.


Sunspot Bio :
Sunspot has memory problems. Mission objectives, target data and the names of his allies slip from his mind like sand through a sieve. He remembers how to fly most of the time, which is lucky, because he finds a simple joy in being airborne. What's unlucky is that he tends to forget the Autobots are constantly on the lookout for Decepticons operating on their own, so he often wanders directly into a surprise attack.
