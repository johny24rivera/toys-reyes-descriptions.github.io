Wideload (RotF)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size: Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme: Light whitish tan, light pale milky gray, light orange, and some black, off-white, very dark navy blue, and silver
Rating : 7.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen Rollbar. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen Rollbar here .)

Wideload's color scheme is a bit more reminiscent of a UPS delivery truck than his mold's predecessor's-- and the "Energon Express" logo on the sides of his vehicle mode seem to support that that's the kind of truck he is-- it takes a few more liberties than slavishly looking like your average delivery truck. It's sort of a like a delivery truck that delivers breakfasts or something. Wideload certainly has a color scheme that reminds one of a morning sunrise, with a rather nice creamy whitish tan being his predominant color, and light orange being a major accent color only helps that image. The bits of dark blue he has are a great dark contrast color against all that light plastic, but I'm not fond of the light gray. First off, it's a really bland color, but secondly as a major color in robot mode it doesn't either mesh well or contrast well against the whitish tan. If it was a bit darker like the silver it really would've worked better, methinks. As far as paint apps go, Wideload has a pretty decent amount of them, particularly in vehicle mode, with the silver and the "Energon Express" logos helping to break up the colors. In robot mode he's a bit sparse, though-- this is made doubly odd by the fact that two of his paint apps were rather wasted on the light paint apps on the inside halves of his shoulder connector pieces. Um, since those are definitely pieces of vehicle kibble, shouldn't the purpose be to draw attention AWAY from, not TO, them?
No mold changes have been made to RotF Wideload.
RotF Wideload has a pretty unusual, but original, color scheme that works fairly well. I think the light gray would've been better used as a dark gray or perhaps a bit more dark blue, but he'll definitely stand out (in a good way) on any Movieformer shelf. Recommended over Rollbar.


Wideload Bio :
Wideload thinks about two things: doing his job well, and looking good. So when a Decepticon scum like Sonar ruins not only his perfect polish, but also a cargo of sensitive electronics, he swears revenge. He doesn't know how he's going to take out the sea bound warrior, but he's determined to sink Sonar if it's the last thing he does.
