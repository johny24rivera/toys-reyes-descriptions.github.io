Scorn (Power Battler; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately dull fiery red, light milky gray, and some light sky blue, black, dull orange, fiery red, and silver
Rating : 7.6

One thing about Scorn's wacked-out spinosaurus mode when compared to his "Generations" deluxe version is that this one is actually more accurate to the movie model. Whereas the deluxe had a more traditional spine-fin along the back, this one has more accurate separate, long spikes (made of rubbery plastic for safety purposes, of course). Granted, there should be more rows of them, and they should be longer, but because of the transformation this might not be practical. Overall the proportions are quite good in this mode- there really aren't any robot mode bits to speak of, which is always good. (The pointy robot "knight" feet on the back end of the dino feet are technically extras, but they blend in so well as heels I don't really count them. They also help keep Scorn considerably more stable in this mode.) There's plenty of excellent mold detailing in this mode, with spike or spike-like protrusions everywhere and what looks like a more movie-accurate dino head when compared to his deluxe version. There's also a really nice segmented-and-spiky tail section (again, made out of rubbery plastic), and the fake teeth on the upper legs (for robot mode accuracy's sake) also look pretty good. The overall color scheme is pretty basic, being mostly a fiery red and light milky gray. Unfortunately, the shade of light gray is exactly that really boring blah shade that I wish Hasbro would quit using and instead use a more metallic shade. There's a bit of black on the feet and dino head, light blue paint on the eyes, and a bit of silver on the top of his spines. Unfortunately there isn't any of that orange like on the deluxe, which really would've helped spice things up a bit in this mode-- it's not a BAD color scheme, just a bit dull, particularly given the shade of gray. Most of the paint apps are also focused on the dino head, the spines, or the lower legs, which means that his body and tail are very much two-tone and need a bit more variety. Also of note is that the light red paint used on Scorn is just noticeably lighter than the red plastic, though just slightly-- you likely won't notice upon a first glance. For articulation in this mode, Scorn can move up-and-down at one section at the tail, back-and-forth at the hips, knee rotation, and downwards movement at the neck. I really wish some dino arm articulation or at least jaw movement would have been added, but otherwise it's adequate.
The transformation is pretty simple, even for a Power Battler-- you just split apart the head and fold them down the sides to become the robot arms, fold up the dino arms on the shoulders, and then straighten out the legs. Scorn's proportions for the main body and legs are quite good in this mode, though the legs could stand to be just a TITCH longer. The arms are where this mode suffers, with the dino and neck-halves not being thick enough to really work as arms, not to mention how movie-inaccurate they are (with the hands just merely molded into the inside of the dino head-halves). (This is another reason Scorn REALLY should have had jaw articulation-- so his hand-claws could open and close in this mode.) Other than that, the dino mode kibble folds away pretty well-- the dino arms work nicely folded against the shoulders, and the spines and tail are behind Scorn's back and out of the way. The mold detailing on the robot parts is also extremely well-done and surprisingly intricate, with "abs" and various armor plating-like detailing on the main body, and a hip "skirt" and feet also very well-sculpted. There's also some silver on the faceplate and sides of the main body, with some orange on the main body as well, all of which helps to break up Scorn's color scheme and make it more attractive than it was in beast mode. (The orange is a bit duller than I'd like, though.) Scorn's Power Battler gimmick is an odd one-- rotate the right shoulder forward and the middle section of his entire body will actually fold all the way around, allowing for the "tail whip" gimmick advertised on his packaging, with his tail swinging forward in front of his body rather abruptly. What's especially odd about this is that there's an entirely new front and head for Scorn when his tail is deployed in the front (it's on the back normally). It's almost identical to the one seen when his tail isn't deployed, but his visor is blue as opposed to black for this version, and his faceplate is black instead of silver and a bit more angular and mouth-like. I rather like this head design better, even if it isn't movie-accurate. Simply flip the tail back to put Scorn back into his "normal" robot mode. What's great about this is that if you don't want Scorn's tail swinging loosely behind his back when it's not deployed you can simply closeup the dino back halves, and it's just as secure then as it is in dino mode (that is, very). On the negative side, the shoulder method of deploying the tail whip often doesn't work, so I usually just manually do it. For articulation in this mode, Scorn can move at the shoulders (at two points), elbows (at two points), hips, rotation at the knees, and at three points on his tail when it's deployed-- not spectacular, but a little above-average for a Power Battler.
Power Battler Scorn is the only "simplfied" toy for him in the AoE line, with no 1-step changer being released for him. As such, if you want a simpler-to-transform toy of the character, this is really your only option. Luckily, this is one of the best Power Battler molds overall, with minimal kibble in both modes, good proportions, excellent mold detailing, and fair paint detailing (he could use some more in beast mode). His tail gimmick is rather odd-- particularly in that it gives him two completely different robot chests and heads-- but if you don't like it, just keep the back closed in robot mode and you never have to give it a second thought.

Review by Beastbot
