Divebomb

Allegiance : Decepticon (Terrorcon)
Size : Basic
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : White, transparent light green, dark blue-green, milky tan, and some goldish yellow, dull gold, dark blue-gray, bright red, glossy dark purple, and dark red
Rating : 9.6

Beast mode is a hawk. This mode is definitely one of the best avian modes a Transformer has ever had, if not THE best. The proportions are much, MUCH closer to normal than they are on the other bird TFs- he's got larger wings than the others, while having a smaller body and feet. One oddity, though, is that Divebomb has no tail feathers to speak off- instead, it's just his robot head facing downwards. Needless to say, I think that this is a pretty poor attempt at hiding it, especially since it would have been so much easier just to put another fold-down tailfeather piece behind the head to cover it, and that would have solved the problem. It's a small complaint, though, seeing as how you're not usually looking at Divebomb from a rear view. Another small downside is that the Energon "neck feathers"- made up of the two halves of his weapon in robot mode- are definitely overly large, and look kinda tacked on. You can just take them off if they bother you that much, though. The color scheme itself is excellent, and fits in with the other Terrorcons in its "white, transparent green, and some other complement colors" scheme. He's also got quite a few paint apps for a basic toy, and a great variety of colors used just once or twice to give him a more eye-catching look to him. The mold detailing's pretty good, too. Oddly enough, Divebomb also has pretty good articulation in his HAWK mode. His wings can are on ball joints, so they can move pretty much anywhere you want them to. (Although it remains to be seen if the ball joints will eventually become weak enough where they won't be able to support the wings.) He can also move his little legs at the hips, knees, and can clasp his talons together... Wow. That's pretty darned impressive for a bird Transformer.
Divebomb's robot mode is equally impressive. Heck, when you've got a 'bot who can impale someone on his KNEECAPS, then you know he's gotta be aweseome. ;)Â  Anyways, for starters, he's got GREAT articulation. He can move at any point you can reasonably ask for on a basic figure, with the sole exception of the waist. His feet are also long, stable, and slightly adjustable, so you can get Divebomb into a good number of poses. My only complaint is that the bird legs hanging off the back of his arms don't look like anything- they're just kibble. They don't get in the way much, though, so it's a minor problem. Divebomb's proportions are also great. His feet are a little on the large side, but that seems to be part of the schitck with Energon anyways, and using the wings as legs was a GREAT idea, so I can easily let that pass. His head sculpt is also great- very bird-like, while stil retaining its evil robotic roots. The chest paint patterns are also nice, with several different complementary colors painted on. Divebomb's Energon weapon is a Z-shaped boomerang- it's cool-looking, and actually rather imaginiative. I don't think a Transformer's ever had a boomerang for a weapon before... The weapon has also got two Minicon ports on it, in case you want to mount it on a larger Transformer. Like the other Terrorcons, Divebomb has his Decepticon spark crystal displayed prominently on his chest in robot mode.
Divebomb is one of the best basics of the Energon line, if not THE best. And considering how good most of the other basics are, that's saying a lot. Divebomb is excellent in both modes, and quite possibly the best avian Transformer ever. I highly recommend him to anyone who has $7 (U.S.) to spare.


Divebomb Bio :
MOTTO: "To challenge me is to accept your defeat."
Divebomb is a ruthless ninja-like Terrorcon. Like a bird-of-prey, he never misses his target. In robot mode, his sharp Energon blades and slashing tools give him the edge in close-range fighting. He uses his leg turbines to hover when battling his enemies, overwhelming them with powerful slashes. In hawk mode, his dangerous feathers can cut like blades. There is no stopping this Terrorcon once he has selected his prey.
