Autobot Hound (Voyager) [AoE]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dark military bluish green, milky gray, and some black, clear plastic, silver, light electric blue, light red, semi-metallic gray, and metallic copper
Rating: 8.7

Hound's OSHKOSH military transport truck alternate mode is represented quite faithfully here-- the proportions are very spot-on, and other than just a touch of his upper legs sticking out from the bottom middle, there are no robot mode extras whatsoever in this mode (impressive, given his robot mode design). The back end is a bit sparsely detailed, but the rest of this mode has excellent mold detailing-- the rails on the sides, all the little details along the edges and center of each tire, the front grill and bar in front of the transparent windows-- all the necessary details are here. The problem is, only the very front of this mode is really painted. The various headlights are painted silver or copper, there's a black stripe along the grill in the front (complete with a tiny OSHKOSH logo), and the bottom bars are painted silver. That's it. Nearly all of the rest of this mold is a dark military bluish green, with a bit of milky gray (mostly due to the weapons) and black wheels. The front windows are also clear plastic, though there isn't a detailed interior or anything-- just a gray bar of plastic a little behind the windows. Now, granted, paint apps have certainly gotten more expensive, but out of all the "mainline" AoE toys, Hound probably needs paint the worst. To have so much of a voyager-class toy-- the side rails, the top hatch, the rear section, the taillights and rear bumper-- all unpainted dark green plastic is just a crying shame, given all the detailing in most of those spots. It's also very obviously inaccurate to the movie, to boot. Before I get onto the robot mode, though, I'd be remiss to not mention what is arguably Hound's defining factor on this toy-- his HUGE assortment of weapons. He's got two pistols, a (silver-painted) dagger, two short little guns that look like some sort of grenade launchers, two medium-sized blasters, and a quite large triple-barreled rotary machine gun weapon-- all of these weapons have various slots and pegs and can be combined in various different ways as well, and there's little ports and pegs on the sides of Hound's vehicle mode to hold them, along with a 5mm port on the top to hold one more. Really, having one toy with this amount of weaponry-- and how customizable it all is-- is just lovely.
Hound's transformation to robot mode mostly involves unfolding the feet from the bottom half of his truck mode, splitting apart various wide panels and folding them around his midsection to cover up his front vehicle section, and unfolding his arms from the sides. The result is a robot mode that's more accurate than you'd think. By and large his proportions are quite movie-accurate, with the exception of his stomach, which should be hanging quite a bit over his waist but on the toy is rather flat. (Of course, making a robot with an actual paunch would have been quite the challenge, at least at this scale with this budget.) His head is also a tad small when compared with his movie model. There is a bit of vehicle kibble, though-- most notably on his arms, where there's bits of the shell of his vehicle mode hanging off his hands and the back end of his elbows. There's also a little bit extra "vehicle shell" on his foot pieces, but honestly those are small enough to not really bother me. Hound's mold detailing in this mode is nothing short of amazing, with things like the copper ammo clips on his arms, the various details on his chest, the "compressed and fractured metal" bits on his legs, the wheel-feet, and the fake headlights on his shoulders all represented quite faithfully. Particularly impressive are his headsculpt--which is pretty darned movie-accurate, and even has a rubber "wire beard" sculpted onto the bottom that can move a little when you turn Hound's head from side-to-side-- and the incredible detail on his lower arms, which look like they're made of several ammo clips compressed together-- they're also made of the rubbery, bendy kind of plastic, and his lower arms can rotate a bit between his elbow and his hands because of this. Thankfully, he ALSO has a good amount of paint detailing in this mode to highlight all these details, like the black on his wheel-feet, the silver on the "vehicle fragments" on his legs, the bronze ammo details on his upper arms, and a silver face with Autobot-blue eyes. In short, he looks MUCH more interesting in this mode than in vehicle mode. He also has various little clips and pegs to hold his many weapons just like in his vehicle mode, though the weapons you put on his hips can get dislodged quite easily. For articulation, Hound can move side-to-side at the head, as well as move at the shoulders (at two points), elbows (at three points), waist rotation, and movement at the hips (at two points, though movement backwards is severely limited because of the shape of said hips), knees (at two points), and ankle tilts. Thus, with all of his weaponry, he's capable of pulling off some pretty awesome and varied poses (though I wish his heels were just a tad longer to give him that extra bit of stability).
AoE voyager Hound has a lot of really neat details and features-- ammo clips for arms, some unique robot mode proportions, a "beard", and-- most importantly-- a TON of highly customizable weaponry that can store in both modes. His vehicle mode is also almost perfect proportionally. That said, some issues-- such as really obvious vehicle shell fragments on his arms and a DESPERATE need of more paint in his vehicle mode-- keep him from reaching the tip-top of the AoE toyline. (I also miss his beer gut.) Still, a recommendation if you like the character (i.e., a gung-ho John Goodman) or lots of customizable weapons-- my favorite AoE toy mold of Hound.


Autobot Hound Bio :
Autobot Hound might only be a single soldier, but he prides himself on being able to do the jobs of ten. Whether the objective is a tactical deployment, recon mission, or infiltration and sabotage, Hound can get the job done. Heavily armored, he isn't about to back down from an all-out firefight if one comes his way. Some bots might see that versatility as showing off. To Hound, it's all in the line of duty.

Review by Beastbot
