Dinobot Slug (Power Battler; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Dark bluish purple, silver, light milky gray, and some light sky blue, dark metallic gunmetal gray, and red
Rating : 7.4

With a couple of notable exceptions, Slug's triceratops mode is done pretty well. Let's cover the bad stuff first. The proportions are slightly off, for one-- not obnoxiously so, but the main body isn't quite as bulky as it should be, and the triceratops head just a titch small. There's also some really obvious robot extras-- namely, the lower arms and their attached weapons, which just hang below the stomach and under the tail really obviously (made all the more obvious by their light blue coloration). On a lesser note, the tops of the rear legs are pretty obviously his robot feet, and the robot head is slightly visible below the triceratops head, but only from a mostly side-on view. The mold detailing-- while not QUITE as good as the deluxe -- is still pretty darned good, with bone-like "armor" detailing all over the place, particularly along his spine and the rather wicked-looking detailing on his dino face. The color scheme sticks to Slug's AoE toy colors pretty closely, with a dark bluish purple being the main color. There's also quite a lot of silver paint-- on much of the face and horns (which looks pretty darned awesome, if I do say so myself), shoulders, and the end of the tail (though not the rest of it, oddly). The rest of the tail-- and some parts that are more visible in robot mode-- is that darned blah light milky gray I wish Hasbro would pretty much get rid of for good. It's sooo boring looking, and should have been replaced with a more metallic-looking plastic. The red apps on the crest-- and visible from the sides of the stomach-- also help add some more color variation, and contrast well against the other main colors. There's also the usual Autobot-blue optics (which are totally not movie-accurate, it's worth noting-- but then again, neither are most of Slug's other colors). For articulation in this mode, Slug can sadly only "offically" move his tail up-and-down at the midpoint-- an unacceptable lack of articulation for a dino mode, even given that that's not the focus of this subline. You can also technically get his front legs to move back-and-forth slightly at the shoulders, but this involves bending the pieces below his stomach/his lower robot arms a bit, so I generally don't recommend it.
Slug's transformation is fairly straightforward-- you unfold his legs and lower arms, stand him up, and crack his skull in half and rotate the halves to his shoulders. Voila! Robot mode. The proportions in this mode are generally pretty good, with a better-proportioned chest than his deluxe version, though I wish the skull-halves sat a little higher and not as far back behind his arms (though, to be fair, this would interfere with his gimmick). He also looks a bit stiff, though this is more because of his limited articulation-- he can move at front-to-back and outwards at the hips, and front-and-back at the shoulders, but that's it. I don't see why at least knee and/or head articulation couldn't have been added. The mold detailing continues to be quite good, with the ridged armor becoming a bit more refined for most of his robot bits on his chest and upper legs. His robot headsculpt is also spot-on, very much a round medieval knight helmet with a blue visor. The paint is also still quite liberally applied to this mode, on the chest and abdomen, shoulders, and head. The addition of metallic gunmetal gray helps to vary up the color scheme more and provide a better alternative to that light milky gray, though I'm not a fan of the light blue used for his weapons and the oddly simplistically-sculpted hands they're permanently attached to-- it sticks out a bit TOO much without other accenting on Slug's body, and doesn't really fit with the rest of Slug's scheme. These light blue parts are made of rubbery plastic, and are part of Slug's Power Battler gimmick. Pull back-and-forth on Slug's tail behind his abdomen and his arms will move up-and-down quickly-- given the mace and sword on the end of each arm, this is the "Smash and Slash" the packaging talks about. It's honestly a pretty fun little gimmick, even if it's not mind-blowing. It also doesn't interfere with shoulder articulation as much as you'd think, as you can still push them up-and-down manually, you'll just hear the click of some internal ratchets (not breaking, just moving) as you do so.
Slug is one of the better Power Battler molds. Sure, he's got a few issues-- namely an unacceptable lack of articulation in dino mode, as well as some obvious robot extras in said mode, and his robot mode is a bit stiff-looking-- but his action feature is pretty fun, he's got a good color scheme with a good amount and variety of paint colors, and he looks accurate enough to the movie design (colors aside) in both modes. If you're looking for a simpler-to-transform version of AoE Slug, I'd go with this version.

Review by Beastbot
