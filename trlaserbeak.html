Laserbeak (Titans Return)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation to Vehicle : Very Easy
Difficulty of Transformation to Beast : Easy
Color Scheme : Black, red, and some silver, dark glossy purple, and yellow
Rating : 6.1

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Combiner Wars Buzzsaw. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Combiner Wars Buzzsaw here .)

This mold first came out a bit "early" in Combiner Wars, but now it's out in the Titans Return line proper as the more popular of Soundwave's two birds, Laserbeak. Like Laserbeak, the main color on this toy is black, and he's got some red that really contrasts excellently against the black-- unfortunately, this red is only really visible in bird mode, on the fold-out wing bits and on the back of his neck. There's also some silver on the "vents" at the bottom area of the smartphone, as well as some silver on the beak, head crest, and bottom of the feet. As far as unique colors, the eyes are yellow and there's a nice shade of glossy dark purple that's used in the center of the bottom of the smartphone mode, and which looks excellent against the silver-- unfortunately, such an awesome color is used nowhere else. Because of just the overwhelming dominance of black in all of Laserbeak's modes (but especially his smartphone and vehicle modes), he ends up looking a lot more boring than he otherwise would have, unfortunately. Now, the stickers used on the smartphone screen COULD have been used to negate this somewhat, but unfortunately the screen stickers are mostly black too. That's not to say that they don't look good; the stickers have a nice "graph" look to them, with red lines used for the graph and silver on the background of the small on-screen labels. It has the usual little "smartphone symbols" on the top like wireless signal strength and the time, but most of the rest looks more like Laserbeak's spying readouts-- he's got "soundwaves" being recorded, some little dots on the graph that are labeled "Intercept", a "Playlist" next to an icon of his G1 cassette form, an "anti-Autobot" Spyware icon, and of course a Decepticon emblem in the middle. It's all quite creative; I just wish it didn't blend in so well with the rest of the smartphone mode.
No mold changes have been made to Titans Return Laserbeak.
Titans Return Laserbeak is the better of the two releases of this mold, mostly because I'm not a fan of the yellow and gray on Buzzsaw. Laserbeak's black looks great when it's contrasting against the red (and to a lesser extent the silver), but he doesn't have enough of these other colors to make him visually interesting-- even his smartphone screen stickers are mostly black (which unfortunately can scratch off a bit on the edges as well). If you absolutely must have more minions for Titans Return Soundwave, you really don't have many other choices, but it's a very below-average mold with a boring color scheme; there's many versions of this character that have much better toys.
