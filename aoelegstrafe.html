Strafe (Legion) {AoE*} [Discount Store Exclusive]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legion
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark blue, moderately light blue, light greenish blue, and some light blue and silver
Rating : 7.4

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Prime legion Windrazor toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Prime legion Windrazor toy here .)

This store-exclusive release of Strafe at discount stores like Family Dollar and Dollar General keeps the dark blue of the mass-release toys of the character, but instead of using black as a major secondary color, a moderately light, milky and dull blue is used as the other color for most of the main body. This leads to a nice effect where the two main colors both contrast and complement each other fairly well. The most noteworthy thing about Strafe's color scheme, however, is the rather eye-catching greenish blue spray paint apps on his wings. They "fade into" the dark blue quite well, and again, both contrast and complement against the dark blue. However, when seen as a whole, the whole toy doesn't really have enough shades OUTSIDE of blue to make the color scheme particularly awesome-- it's just blue, blue, and more blue, even if there's multiple shades of it. If it wasn't for the blue-green spray, it would be outright boring. There's a bit of silver paint on the beak and headcrest in beast mode and the face in robot mode, but that's not nearly enough to make a substantial difference on the toy. (The light blue eyes in both modes do look nice, as well, but again-- just another shade of blue.)
No mold changes have been made to this version of Strafe.
AoE legion Strafe is a nice "consolation prize" for those who can't afford the more expensive (and more accurate) Strafe toys, and it's a pretty decent mold, but I wish there had been a bit of black or more silver paint added to break up the color scheme of the main body more. The greenish blue spray on the wings is nice, but the toy needed a bit more to make it stand out.
