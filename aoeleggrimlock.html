Grimlock (Legion; AoE; browner paint job) [Wal-Mart Exclusive]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legion
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Black, milky orangish bronze, and some silver and light sky blue
Rating : 7.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Prime legion Rippersnapper toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Prime legion Rippersnapper toy here .)

Gu- huuuh? Well, this is certainly one of the most bizarre mold re-uses I've ever seen Hasbro do. A Prime Predacon re-done up as movie Grimlock?! Granted, there are some similarities-- the alt mode has t-rex-sized arms and legs, a tail, and sharp teeth-- but pretty much everything else is different. I mean, Rippersnapper was a shark monster, and the the robot modes of this toy and movie Grimlock look NOTHING alike. However, putting all that aside and focusing on the color scheme, this Wal-Mart exclusive carries over the general color scheme used for most of AoE Grimlock's toys, with a bronzish color being used as the main plastic color, and with a fair amount of black in there to provide some contrast and a bit of silver to mix things up a bit further. The bronze for this version, however, is of a distinctly more orange-ish shade than a brown-ish shade, and I don't think the lightening of the color looks all that great. A brown fits the alt mode and general color scheme better. What's more than that, Grimmersnapper needs some more paint apps or some different plastic colors to break up the toy (all of the plastic on the toy IS milky orangish bronze-- all the black is paint). In robot mode Grimlock looks decent enough in terms of his color makeup, with the black and silver chest looking pretty good, along with the silver bits on the sides of the lower legs and the nice light blue eyes. However, in beast mode the arms, legs, and tail are entirely void of paint, and the shark fin section doesn't have all that much paint on it, either, making him look more dull than he should.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Grimlock, though unlike with Rippersnapper, this mold comes with no weapon accessory this time around.
I'd only recommend this toy for Prime Predacon completists or those who can't get find any other version of Rippersnapper for a decent price. The mold itself and the color scheme of the toy isn't bad, but it isn't great either, with the bronze plastic being a slightly uglier, orangish shade compared to most other AoE Grimlock toys, while the paint is a bit barren in beast mode. The mold utterly fails at being Grimlock, as well; I'd recommend coming up with some alternate name for the character if you want to add him to your collection, and pony up for one of the larger Grimlocks if you want a toy of his Age of Extinction design.

Review by Beastbot
