Spider-Man (Race Car; Black Costume) [Marvel TF Crossover]

Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Black and some white, silver, red, and milky silvery gray
Rating : 8.4

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Marvel TF Spider-Man race car. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Marvel TF Spider-Man race car here .)

Predictably, the race car version of Spider-Man has gotten a "symbiote"-flavored repaint, much like the first motorcycle version of the character. Considering that the color schemes are pretty much identical, my comments on the overall black costume color scheme are pretty much the same here. Black and white would make for an interesting color scheme-- provided there was more color variety. This toy is almost entirely black plastic, and white is relegated to a few paint apps here or there, with the only major white paint apps being those symbiote spider symbols. (What's weird is that the symbol used for the mech mode chest is partially, but not completely, covered up by the cockpit in vehicle mode, making it look a bit odd in that mode.) There are a few, very minor, instances of other colors-- namely just the red paint used for the taillights and the gray/silvery plastic and paint used for the vehicle mode tailpipes and the "wrist blasters" in mech mode, respectively-- but it's not nearly enough to keep him from looking really monochrome. And boring, quite frankly.
No mold changes have been made to the black costume version of the Spider-Man race car.
Even though black is more fitting for a race car than a blue/red combo, this toy is almost completely black, with a a bit of white here and there. (The other colors are used so few times they're essentially nonexistent.) I really wish they had given the other colors a bit more of a presence on this mold, as it really could've looked cool then. But as it is, it's just way too much black and not enough anything else. I'd recommend the first version over this one.
