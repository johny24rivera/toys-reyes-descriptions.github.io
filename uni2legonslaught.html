Onslaught (Legend) [Universe 2.0]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Legend
Series : Classic/Generation One
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dull gray, black, silver, and some light red, white, and dark glittery blue
Rating : 3.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Legends of Cybertron Red Alert. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Legends of Cybertron Red Alert here .)

The idea for this repaint of Cybertron Defense Red Alert into Onslaught was apparently heavily inspired by the new Ultra Onslaught mold also made for Universe 2.0, given that it's now a S.W.A.T. vehicle instead of Onslaught's G1 alternate mode of an artillery truck. Quite honestly, though, it's not that great of an homage. If I hadn't been told that it was Onslaught-- and if Onslaught hadn't been released in another form in the same line as a S.W.A.T. vehicle-- I never would have guessed this was him. The original Onslaught had more a greenish-bluish-gray scheme to him, while this Onslaught is just dull gray and black mostly, with a bit of dark blue. It's close, but not close enough to really be enough visual recognition. Taken by itself and ignoring the homage, Onslaught's color scheme certainly doesn't clash. Dark blue, black, and gray are all dull colors. The silver gun provides a nice bit of contrast, but there should have been more of that silver paint, as the figure itself just looks waaay too dull. Not nearly enough contrast, and what's worse, the dark blue, silver, and light red are pretty much the exact same shades and in the exact same spots as on the Legends of Cybertron Red Alert toy, so it actually looks a bit like Red Alert at night time or something. Not quite different enough, if you ask me. (As an odd side note, Onslaught here is another one of the Universe 2.0 toys to have the Movie Decepticon symbol instead of the normal one. Huh.)
No mold changes have been made to Universe 2.0 Legends Onslaught.
There's not much positive for me to say about Onslaught. His color scheme is pretty boring for the most part, he has too many paint apps in common with LoC Red Alert, it's a horrible mold, and the homage is a little nonsensical to boot. Not recommended except for completists-- save up for the Ultra-class toy instead.
