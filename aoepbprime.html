Optimus Prime (Power Battler; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Dark semi-metallic blue, light milky gray, light red, and some silver and black
Rating : 4.9

Optimus Prime's vehicle mode of a Western Star truck cab is reproduced fairly faithfully for his Power Battler version... if you look at it from the front, at least. The proportions are very close to ideal, and from the front at least there's no robot kibble visible. This all changes when you look at this mode from the back, though-- the back third or so of the cab is VERY clearly Optimus Prime's arms, with the rear four wheels pretty much just molded onto the sides of them. There's even only robot upper arms connecting that back section to the center portion. On a lesser note, when looked at from a rear view, one can clearly see Optimus' face and upper chest inside the main body of the cab, which is a little distracting. As far as the colors go, they're pretty classic Optimus-- dark blue, red, and gray (unfortunately that ugly light milky shade of gray that's used way too often on TFs these days). The dark blue plastic has a bit of a swirly, semi-metallic glint to it-- I rather like this shade. The light red is pretty straightforward, though it's light enough where when it's painted on the dark blue plastic, the blue "bleeds" through a bit and makes the red look a little darker than it actually is. There's also some silver paint on the front roof section, but other than that and the flames this mode is really lacking in paint apps, which is a real shame given how intricate the mold detailing is in places. The little traction bits on the side steps, the side tanks, the smokestacks, the front grill... all are begging to get some black and/or silver paint, but there isn't any. I will say that the windows look surprisingly good unpainted, given that they're dark blue plastic.
Optimus Prime's transformation is fairly simple, most of it involving folding out the truck halves from the center and them moving around a few panels. I do like that the grill panel has a tab to firmly stick it in Optimus' lower back, and that the smokestacks actually fold back to form heels, to keep him nice and stable. That said, the roof panels don't really hide themselves very well on the lower legs, and the tabs that are supposed to lock them in place in this mode aren't as solid as I'd like. From the FRONT, this mode looks... okay. I guess. Anything above the knees looks pretty decent and fairly proportional, with excellent mold detailing on the chest and upper legs specifically-- despite the smaller size, nearly all of Optimus' chest bits like the small circles, "skirt" armor, and the like are replicated quite well. That said, his chest does stick out from in front of his head a bit too far, and though his arms also look pretty good when it comes to the mold detailing, they're raised a bit too much, like Optimus is permanently in a "What you lookin' at, punk?!" pose. The lower legs, though, look terrible, ESPECIALLY if you look at them from the side. There's no getting around it. From the sides, they look like the hollow truck halves that they are with a couple of odd panels sticking out. From the front, they don't align with the upper legs, the center portions are waaay too obviously the front sections of the truck mode, and the parts that are supposed to be his "feet" have incorrect detailing and are way too long. They're just a mess, really. Although Optimus' paint is a bit more plentiful in this mode-- what with the red and dark blue detailing on the chest, and the light red detailing on the lower arms-- there's still some parts on his chest and upper legs that are begging for some silver paint. All this ugly light milky gray just looks bad. For Optimus Prime's Power Battler gimmick, he has "Sword Strike" action-- if you squeeze his legs together, his arms will fold back behind his back a bit while fake, all-gray arms fold forward over his shoulders, holding a dark blue sword that's stuck in their grip. Given how the regular arms are still clearly visible and how odd these "fake" arms look, this action feature looks... really weird and is just another awful aspect of this mold. For articulation, Optimus Prime's shoulders and knees can move outward, and he can rotate at his elbows. Other than the elbows, that's pretty much no useful articulation... that's bad, even for a Power Battler.
Unfortunately, Optimus Prime is pretty much the worst of the Power Battlers. His truck mode is small for the size class, with not enough paint and very obvious robot extras on the back end. In robot mode, he's got extremely awkward legs, pretty much no normal articulation, and an incredibly bad action gimmick. Unless you're an Optimus completist, there's really no reason to get this toy-- if you're looking for a simplfied AoE Prime, get the One-Step Changer or the Smash 'n Change version instead.

Review by Beastbot
