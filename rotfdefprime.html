Optimus Prime (Defender)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation : Hard
Color Scheme : Dark flat navy blue, light milky gray, moderately light gray, and some gray, light pale metallic red, silver, dark metallic blue, light pale red, metallic bronze, and clear tinted plastic
Rating : 9.1

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to
the Target Exclusive Robovision Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Robovision Optimus Prime here .)

Defender Optimus Prime is like many of the late-line RotF repaints, in that some deco bits have been altered along with one of the main colors, but it's by no means a "full" repaint. The dark blue is pretty much everywhere on this version of Optimus Prime that it is on the original deco job, though the blue is a bit flatter, and a slightly duller shade. The main change is that the front portion of the cab-- as well as more of his robot mode-- has been changed to a light gray. Having too much light milky gray is already a problem with many of the movie toys' color schemes, and adding MORE of it certainly doesn't make Defender Optimus Prime more attractive. It really makes him look boring, particularly in vehicle mode, where it almost looks like the front part of his paint job has been burned off because of extreme heat or something. The change of the flames coming off the front grill being changed to a light metalllic red helps a little bit, but it doesn't contrast as much as it should-- a darker metallic red would've looked a bit better there. (That said, the silver flames against the dark blue look really nice.) His robot mode looks a little better, with more paint apps spread out over all his gray plastic to vary it up a bit more-- including a really nice shade of metallic bronze-- but generally his robot mode also looks more boring than it did in its original paint job.
No mold changes have been made to Defender Optimus Prime.
Defender Optimus Prime is pretty much the antithesis of what you'd want from a redeco of a mold that's been released several times so far-- it takes the color scheme and adds in a bunch more gray, thus making it look really boring next to any other color scheme this mold has had. There's a few minor improvements-- like the shade of bronze in robot mode-- but definitely try to get ANY of the other voyager class Movie Prime toys before trying to get this one. Bleh.


Defender Optimus Prime Bio:
Since the battle in Egypt, Optimus Prime has outfitted himself with the gear he needs to take out airborne opponents. Unfortunately, Mindwipe attacks on two fronts, taking cheap shots at the Autobot from the air, while his hypnotized flunkies assault him on the ground. Optimus Prime struggles desperately, searching for a way to take out Mindwipe wihtout harming any of the innocents he's brainwashed.
