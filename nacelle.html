Nacelle (TFCC 2015 Subscription Service Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : White, dark periwinkle, moderately dark dull red, and some black, moderately dark red, metallic silver, transparent light orange, and dull orangish yellow
Rating : 9.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Classics Starscream. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Classics Starscream here .)

Nacelle is yet another use of the Classics Starscream mold, which never seems to get enough uses (and I'm not complaining-- I love this mold!). This time around it's another obscure blink-and-miss-him Seeker whose coloration who was seen very briefly in the original cartoon. The more specific paint apps for Nacelle are taken from a Japanese catalog image for G1 Thrust (obviously G1 Thrust didn't actually make it to the market in these colors). Nacelle sports a fairly patriotic color scheme, with white, red, and blue being his main colors. The blue and red are a bit different from the usual shade, however-- the blue is actually a dark periwinkle, and is my personal favorite of Nacelle's various colors. The red is a somewhat dark version of the color-- almost a "brick red", if you will, though the paint is noticeably lighter in shade than the plastic, which is slightly off-putting when they'e close together. All of the colors contrast excellently with each other, though, and I particularly like how the jet mode goes from blue to red-and-white to white at the back end. The red line paint apps on the front of the wings, sides of the fins, and the faded circular paint apps on the wings help make Nacelle stick out a bit more among his various Seeker brethren, and was a nice choice. Like pretty much all Seekers, Nacelle has some transparent light orange used on his cockpit windows, and black on his nosecone and rear thrusters. His face is mostly unpainted blue plastic, though, with a dull red face and dull yellow eyes. I like how the blue head helps differentiate him from most Seekers, but I've never been a fan of red faces, and Nacelle is no exception. His intakes and parts of his chest are painted a VERY glittery metallic silver which looks fantastic, and which has some minor dull red and yellow details on them. There's also a few other dull red and yelow details on some of his robot parts, like around the waist and lower legs, and the white plastic looks good on his lower legs arms, with red helping to break up the colors even more on his upper legs.
Nacelle has the same wings used on BotCon 2007's Thrust , but he retains Starscream's head. I like the differentiation from both Thrust and Starscream here by swapping parts, but unfortunately he has two misassemblies. His tailfins are swapped, which is kind of annoying but not a huge deal given that they're flat anyways. The slightly bigger issue is that the pieces that connect his wings to his main body-- and are used to flip his wings around during transformation-- are also backwards, so there is no curved "notch" to allow the wings to flip around. If you try to force them to, they may actually pop off altogether. Thankfully, this has a pretty simple fix-- just take an exacto knife or the like and pick off a little notch of your own on the right side of the connector piece, and everything will be fine.
TFSS Nacelle is a nice figure for an obscure Seeker who had yet to get a toy, and his color scheme is pretty good and varied up nicely-- as far as colors go, I'd put him slightly above-average for Classics Seeker squadron, with his rating getting a notch down because of his misassemblies. One should keep these in mind, and make sure in particular to fix the pieces that connect his main wings to his body before you transform him. An easy recommendation for Seeker fans; if you're sick of the Classics Starscream mold, though, he's not amazing enough to change your mind.


Nacelle Bio :
FUNCTION: Decepticon Aero Engineer
MOTTO: "Blasting you is as cathartic as streamlining one's fuselage."
Nacelle is almost as much at home on the battlefield as he was back in his old laboratory at Tesarus. He has a keen mind for the sciences, having worked alongside Bitstream and Hotlink under Starscream's Helex-Yuss Decepticon Research & Advancement team. Upon the Nemesis's departure to Earth, he was assigned as a lab assistant to Shockwave. During that time, Nacelle engineered the modified wing designs used for the second wave of Earth-bound Seekers, eventually adapting one of the prototypes into his own chassis. Due to his longing to return to his laboratory work, Nacelle can become absent-minded in the midst of battle. Nacelle's overall effectiveness, however, outweighs any liability he may cause. He can fly at speeds up to MACH 2.7 and his high-powered turbines can double his speed for up to 1 minute.
