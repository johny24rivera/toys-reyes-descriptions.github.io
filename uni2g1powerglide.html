Color Scheme : Light red, dull brownish white, and some black, transparent light blue, and dark metallic blue
Rating : 7.9

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original, mass-release Universe 2.0 Powerglide. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Universe 2.0 Powerglide here .)

Odd that the store-exclusive repaint of Powerglide is the G1-accurate one, considering that the first version was meant to obviously be the G1 character, too. Well, regardless, Powerglide is now in colors much more reminiscient of his original form, with red being the dominant color this time as opposed to white. (The white-- or, rather, a "dirty" coloration of it-- is now the secondary color, so they've essentially swapped places.) He also still has some black as a bit of accent color, used mostly on his robot parts. All in all, it looks quite good, and certainly better than the swapped color palette of his original version. However, it still doesn't look as good as it COULD-- there are some definitely large areas of red plastic that are bereft of paint apps, making him a look a little more "boring" than he otherwise would have. He has some pretty interesting paint apps-- I like the Autobot-star insignias on the wings and the metallic blue on his stomach particularly well. (More of that metallic blue really would've made the paint job snap, in my opinion.) I also really love the "NAWAYW360" on his tailfins-- it's short for "And away we go!", his catchphrase in the G1 cartoon. However, these paint apps tend to be more "concentrated" in certain areas, leading to an impression that he doesn't have many paint apps when he does, in fact, have an average amount.
No mold changes have been made to the "G1" redeco of Universe 2.0 Powerglide.
The "G1" redeco of Universe 2.0 Powerglide is a color scheme many fans of the character have been waiting for, and if you're one of those fans, but all means it's a worthy purchase. Still, it could have been done better, with the paint apps spready out more evenly so his form-- particularly in vehicle mode-- didn't look so sparse. Given that I think the Stormcloud redeco looks much better overall, though, and given this exclusive's slightly-inflated pricepoint, I'd have to recommend Stormcloud instead if you only want one version of this mold.


Powerglide Bio :
Even before the war, Powerglide took great pride in his abilities as a flyer. He would blast into the sky at top speed with little or no provocation, twisting, diving and looping in an attempt to impress anyone who might glance skyward. He continues to be proud of his skills, which can sometimes annoy the other Autobots, who he sometimes mocks for their inability to fly. Despite this, the other Autobots all recognize his skill, and appreciate the cover he offers them from high in the air.
