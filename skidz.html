Skid-Z

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Basic (comes in a Deluxe-priced 2-pack with Wind Sheer )
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Japanese Name : Indy Heat
Color Scheme : White, shiny light fuchsia, and some dull purple, black, chrome silver, silver, shiny teal, and transparent orange
Mold History : Skid-Z's mold was first used for the Machine Wars toys Prowl and Mirage back in 1997.
Rating : 6.4

Vehicle mode is an Indy race car. This mode is rather... plain-looking. It doesn't really catch the eye at all. The silver paint in the middle and the fuchsia Autobot symbol are the only things that stand out. The rest is just a rather boring amalgamation of white and purple. On the upside, though, this mode has absolutely no extras to speak of, and is pretty solid.
During Skid-Z's "flip-changer" transformation, it's important to note that the lever which initiates his transformation (the race-car back fin) "sticks" a bit to the rest of the car, and, given that the long part of the lever is very thin, it almost seems like it's going to snap when you're changing him from vehicle to robot mode. Be careful during this part!
Skid-Z's robot mode is a bit better in the color scheme department. The shiny teal really adds a lot towards making this figure look better. However, there's one problem I have with this mode, and it's a huge one (literally); Skid-Z's race-car-front chest is waaaay too long and large. It makes it look like he's all just a chest with arms and legs- he has no visible waist or anything to speak of. The wheels on the bottom sides of his legs also look rather awkward as well. At least his articulation's decent- his head can move, as well as his shoulders, elbows, knees, and upper legs. However, the upper leg movement is inhibited a bit by his very large chest. Skid-Z also has a pretty cool gun- it's stored in two halves inside his legs, so you can take one piece out to form a "short" gun, or both out to form a "long" gun. Pretty nifty. The "light piping" effect used or his eyes works very well, too.
Although he has his good points, such as articulation and his nifty weapon, Skid-Z is, overall, a rather dissapointing figure. If it's not his boring color scheme in vehicle mode, than it's his awkward chest in robot mode. There's a good chance you can find a better Transformer out there.
