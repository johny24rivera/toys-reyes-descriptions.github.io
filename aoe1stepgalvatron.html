Galvatron (1-Step Changer) [AoE]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately dark milky bluish gray, milky gray, silver, black, and some dark metallic blue and pale metallic bronze
Rating : 5.4

Galvatron's Argosy Freightliner alt mode makes it pretty decently to the 1-step changer size, with the proportions being more-or-less on-point. There are two notable exceptions here, though; for one, the rear endhas overly tall rear bumpers, an obvious side effect of them becoming the robot feet. Also, there's a big obvious black tab sticking out behind the back end of the cab portion, which is used for the transformation. The colors here are pretty much the same as on the other AoE Galvatron figures, with a dark milky bluish gray forming the main color-- and for a gray, it looks pretty decent, with a bit of milky shine to it that makes it more interesting than your average gray. There's also some black on the front bumper, front windows (but not the side windows), and wheels, and a bit of regular milky gray on the back end of the cab, which becomes much more prevalent in robot mode. The paint apps on Galvatron are pretty nice on the front part-- with the aforementioned black windows and grill, as well as silver stripes along the sides-- but the back end of this mode desperately needs some paint apps to break up all that milky bluish gray, especially with some details like a trailer hitch molded in back there. I feel that putting a big purple Decepticon symbol on the right side of the cab was a mistake, as it blends in too well with the gray and could've been used for some more paint-- say, for the side windows, or to make the silver paint a bit more extensive like it is on the voyager .
Galvatron's transformation is rather cool-- it involves pushing down on the aforementioned black tab behind the cab a little and then rotating the whole roof of the cab around, which causes the feet to split apart, the arms to flip out, the chest to flip down, and then the cab just hides behind Galvatron's back as you stand him up for robot mode. Granted, the cab in the back is a BIG extra, but it doesn't stick out too much from any angles approaching the front, and his large heels keep Galvatron from toppling over easily. My main issue with this mode is just how off the proportions are-- the legs and head are okay, but the chest is WAY too wide and the arms WAY too skinny. Galvatron needs to stop eating fast food every day, it's not good for him. As the bad guy "leader", he should look menacing, but he honestly just looks rather ridiculous in this mode with these proportions. Additionally, his chest panel which folds down in this mode doesn't QUITE sit flush with the surrounding parts, sticking out just a titch at the bottom. It's a minor downside, to be sure, but still notable. On the plus side, the mold detailing, while adequate in vehicle mode, is great in this mode, with details like the "chest grinder", curved metallic details on the hips and legs, and some details on his arms molded quite well. The headsculpt is particularly well-done, capturing his movie head pretty darned nicely, with an appropriate scowl on his silver face, with bronze eyes and a dark metallic blue spot on his forehead. The added colors also make this mode more attractive as far as the scheme goes, with the dark metallic blue on the arms and lower legs, silver on the hips and waist, and metallic bronze on the chest grinder all helping to diversify up the color scheme considerably-- which it really needs, given how much more prevalent the boring "regular" gray is in this mode, being the plastic color for his head, chest, and arms. For articulation, Galvatron can move back-and-forth at the shoulders, and that's it. I know 1-step changers weren't made for articulation, but I don't see how, given the transformation, simple knee and/or elbow articulation could have hurt.
The 1-step version of Age of Extinction Galvatron is the weakest version of him. The vehicle mode is alright, and the transformation is interesting and unique, but the robot mode-- despite the nice mold and paint detailing-- looks ridiculous with its tiny little arms and huge, wide chest. If you're looking for a simpler-to-transform version of this design, I'd go with the Power Battler version instead.

Review by Beastbot
