Scourge (Botcon 2009 Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe (comes in a 5-pack with Flak , Kup , Landshark , and Thunderclash )
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Moderately dark sky blue, silver, and some transparent dark red, white, and red
Rating : 9.5

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron Sideways. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron Sideways here .)

As far as mold homages go, Cybertron Sideways' serves fairly well for Scourge. It's rather "pointed" and "edgy" while still having a rather regal look (particularly in robot mode) what with the detailing lines and all. The alt mode is also a fair approximation, being a flat futuristic spacecraft, though no longer is he a flying soap dish-- he has actual wings now (not that I'm complaining, but it IS a bit harder to see the toy as Scourge in vehicle mode). The main blue used is a very nice shade-- a bit of a "creamy" shade, not too dark and not too light. It's definitely a "Scourge-y" color, but it's not quite as light as his G1 toy, which I think was a good decision-- it allows the silver and white to contrast against it considerably better, which I think looks great. The transparent red plastic is also gorgeous, and makes Scourge look pretty darn evil in vehicle mode where the cockpit windows are his "eyes". He's also got quite a lot of silver paint detailing everywhere so he doesn't look too plain anywhere, though I do wish there was a bit more red paint and less silver, just to mix things up a bit more. Also, though Scourge's Cyber Key gimmick still activates his arm-blades, there are no faction symbols inside that little visible square on the middle of that piece on the back of his left arm, so there aren't any new symbols that show up when you activate his gimmick or anything.
Scourge has a new head mold, which is very accurate to his G1 persona but with a smaller "forehead light", due to space constraints. That in itself I don't mind, but his head is TEENY tiny because of space limitations in vehicle mode. I understand why it was done, and it's well-detailed and painted, but it still doesn't change the fact that it's REALLY small. The Cyber Key is the same, but it's been painted into Scourge-y colors with the Decepticon symbol de-emphasized.
Botcon 2009 Scourge is a pretty cool exclusive with a nice choice for the mold. It's not perfect-- I would've preferred maybe one more color in there and, as mentioned, his remolded head is way small-- but other than that it's superbly done with great colors on an already great mold. If you have the money for Botcon exclusives and are looking at some, I'd mildly recommend this one, though I think Cybertron Sideways' colors are the best this mold's going to get.


Scourge Bio:
FUNCTION: Sweeps Commander
MOTTO: "All who defy me shall lie at my feet; broken, crushed and defeated!"
Born from the wreckage of fallen Decepticons, Scourge is a merciless warrior without fear. He is often seen following the lead of Cyclonus, but internally he is constantly calculating new ways to maneuver the Decepticon Empire towards greatness through his service. While loyal to the Decepticon cause, there is no one he will not defy if he feels there is a better way to bring destruction upon the Autobots. Scourge is a tracker by nature, using his vast array of detection equipment to find his prey. Once found, he has an arsenal of weaponry at his disposal to destroy his targets including disintegration beams, acid rays and tractor beams. Scourge leads "The Sweeps," a tactical unit comprised of battlefield specialists who serve as extensions of his will. Scourge's only weakness is his arrogance.
