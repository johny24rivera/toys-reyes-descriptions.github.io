Thundercracker ("GDO" Generations) [Toys "R" Us Exclusive]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Legion/Legend
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Dark glittery navy blue and some black, silver, light red, and metallic bronze
Rating : 7.8

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Autobot Alliance Legends "G1" Starscream. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Autobot Alliance Legends "G1" Starscream here .)

Pretty much any Starscream mold is bound to be repainted into at least one of his Seeker brethren, and for the Autobot Allliance Legends mold, that other Seeker is Thundercracker. Like nearly every other Thundercracker repaint, this one does it fairly by-the-numbers and keeps things quite accurate to his original G1 toy . His main color of dark blue is a bit darker here than on most other toys of Thundercracker, but it still has that nice semi-metallic glittery shade to it and looks quite good. Black is used on the nosecone and a few robot mode bits, but it's not nearly as prevalent here as on most other Thundercracker redecos. The light red stripes on the wings, the metallic bronze cockpit, and the painted chest and head also look quite nice and contrast well, but my main (well, only) complaint about this color scheme is that there's a bit TOO MUCH dark blue plastic. Some other bits, like some of his tailfins or weapons, being painted black really would've helped break up the colors a bit more, particularly in vehicle mode.
There haven't been any mold changes made to the core figure, except little peg holes for new weapons that were introduced to this mold with the "Dollar Store Release" of this mold as Starscream-- namely, two extra shoulder-gun accessories. Don't get me wrong, they're neat little extra weapons, but... this mold was already engineered to have the shoulder-guns form out of the middle of the jet mode. Including another two shoulder-guns seems really superfluous and honestly rather silly, though I'm not going to outright call it a knock against the figure since they ARE extra accessories that the mold didn't have before.
Generations "GDO" Thundercracker is a nice repaint of a supremely okay mold. The shades of color on this version of Thundercracker are done quite well, though a bit more paint in vehicle mode would've helped break things up a bit more. Recommended over this mold's releases as Starscream.
