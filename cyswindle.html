Swindle (Cybertron)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Scout (Basic)
Homeworld : Earth
Cyber Key Code : s4mr
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Yellow, dull grayish brown, bluish black, dark dull maroon, and some silver and red-orange
Rating : 7.4

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Hardtop. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Hardtop here .)

ybertron Swindle is a homage to G1 Swindle, in terms of his alt mode and general color scheme. Though really, even though given the name it's obvious to see what Transformer this is a homage to, it's not really obvious by itself-- for one, G1 Swindle was orange, not yellow, and second, G1 Swindle's alt mode was a jeep, not a sand rover. But regardless, the new color scheme looks fairly good on the mold, and is certainly fitting for a sand rover. Although I'm not too fond of yellow being the main color on a toy in most cases, I do have to admit Swindle's secondary colors of dull maroon, bluish black, and grayish brown all complement each other and the yellow pretty well, and there aren't any really big spots of yellow without some nice contrasting paint apps to darken things up a bit. That said, as is the case on most TFs, the bluish black really would look better on the figure as just a solid regular ol' black. Nearly all of the paint apps are also the same on Swindle as they were on Hardtop in terms of their placement-- I'd have preferred more different paint apps on a mainline repaint of a relatively recently-released mold. I do like the fact that the Decepticoon symbol was moved from the front of the vehicle mode to the chest of the robot mode, a much more appropriate place for it.
No mold changes have been made to Cybertron Swindle.
Cybertron Swindle is a decent repaint with some nice contrasting colors, but having a mostly yellow Transformer just doesn't look as good to me as a mostly dark green Transformer, which is why I still recommend Hardtop over Swindle.


Swindle Bio :
Though he is outfitted with a sensor package and weapons system nearly identical to his twin brother Hardtop, Swindle scorns long-range tactics. He'd rather charge an Autobot and club him across the visual cluster with his laser rifle than actually use it to blast another robot. Swindle is deceptively agile and tough for his size, traits developed over years of picking fights and winning. He's not as slavishly loyal to Megatron as his brother; he'll work for anyone who lets him trash Autobots.
