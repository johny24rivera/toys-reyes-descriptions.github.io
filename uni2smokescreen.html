Smokescreen (Universe 2.0)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Series: Classic/Generation One
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Light red, blue, clear plastic, black, and some white, yellow, transparent yellow, dull metallic gold, silver ,dull dark blue, and light milky gray
Rating : 8.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Universe 2.0 Deluxe Prowl. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Universe 2.0 Deluxe Prowl here .)

Smokescreen, both in terms of the overall design of the modes and the color scheme, is a new "upgraded" version of his G1 self , and so has a rally car-esque color scheme. The amounts of the colors are bit different, though-- there's a bit more white on the vehicle mode due to thicker stripes, a bit less blue, and the red is a bit lighter. In vehicle mode, the slightly-changed color scheme is pulled off rather well, though it's admittedly more loud than the other color schemes this mold has gotten (but that's always been the case). The "38" decals on the side doors and in front of the windshield are really plain, though, and look almost hand-made considering how simplistic they are. I also don't like how the rear windows are painted a dull blue-- if you're gonna paint some of the windows but make the others transparent, at least make all of them the same color! In robot mode, Smokescreen shares a few minor color changes from his G1 self as well-- namely, less black and more of the red/blue colors. It doesn't look BAD, but still a bit "too" colorful. The original G1 toy had some nice darker red and more black in robot mode to offset the lighter colors, and this release of Smokescreen doesn't, which makes him look borderline garish in robot mode. (One final note-- the red painted parts don't contrast with the red plastic parts at all, it just appears that way in the above pictures due to camera lighting.)
Smokescreen has the same mold change that Universe 2.0 Silverstreak does-- that is, the removal of Prowl's headlights-- but otherwise no mold changes have been made to the toy.
Universe 2.0 Smokescreen is a nice G1 homage and the mold certainly isn't bad, but two minor changes to the classic color scheme-- namely, the lightening up of the red and the removal of some of the black in robot mode-- make him look lighter and a bit TOO colorful overall, and as such he's my least recommended of the three paint jobs this mold has gotten. Go with Silverstreak instead.


Smokescreen Bio :
This cunning robot's job is to distract, disrupt and confuse the enemy. He is a master of misdirection, skilled at making Decepticons look in exactly the wrong direction at just the perfect time. More than one Autobot sneak attack owes its success to the skill of Smokescreen. He enjoys playing games with the enemy, taking a huge amount of pleasure in the frustration they display.
