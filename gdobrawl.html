Decepticon Brawl ("GDO" Generations) [Toys "R" Us Exclusive]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Moderately dark military green, dull purplish brown, and some black, silver, white, brick red, and metallic gold
Rating : 9.1

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Autobot Alliance Breacher. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Autobot Alliance Breacher here .)

An amphibious assault vehicle gets redecoed as... Brawl? The tank Combaticon whose most noteworthy bit is being part of a combiner? Well, there's a turret, so I suppose it's not completely off-the-mark... Brawl's main color is-- as it usually is-- green, in this case a dull military green that's pretty much exactly the right shade for a tank. He's also got a bit of black on his wheels and double-barreled gun/turret, but in vehicle mode he largely is bereft of paint apps (particularly on the rear two-thirds of the mode), making him look pretty boring. They really should've taken advantage of all the mold detailing on this toy and given him a few more paint apps here. (On a side note, the "D67"s on the sides of this mode call back to Brawl's G1 Japanese release number, D-67.)In robot mode, colors are mixed up considerably more, with a rather interesting shade of purplish brown forming most of his "robot bits". It's a nice color, but something that was a bit brighter would've contrasted with all of his dark green (though yes, I realize that wouldn't have been G1-accurate... but a lot about this toy isn't, anyways). He does have a fair amount of gold and silver paint apps visible in this mode as well, though, and where they're placed, at least they provide a bit more contrast/visual interest here. (He definitely still needs some more paint on his arms, though.) I find it rather distracting that Brawl wasn't remolded with a more G1-esque head with a faceplate. By itself, the brick red mouth and gold detailing on his purplish brown, two-eyed face looks pretty nice, but it's not Brawl, not by a long shot.
No mold changes have been made to GDO Brawl.
"GDO" Brawl is a waste of an honestly great Scout mold. There were no shortage of Brawl toys that LOOKED like Brawl around the time this release came out, and neither the vehicle mode nor the robot mode (especially the face) bring Brawl to mind. He also (obviously) doesn't combine, kind of a major deal for the character. His color scheme has a decent amount of contrast in robot mode, but in vehicle mode he also looks pretty dull. If you want this mold, get Breacher instead. If you want a Brawl, there's plenty of other Generations options for you to consider over this one. Despite my high rating (because of the mold) I'd only recommend this for people who absolutely cannot get a Breacher for a reasonable price.
