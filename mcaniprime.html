Optimus Prime (Animated; McDonald's)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Happy Meal Size
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark blue, dull red, and some black, yellow, dull faint blackish blue, light sky blue, and chalky bluish off-white
Rating : 4.0

Optimus Prime's Animated vehicle mode comes out looking fair for a Happy Meal toy, all things considered. The proportions are fairly good, though the wheels poke out a bit too much from the sides and look a little doofy, especially since they aren't painted black and don't actually roll (there's much smaller wheels on the underside that accomplish that). That side, though, Prime has a pretty good amount of paint for a Happy Meal toy-- all the necessary details on the front, like the windows and headlights and grill, are all painted up, which are all the major features. You could always use more paint, such as the side windows or rooflights, but those are minor nitpicks when you consider the price of this thing. Other than a few odd proportional things like an overly bulky rear end, there are no real robot extras EXCEPT for the robot fists visible on the back end of the cab.
Surprisingly, Optimus Prime's robot mode isn't all that bad either, (all together now) for a Happy Meal toy, and is in fact overall a little better than the alt mode. The proportions are still more or less pretty good, the only real exception here being his arms being overly blocky and hollow on the top half nad a bit too small on the bottom half due to the transformation. I wish the legs were one big uni-leg, but they're still pretty proportionally solid, and the way the "robo-boots" slide out of the legs to give him a more solid stance works quite well. I do not like his head sculpt, though-- though it has a pretty good amount of paint on it, it doesn't have the Animated style. It's sort of like a normal "serious" style but with a chin that even looks a bit big for Animated. It's just off. Prime's articulation also is pretty bad even for a Happy Meal toy-- he can move forwards at the elbows and rotate his shoulders backward a bit, but that's it.
Happy Meal Animated Prime is the only Animated McDonald's toy that doesn't horribly suffer in both modes. Sure, he's got problems, like a severe lack of articulation-- he IS a low-budget Happy Meal toy, after all-- but his proportions are pretty solid in both modes, and he isn't severely lacking in paint apps either like some of his Happy Meal Animated kin. My personal favorite of the McAnimated toys.
