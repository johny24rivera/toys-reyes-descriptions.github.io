Oil Pan

Allegiance: Decepticon
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Moderately light orange, black, dark pale bluish purple, and some white, dark metallic gunmetal gray, moderately light red, and silver
Rating : 7.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen Brakedown. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen Brakedown here .)

Oil Pan's a new-character redeco, and features a primarily orange paint scheme, with a dull bluish purple color serving as the main accent color in robot mode. It's certainly a rather unique scheme, and the colors complement each other well, with neither being too loud-- though on that subject, I wish the bluish purple wasn't QUITE so pale, so as to provide just a tad more contrast. The black-- used mostly for the wheels, and windows-- serves as a decent neutral color, particularly in vehicle mode to keep the whole theme from getting too "light"-looking. The white stripes in vehicle mode help Oil Pan from looking too one-tone in vehicle mode as well, and given the exact tones of the orange and white, ends up reminding me a bit of a creamsicle in that mode. The white also works marvelously on the few places it's used on the robot mode, like the chest and the head-- particularly the latter, as, combined with the red eyes, the mold detailing, and the darker head, makes Oil Pan's face look a bit like a evil helmeted skeleton (certainly appropriate for a Movieverse Decepticon). The other bits of painted color-- the silver on the headlights and hubcaps, the red on the taillights, and the gunmetal gray near the rear of the vehicle mode-- serve as decent colors to help break up all the orange, but color-wise I don't think they add anything to the deco job that the black and/or white didn't already.
No mold changes have been made to Oil Pan.
Oil Pan is a fairly nice, unique paint scheme on a mold that's sadly medicore at best. Given that he's not in any fiction and doesn't have any real characterization, I imagine most people won't find quite the draw to him compared to RotF Brakedown, but compared to that initial iteration of this mold, I DO think this one's color scheme is slightly better.


Oil Pan Bio :
Oil Pan is used to running, and he's become quite good at it over the years. He's the ultimate defensive driver-- using traffic as a shield against Autobot laser fire, and disappearing down exit ramps before his pursuers even realize where he's gone. The pair of Autobots chasing him may think he's trapped, but he'll be away and free before they know it.
