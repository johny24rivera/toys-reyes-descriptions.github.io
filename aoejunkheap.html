Junkheap (Power Battler) [AoE]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : White, dark forest green, light milky gray, and some yellow, silver, and black
Rating : 7.7

Junkheap's alternate mode is a Waste Management trash/recycling truck, though in the movie THREE Junkheaps were formed from ONE truck-- but especially for the Power Battler line, that's not really doable, and the guy wasn't in the movie nearly long enough to justify a large toy of him that would be able to pull it off, so this option's fine for me. The proportions in this mode are generally solid, as you'd expect from a toy with a licensed alt mode. The thing is, there are some VERY obvious robot extras in this mode-- the worse offenders being the robot arms, which not-so-convincingly make up the roof of the front part of this mode. Although not QUITE as obvious, but still pretty high on the blatant-o-meter, is the robot head, which is upside down facing forward right behind said arms. From a side view or a mostly top-down view it's not obvious, but from a view anywhere near the front? It's just right THERE, man. Couldn't it have faced the other direction and rotated at the neck during transformation? Ugh. On a much lesser note, a bit of the robot upper legs stick out of the bottom, but it's only a little. Same with a couple of robot details on the back end-- just barely worth mentioning, given the aim of the Power Battler line. The mold detailing in this mode is pretty good, with not just the necessary details like the windows and wheels molded in (though the middle wheel on each side is fake; only the front and back wheels actually roll), but little details like ladders on the sides, hydraulics, and the like molded into the sides. The color scheme is exactly what you'd expect for a Waste Management truck-- white and dark green, with a little yellow for good measure. The white and dark green contrast against each other quite well, and obviously the yellow contrasts and complements the green well on the back end. There's some black where necessary, like on the wheels, though some of the robot parts (more obvious in robot mode) are that really blah light milky gray that I really wish would just disappear from TFs entirely in favor of a darker, more metallic version of the plastic color. The paint detailing is mostly limited to the cab and the yellow on the back end. The front windows and grill are silver, along with the front bumper being painted black, but sadly the side windows and headlights are unpainted, as are details on the side like the ladders. There is a nice "Waste Management" logo on each side, though. Junkheap can activate his Power Battler gimmick in either mode-- push down on the white button on the top of this mode, and the back end will open up and lunge out a considerable amount, in just the sort of "claw crush" action advertised on the packaging. It's a pretty simple, but cool gimmick, like a sudden lunge at a soon-to-be meal. I love it.
The transformation to Junkheap's robot mode is pretty simple, with the front and bottom sections swinging down to become the legs, while the arms and head flip over and clip onto the back end, making the back end now the chest (and making the action feature a literal "get in my belly" feature. Again, I love it). Other than the obvious cab halves on the feet, the vehicle parts meld into Junkheap's overall silhouette very well in this mode. He's a little "deeper" than you'd expect if you look at him from a side angle, but not obnoxiously so. His arms are just a LITTLE skinny, but otherwise his proportions are pretty good in this mode. His mold detailing is insanely intricate in this mode, and very accurate to his movie model, with little mechanical hinges, pistons, and greebles all over the place on the arms, legs, chest, and head. Unfortunately not many of his details are actually painted-- he has the silver paint on his chest, and the silver bits on his mouth vent and forehead, along with some yellow on his eyes (?-- it's difficult to tell from the intricacy of the detailing), but his arms are void of paint, as are his legs on any part that's readily visible in this mode, beyond some green on the sides used for vehicle mode. That said, his colors are broken up more in this mode, so it's not as obvious as in his vehicular form. Articulation is always one of the weaknesses of the Power Battler line, and although Junkheap isn't up to the standard of a "mainline Generations" figure, he can move at the shoulders (at two points), knees, and SLIGHT movement at the ankle tilts. It's not a lot, but it's better than most in the line, and given the transformation he couldn't really move at the hips anyways.
Junkheap definitely has a few downsides-- like his very obvious head and arms in vehicle mode, a few too few paint apps, and odd boxy cab-halves for feet in robot mode, but other than that he's a pretty solid entry in the Power Battler line with a really fun gimmick, which is fortunate given that there's no other toy of him. As long as you can overlook the obvious arms and relatively little articulation, I'd get this toy even if you normally skip this subline for the "mainline" movie toys-- it's just a fun toy of a little-known Decepticon in a movie that had very few unique designs for them.

Review by Beastbot
