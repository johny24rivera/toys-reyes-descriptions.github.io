Size : Scout

Rating : 9.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to High Score 100. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of High Score 100 here .)

OMG! MASTER CHIEF! Although not officially confirmed by Hasbro (and I doubt it ever will be, given the legal issues with that), Twitcher F451 is most definitely an homage to the character Master Chief from the Halo video game series. The color scheme is practically identical, and the head sculpt, while certainly not close enough to provoke a lawsuit by Microsoft, does have some "Master Chief-ish" qualities to it, such as the visor and faceplate design. Twitcher is also an homage to another Xbox 360 product, namely the "Halo 3" special edition Xbox 360 controller itself, which is very similar in colors to Twitcher here. Homages aside, though, the color scheme itself measures up very well. You wouldn't think that three different shades of green would make for an interesting color scheme, but they surprisingly do. I think it's the light green, which provides most of the contrast, that gives the colors the zing. The black also helps a lot in terms of providing a really dark color, as well. And the gold accents in robot mode look excellent against the green, and really help to diversify the colors a bit more.
No mold changes have been made to Twitcher F451.
Twitcher F451 is not only a nice homage to Halo and Master Chief, but a great toy by itself with a very interesting, eye-catching color scheme that, to me at least, is head-and-shoulders above High Score's. My most recommended out of all the Real Gear toys.


Twitcher F451 Bio :
"Pressing Your Buttons"
Twitcher F451 is a psychological profiler, specializing in anything that makes the Autobots uncomfortable, angry, or afraid. With only a few minutes of observation, he can draw highly specific conclusions about almost any target's greatest mental weaknesses, and come up with a way to exploit them. He takes a great deal of pleasure in making other robots uncomfortable, so even other Decepticons tend to avoid him.
