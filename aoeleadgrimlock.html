Grimlock (Leader) [AoE]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Leader
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Hard
Color Scheme : Chrome silver, black, moderately dark bronzish brown, and some light sky blue, light semi-metallic gray, chrome copper, metallic dull brownish bronze, and metallic copper
Rating : 7.4

Grimlock's beast mode of a t-rex made of armor and knives is replicated... pretty poorly for his leader class toy, sadly. There's certainly some upsides to it, so let's cover those first; the headsculpt is VERY nicely done with the horns more pronounced on this version (and made of rubbery plastic for safety reasons). The legs also look pretty nice proportionally, and the only obvious robot extras in this mode are the robot feet and hands, the former of which are just... there... in front of the upper legs, though they do fit Grimlock's overall silhouette pretty well. The latter are clearly visible as "heels" holding the t-rex feet in a stable position, but they're pretty small overall and generally easily overlooked. The mold detailing for this toy is also incredibly well done. There's armor and spike-like details EVERYWHERE, from the the "spine" down the upper ridged back to little mechanical bits visible on the sides of his legs to a really nice ridged pattern on his lower back and tail. The many teeth in Grimlock's maw also look great, and are, again, made of the rubbery kind of plastic. He's even got rubbery "rib cage" spikes on his underside, though the front ones can bend slightly over time. Unfortunately, Grimlock's overall proportions in this mode are just horrendous. His body is FAR too long and a bit too flat, while his tail isn't long enough. His t-rex arms are also too short and small, even for t-rex arms. The middle of his chest has a big gap in it, which looks off from an underside angle. It all just looks very odd. He's got a TON of chrome visible in this mode-- the front half of the back being mostly chrome silver, while the back half of the back suddenly becoming chrome copper, which tapers down to copper paint on the tail. The chrome copper might've looked okay used in a lesser amount, but why all the chrome silver? It looks gaudy in the amount it's used, and unlike with Leader Optimus Prime , Grimlock's chrome tends to chip easier, probably because of all the details on those pieces and how certain chrome parts peg into other chrome parts for transformation. The rest of his color scheme in this mode is mostly metallic copper or black-- both nice colors that I wish had been more prevalent over the whole toy. The sky blue eyes do look nice as well, even if they're movie inaccurate and should be yellow. For articulation in this mode, Grimlock can move at the jaw, up-and-down at the neck, shoulders, elbows, hips (at two points, one of them ratcheting), knees (at two points, again, one ratcheted), and slightly forward-and-back at the ankles. I wish his tail could move a bit, but all things considered it's pretty decent articulation for a t-rex alt mode.
Grimlock's transformation is quite complex-- in fact, he's probably the most complex mold in the entire AoE line. Getting him to robot isn't that difficult, but getting everything lined up for beast mode can be quite perplexing, especially the first few times-- particularly when it comes to how to position his legs, given how they basically turn themselves inside out and peg into several places almost simultaneously. Fair warning. That said, Grimlock's robot mode is a MASSIVE improvement over his beast mode-- granted, that isn't hard, but his robot mode is pretty darned good. The best part is the proportions-- his legs are just a touch two-dimensional in that they're a bit skinny from viewed from a head-on angle, but otherwise his proportions are pretty perfect. There is a little bit of kibble in this mode, but the only bits that bother me are the dino feet on the back of the lower robot arms-- they're kind of large bits, there. The upper tail section folds behind Grimlock's back, up and out of the way, and doesn't get in the way of movement. Every other t-rex part goes well with this mode; I love the further folding-up of the t-rex head-halves on Grimlock's shoulders, still emphasizing the teeth but not making them too large like on most of the Grimlock head-halves on other toys for him in this line. The way little rubbery bits of his "dino teeth" go from his chest to peg into his shoulders seemlessly is also a great touch. The mold detailing for his robot parts isn't quite as intricate as his beast mode detailing, but it's still done very well-- particularly the armor plating-like detailing on the chest and the very nicely detailed headsculpt, complete with a nice shiny metallic copper head, (inaccurate) sky blue eyes, and a black face with a gray headcrest-- it all makes him look incredibly fearsome. Also, the usage of Grimlock's chrome on his chest is the sole use of that on this toy that I don't consider gaudy-- it makes it look more like he's wearing armor, as befits a "knight". Grimlock also comes with a pretty cool mace weapon that is formed from his tail, with sections of the tail sliding down the shaft and folding around to make for a pretty nice spike-weapon-- and, unlike his voyager class version, he can actually hold the weapon by the shaft in this mode, as it's supposed to be held! He's also got a shield which can slot into the side of one of his lower arms, though it's a bit small overall and the odd (rubbery) spikes on the front and bottom look weird-- it's all too obviously a section of his beast mode. For articulation, in this mode Grimlock can move at the neck, shoulders (at two points-- one of them ratcheting), elbows (at two points-- one of them ratched), rotation at the elbows, hips (at three points, two of them ratchets), knees (at one point, ratcheted), and ankles (at two points). He's pretty well-balanced, and I wish they would have included some waist movement on the toy given it's a rather expensive Leader-class figure, but other than that he's quite poseable in this mode.
AoE Leader Grimlock is a mixed bag. His robot mode is absolutely amazing in nearly every respect, with great proportions, almost no annoying beast mode kibble, excellent articulation, and some great sculpting with a neat-o mace weapon-- my favorite robot mode of this design, definitely. However, the t-rex just has really bad, awkward proportions that are unacceptable for a toy this large, and looks worse than the voyager toy (which already doesn't look too great in beast mode to begin with). The chrome is also used to an obnoxious extent, not really fitting anywhere except on the robot chest. Thus, despite the slightly better robot mode, I'd recommend the voyager version of this design more-- the beast mode of said voyager is better than the leader's by a larger margin than this version's robot mode is better than the voyager's, and this one costs over twice as much.


Grimlock (Leader) Bio :
He has battled countless enemies across the universe, never concerning himself with trophies, only victories. He has proven difficult to capture and nearly impossible to defeat. When an enemy does manage to get the best of him, it's never for long. Un-caged and unleashed, Grimlock is concerned with only one thing: vengeance against those who would try to control him.
