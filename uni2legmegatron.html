Megatron (Universe 2.0; Legend)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Legend
Series : Classic/Generation Two
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark military green, dark dull gray, dull purple, and some silver and red
Rating : 7.9

Megatron's vehicle mode is a tank, and is an almost direct copy of his Generation 2 toy, despite his packaging having the usual "Classics Series" moniker on it. The fruity purple-on-green camo scheme, though not realistic, is typical of Generation Two, so I suppose it's appropriate for this homage. The added dark gray in robot mode helps to ground the colors a little more in reality, at any rate. The mold detailing on the toy is fantastic, with little mechanical plating and other relevant detailing everywhere. The proportions for a tank are also pretty spot-on. The camo scheme is also very crisp and well-done. Unfortunately, due to the transformation and the inherent limitations of the size class, the turret can't rotate and the cannon can't move up or down in this mode. That's not an insignificant downer, though it's the only downside of this mode overall.
Megatron's robot mode is also highly reminscient of his G2 version, with the same overall proportions and the same cannon mounted on the right side of his shoulders (though it's actually attached to the right side of his head on this version due to his transformation). The chest is a bit broad, and his lower legs rather large compared to his upper legs, but this is to be expected because of the transformation and the homage. The head sculpt is extremely well-done for the size, being quintessentially Megatron and with very crisp details, both when it comes to the mold and the paint. About the only thing I don't like about this mode is that the arms have no discernable elbows, are rather oddly bulky in the wrong areas, as they're halves of the turret from the tank mode. As for articulation, Megatron is above-average for a Legends class figure-- he can move at the shoulders, hips, knees, and neck, so you can get a fair number of poses out of him, and he's not particularly back-heavy, so no problems there.
Universe 2.0 Legends Megatron is both a great homage to the G2 era-- an era seldom homaged-- and just a great toy in and of itself. The camo scheme is a bit funky, but if you can get past that I'd recommend him even if you don't normally pick up Legends toys. One of the best of that size class.
