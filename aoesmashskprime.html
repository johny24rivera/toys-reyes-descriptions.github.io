Optimus Prime (Silver Knight; Smash 'n Change) [AoE] {Target Exclusive}

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme: Light milky gray, red, and some dark metallic blue, silver, dark metallic gunmetal gray, light metallic silvery blue, and black
Rating : 6.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original AoE Smash 'n Change Optimus Prime toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original AoE Smash 'n Change Optimus Prime toy here .)

Following the other "Silver Knight" Optimus Prime toys that are Target exclusives, this version of Optimus forgoes his traditional color scheme for a largely gray one, with more extensive (and more detailed) flame paint apps in vehicle mode than on the original version of the mold, but without any of the "inverse flames" (i.e., on the original, blue "flames" on the plastic cutting into the red paint apps). In vehicle mode, this toy is HORRIBLY boring. I mean, yeah, there's those nice flame paint apps on the front and black windows and wheels, but otherwise he's all light milky gray. ALLLL light milky gray. No grill paint apps, or bumper paint apps, or paint on that nifty shield-like detailing he's got on the "handle" on the rear of this mode-- nothing. Most of the other "Silver Knight" Optimus Prime toys had chrome or a bunch of silver paint or something, but not this one. Thankfully, in robot mode his colors are a bit more diverse-- he's got some dark blue on his lower legs and hips (including some blue "flames" on his knees), a few minor red flame paint apps on his chest, and a rather nice shade of dark metallic gunmetal gray on his chest and lower arms. The parts of his chest that aren't painted other colors are painted silver, as is his head-- with some nice silvery blue eyes. That said, even with this paint, there's no reason why most of his arms, waist, and legs couldn't have been painted silver to make him all shiny.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Optimus Prime.
"Smash 'n Change" Silver Knight Optimus Prime is largely un-needed. If you want a more simplistic version of Silver Knight OP, go for the 1-step changer with more varied colors; if you want one that actually looks shiny, go for the First Edition version or the one that comes in a 2-pack with Grimlock. This version does SOME things right-- like the dark blue paint apps on the legs and silver/gunmetal chest in robot mode-- but he's got way, waaaay too much boring light milky gray plastic (particularly in vehicle mode) to be much beyond blah (and it's already a weak mold anyways).

Review by Beastbot
