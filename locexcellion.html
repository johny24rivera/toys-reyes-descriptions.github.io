Hot Shot (Excellion Redeco; Legends of Cybertron)

Allegiance: Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Fiery red, and some black, flat light orange, dull metallic gold, metallic silvery blue, and silver
Rating : 5.8

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Legends of Cybertron Hot Shot. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Legends of Cybertron Hot Shot here .)

(For a review on the larger Excellion toy that this is based on, check out the review here .)

For some odd reason, even though this Hot Shot redeco is clearly the Excellion redeco of the mold from the main Cybertron line, it's still called Hot Shot. Huh. Well, that aside, Excellion was an awesome redeco of Hot Shot, and this is almost as great of a redeco. The gold paint flames on the front go very, very nice with the red, and the orange color, which is more visible in robot mode, also goes great with all the other colors. Heck, all of the colors go very well with each other, so no complaints whatsoever when it comes to this repaint. There are a few details on the larger Excellion toy that are noticeably absent from this toy, however-- namely the gold flames on the rear part of the vehicle mode and the silver paint apps on the robot shoulders. There's also a couple of minor color inconsistencies between the two figures, as this toy's rear wings are orange while the larger Excellion's are transparent blue. Neither really looks better than the other, though, so that's not really any big deal. The black paint deco pattern on this toy's chest also doesn't match the larger Excellion's at all, though personally I think just a solid black looks better on the chest than the characoal gray of the larger Excellion.
No mold changes have been made to LoC Hot Shot/Excellion.
The LoC Hot Shot "Excellion" redeco is an improvement over the original LoC toy in every way when it comes to the color scheme, just like the larger Excellion when compared to the larger Hot Shot. It's too bad that the mold design didn't translate too well to LoC size, otherwise I'd wholeheartedly recommend it.
