Starscream (G1 Reissue, Toys "R" Us Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Price : $35 (U.S.)
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Difficulty of Sticker Applications : Hard
Color Scheme : Light gray, dark flat blue, light red, chrome silver, and some black and transparent black
Rating : 5.4

Vehicle mode is a jet. This mode is very nice, practically of model quality. The opening transparent cockpit is a nice feature, as is the rubber nose. The detailing is sparse, but this is common for G1 toys. Starscream has a landing gear in this mode, but it's not retractable at all, and is in fact a seperate removable piece- and one that you have to remove to transform into robot mode, on top of that. So I'm not too fond of this feature. This toy is different from the "classic" G1 Starscream toy in that the missiles are elongated to the legal minimum length. So now, they poke out of their launchers more than an inch. I can certainly see why Hasbro did this, but that doesn't make it look any better... Another change made is that the pegs that hold the main wings to the body are longer, making a gap about a centimeter across lying between the wings and the actual body of the jet. This is probably my biggest gripe with this mode, since with this added gap, the toy really doesn't look very solid anymore. The gray, red, and dark blue color scheme is nice, but the color schemes of Starscream's two other buddies, Thundercracker and Skywarp , are better.
Transformation to robot mode is probably my biggest problem with this toy. It involves little more than detaching and reattaching a bunch of parts. Not much of a "transformation".
The robot mode itself isn't really that great, even by G1 standards. The wings just hang off Starcream's back at places that really wouldn't be plausible if he could move at the places a human does. The huge jet cone hanging off the back of his head looks ridiculous as well. In addition, the only articulation Starscream has is on his arms at the shoulders. That's it- substandard, even for a toy this old. In fact, there's really not much I can say that's positive about this mode, other than the arm-mounted missile launchers.
For such a famous transformer, Starscream's toy sure does bite. The vehicle mode is excellent, but the robot mode is sub-par for a G1 toy. Couple this with the whopping $35 price- even though he's only about the size of a deluxe- and he's not at the top of my list. And it'll probably be the same with you, too. Not recommended unless you reeeally liked the Starscream character or are a completist.


Starscream Bio :
FUNCTION: Air Commander
MOTTO: "Conquest is made of the ashes of one's enemies."
Seeks to replace Megatron as leader. Ruthless, cold-blooded, cruel... considers himself the most sophisticated and handsome of Decepticons. Believes Decepticons should rely more on guile and speed rather than brute force to defeat Autobots. Fastest flyer of group, can reach Mach 2.8 and an altitude of 52 miles. Shoots cluster bombs and null-rays which disrupts the flow of electricity. Very good at what he does, but sometimes overrates himself.
