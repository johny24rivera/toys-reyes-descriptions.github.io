Autobot Hound (1-Step Changer; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark military green, dull grayish brown, and some black, silver, light sky blue, metallic gunmetal gray, and metallic bronze
Rating : 7.0

Hound's military transport is replicated fairly accurately here, with one major section-- the back section-- just... rather abruptly ending a bit before it actually should, with the robot hands and feet VERY obviously sticking out the back end. I'm not sure why they couldn't have just extended the back top section a bit more, over the hands-- I would've gladly traded a bit of kibble on the back of the hands for an alt mode that looked much more solid simply with that one change. From a direct-on side view, you can see the upper legs below the main bulk of the vehicle, but that's not nearly as big of a deal, especially when you consider the whole "one-step changer" gimmick. The colors in this mode are somewhat accurate, at least in terms of the plastic, with most being a dark military green, accented by some dull grayish brown. Not exactly the most eye-catching color scheme, but it's realistic. The tires, are, appropriately, black, but nothing else is in this mode. What's rather disappointing is the severe lack of paint apps in this mode. The front windows are pained silver, along with some small side bars, and the exhaust pipe is painted a rather nice shade of metallic gunmetal gray. The OSHKOSH symbol is also present on the front of the truck, teeny-tiny as it is. The rest of this mode is unpainted, which is a real shame-- the top of this mode may not have much mold detailing, but the sides (especially at the front section) certainly do, with details like grated windows, headlights, and various bars on the front just begging for some paint.
Hound transforms in almost exactly the same way as 1-step changer Optimus Prime does (which partially explains the weak back end to the vehicle mode)-- pressing in on the round section at the top of the vehicle mode and unlocking the legs allows him to unfold into his robot mode. Said robot mode looks pretty good, particularly when compared to the vehicle mode. The proportions are only a little off-- the legs are a bit skinny, and Hound's a bit barrel-chested without any sort of gut-- but largely easily overlooked for a one-step changer. There is some kibble-- the halves of his roof behind his shoulders, and the front of the vehicle behind his main body, but both of these stay out of the way and only the former really affects the silhouette of the robot mode at all, and even then in only a minor manner. The mold detailing on Hound's robot parts is exquisite. From all the little armor plating and pipe details on his body (and even his dog tags!) to his wheel-feet to it looking like his arms themselves are made of out of ammo belts and missiles to a face sculpt is that is pretty spot-on (slightly shortened beard aside), it all looks pretty impressive, particularly for the size. The paint is, thankfully, applied a bit more liberally here, with the metallic gunmetal gray on the lower arms and metallic bronze on the upper arms coming off particularly nicely against the dark green. The wheel-feet are appropriately painted black, and Hound has a silver face, black beard, and the usual Autobot-blue optics. That said-- particularly given all the mold detailing there-- his chest could still definitely use some paint detailing, and is the one big oversight regarding this aspect of the robot mode. For articulation, Hound can move at the neck and back-and-forth at the shoulders, but that's it. Not impressive, but then again, articulation isn't the point of these figures, given the aimed age range. Some elbow articulation couldn't have hurt even given his transformation, though.
Back end of the vehicle mode aside, Hound looks pretty nice for a one-step changer, with a largely proportional (though vastly underpainted) vehicle mode and a remarkably solid-looking, well-done robot mode with relatively minimal kibble. Again, he doesn't have much articulation, but that's the case with the rest of the toys in this subline. Still, despite being rather good for the subline, this is my least-recommended mold if you want an AoE Hound, given how good the voyager class and Power Battler figures of him are.

Review by Beastbot
