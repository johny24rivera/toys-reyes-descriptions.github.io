Fire Blast Optimus Prime (Fast Action Battler)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dull gray, light orange, and some dull greyish silver, metallic silvery blue, light sky blue, yellow, dull yellow, pale light grayish blue, dull orange, transparent yellow, metallic teal, dull red, and black
Rating : 8.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Power Hook Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Power Hook Optimus Prime here .)

This repaint of Prime is most definitely a Rodimus homage, particularly in truck mode. The yellow flames, combined with the light orange and dark red, look great together as they always have. They also make the toy look considerably less dull than its original paint job, which consisted mostly of dark colors. Oh, and hey, his truck mode's windows are actually painted this time, like they should have been the first time around. The gray, which is more apparent in robot mode, looks horribly out of place with the otherwise vibrant color scheme, and is the only part of Fire Blast Prime's colors that I don't like. The light and metallic blue looks really nice, and helps establish the toy as still definitely "Prime" by having both red and blue on it. I particularly like the metallic blue paint on his head, that combined with the other details makes it look REALLY nice and shiny. The orange flames on the red chest aren't nearly as obvious as they should be, as both are dull colors and blend together too well for the flames to really stand out, unfortunately.
No mold changes have been made to Fire Blast Optimus Prime.
Fire Blast Optimus Prime is an excellent repaint of the FAB Prime mold, superior in almost any way and making the mold more unique from the other versions of Movie Prime at the same time. The dull grey really doesn't fit the other colors at all, but other than that it's a nice color scheme and a cool Rodimus homage to boot. Recommended.
