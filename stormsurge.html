Color Scheme : Black, dark blue, white, gray, and some silver, electric light blue, and reddish orange
Rating : 7.4

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron Shortround. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron Shortround here .)

S7 apparently has power on the sea as well, since Storm Surge here is a hovercraft with Sector 7 logos on its sides. The color scheme shares a really nice mix of "realistic" colors and having an aquatic theme. The black and gray colors by themselves would look pretty dull, but having such a nice contrast with the lighter white makes Storm Surge look considerably more interesting. Dark blue makes a great other major color as well, and is probably my favorite color shade on this toy. The "Allspark Power" electric light blue accents-- primarily on the robot chest-- look great against the surrounding colors, as well. To offset all the cool colors, though, Storm Surge has some nice reddish orange accents on his turbines, gun, and torpedo tips, which also serve as great contrasting colors, yet still retain a bit of a "naval rescue vehicle" tone in their particular shade.
No mold changes have been made to Storm Surge.
Honestly, I have no complaints with Storm Surge's color scheme whatsoever. The mold is purely mediocre, but the colors chosen for Storm Surge match his vehicle mode perfectly, he's got tons of paint apps (except for on his arms, which are rather bare), and everything contrasts wonderfully. He outshines Cybertron Shortround in every way in terms of his colors.


Storm Surge Bio :
Storm Surge is so named because of his all-encompassing approach to combat. Allies and opponents alike rarely leave a fight in which he is involved without being soaked in corrosive salt acid, or bathed in jellied gasoline. During fights at sea, the water around him erupts constantly into geysers as his depth charges roll indiscriminately into the ocean.
