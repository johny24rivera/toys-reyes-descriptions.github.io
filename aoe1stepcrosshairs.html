Crosshairs (1-Step Changer; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Black, moderately light forest green, and some silver and light sky blue
Rating : 6.9

Crosshairs' Chevrolet Corvette Stingray alt mode looks pretty good for its one-step changer version. Proportionally, it's pretty spot-on-- I really don't have any complaints whatsoever about him in this respect. There are SOME robot extras, but none of them are that big-- the front of the robot feet are a bit obvious on the back bumper. Additionally-- because of his transformation-- the "seam lines" in Crosshairs' alt mode are a bit more obvious. Not enough clips together securely enough, so some bits-- particularly the halves of the back third or so-- can wiggle around a bit in this mode, and the lines in between the sections of his front hood are also a bit wider than I'd like because of this issue. As far as the colors go, this version is pretty close to Crosshairs' Power Battler version , with the green being a moderately light shade of forest green, which contrasts against the black very well. The black stripes on both sides also look really nice, though the inverse "green stripes on black" on the back side is missing on this version (the grill also isn't painted, either). In fact, there's a bit TOO much black on Crosshairs-- there's no taillights painted, and having his windows painted black when they're also partially surrounding by black parts sort of makes them blend in too much. (The side windows are molded open, so at least there's no issue there-- there's no detailed interior, just a flat green plank o' plastic inside. So, not as good as a detailed interior, but better than obvious robot bits inside.) I wish they had been painted the windows silver or silvery-blue like on the Power Battler; as it is, only the headlights are silver in this mode, so this mode could've had a bit more color variation. I'll also say that the Autobot symbol on the roof is juust big enough to be a bit obnoxious (to say nothing of how it's inaccurate to the movie).
Crosshairs' transformation and design is identical to 1-step Lockdown's , so many of my comments about the specifics of the robot mode are shared between these two toys. The transformation is activated in the same manner-- by pressing in on a button on the windshield to cause Crosshairs to "split apart" a bit into his robot mode, with you just pushing the roof piece into Crosshairs' back to get him back to vehicle mode. Unfortunately, the button often gets stuck and then you have to gently pull apart the parts yourself, which is definitely a big downside given that the whole point of these toys is that even a little kid can transform 'em. The proportions for Crosshairs are generally good for everything except the arms, which are a bit skinny. The vehicle kibble on the robot mode isn't really movie-accurate at all (there's only a tiny "cloak"-detailed piece behind his upper legs), but hey, you gotta make some sacrifices for a one-step changer gimmick. He's got halves of his car sides and front grill hanging off his arms and shoulders, but they enhance his silhouette a bit even if they're not movie-accurate, so I don't mind them. The car roof on his back does stick out a bit, but it doesn't look TOO bad, and given how many 1-step changers have proportional issues and this one doesn't primarily because of the transformation, I can't get too bothered about this issue. Unlike the vehicle mode-- which has fairly minimal mold detailing because of how sleek the Stingray is-- Crosshairs' robot bits have an impressive amount of intricate mold detailing. Pistons, pieces of "car armor", the fake headlights on the chest, all the intricate detailing on the chest, amrs, and head-- it's all pretty movie-accurate, with Crosshairs having his signature slight scowl molded on his face (really, all his headsculpt is missing is a toothpick in the mouth to be perfect). There's a bit of silver on the chest and his head is well-painted, but otherwise his robot pieces are devoid of paint, which is particularly inaccurate on the legs-- I wish a few more bits of silver were added, here. For articulation, Crosshairs can move back-and-forth at the elbows, and that's it. Disappointing, even considering how these toys aren't made to be super poseable.
1-Step AoE Crosshairs definitely has his drawbacks-- parts that wobble a little in vehicle mode is the biggest one, along with some movie-inaccurate kibble in robot mode, VERY limited articulation, a trigger button that doesn't always work--- but as far as this subline goes, this is one of the better designs, with no real major proportional issues in either mode. Recommended over the Power Battler if you want a simpler Crosshairs design.

Review by Beastbot
