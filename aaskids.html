Autobot Skids (Tuner; Deluxe) [Autobot Alliance]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Hard
Color Scheme : Black, milky green, rusty brownish orange, very dark transparent blue, and some light milky gray, white, silver, light orange, dark brownish gray
Rating : 7.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen deluxe Autobot Skids. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen deluxe Autobot Skids here .)

Since Mudflap got a "Tuner" deco , it was only a matter of time before Skids got one, too. Like Mudflap's "Tuner" version, this version of Skids keeps the same basic colors as the original version, with a little extra "color" thrown in and some changed vehicle mode paint apps. Skids' main color of green has been toned down a bit to more of a medium shade, which honestly looks a bit better against the light milky gray parts of his robot mode-- more contrast. (The painted green parts actually fit in very well with the plastic green parts-- the disparity in colors in the photos above is just a result of camera light.) A really nice shade of brownish orange has been added to some of his robot parts, which really helps the color scheme more than you'd think, and makes his robot mode colors pop a bit more than in the vehicle mode, where that color's hidden. That-- along with a bit of dark gray-- helps to edge out almost all of that ugly light milky gray, to boot. In vehicle mode, a patch of black has been added to his roof, which was SORELY needed on the first release, so I'm quite grateful it's on this version. There's also a rather neat black trim around the bottom of the mode, with some little reverse-color "skid" marks on them (i.e, green marks "on" the black paint). It's a very interesting deco job, though I personally prefer the larger, more obvious skidmarks on the original version. The transparent blue plastic has been significantly darkened on this version, which means you can't see the robot mode bits through the windows in vehicle mode, which I'm grateful for. However, it also makes the headlights and taillights too dark, so it's a bit of a trade-off. On his back end, Skids has two hilarious little paint apps tampoed on-- his license plate reads "1H8CONS"-- obvious 133t-speak for "I Hate 'Cons"-- and a "bumper sticker" that says "I helped make the Fallen Falled!". Both fit RIGHT in with Skids' personality and are great little touches.
No mold changes have been made to Tuner Skids.
The deluxe version of Tuner Skids has a slightly-improved (though still quite similar) color scheme when compared to the initial release, and some fairly neat paint apps. I still wish they had changed the overall scheme a LITTLE bit more to justify a second purchase, though. If you don't have a version of (admittedly medicore) deluxe mold and want one, get this version-- but if you already have the RotF version, there's no reason to get this redeco unless you're a completist.


Tuner Skids Bio :
Oil Pan may have crazy good driving skills, but he's not used to dealing with someone as unpredictable as Autobot Skids. The Autobot is more than happy to cause total chaos on the freeway, as long as it means catching his quarry. The other Autobots might wish he was more careful, but at least he gets the job done.
