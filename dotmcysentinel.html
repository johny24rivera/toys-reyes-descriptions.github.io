Sentinel Prime (Commander; DotM)

Allegiance: Autobot
Size : Commander
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Black, red, milky gray, and some silver
Rating : 8.9

This movie incarnation of Sentinel Prime transforms into a Rosenbauer fire truck. In vehicle mode, there's little to complain about, particularly for a figure at the Cyberverse scale. The proportions are spot-on (as you'd expect for a licensed vehicle), with the only real extra being that you can see the sword stashed underneath the wheels if you look at him from a side angle-- but this is pretty minor. The color scheme of black, red, and gray is pretty straightforward, but it's certainly still fitting for a fire truck, and the black angular stripes on the bottom third of this mode help to break up the red a bit. The windows desperately need some paint apps, but otherwise the necessary paint is there, such as his Rosenbauer emblem, Autobot symbols, and "316" markings on his sides. The mold detailing is really superb for the size, with anything remotely needing detailing having it-- even right down to the tiny headlights on his front bumper and the ladder on his back end (though they aren't painted, natch). His ladder/gun weapon can rotate around its center point in this mode, but not up or down.
Sentinel's robot mode is also generally pretty impressive, and manages to incorporate most of his vehicle parts into his overall form pretty well. The sides of the vehicle form his arms and shoulders pretty accurately, with parts of his roof forming a "cape" behind him that looks cool, but isn't so large that it gets in the way of movement or articulation. The sides of the front bumper that form his chest don't have pieces to "connect them" to each other, making his chest look a tad off, but that's a small quibble. His legs suffer the most from the size class, being a bit overly blocky (particularly at the lower feet), and having shells of his vehicle mode hanging out in front of said lower legs. That said, at least they don't get in the way of movement. The mold detailing on Sentinel's robot bits-- like his face and chest-- are exquisitely done, even if the rather plain silver paint apps don't bring out all that detail in the best way. Interestingly enough for a Commander-class toy, Sentinel Prime comes with BOTH of his signature weapons-- a really cool long double-bladed sword and a shield-gun made out of part of his roof folded up. When it comes to articulation, Sentinel's is pretty decent for a Commander figure-- he can move at the neck (but only up-and-down), as well as at the shoulders (at three points), elbows, hips (slightly restricted because of their square shape), knees, and ankles.
Sentinel Prime is a very well-engineered Cyberverse toy, looking very accurate in both modes and only having some rather minor kibble in robot mode. Add that to his good proportions, cool weaponry, and nice articulation, and unless you absolutely must have a bigger Sentinel Prime and/or prefer very complicated transformations, this is the version of the character I'd go after.
