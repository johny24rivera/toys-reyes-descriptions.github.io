Perceptor (Classics)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Red, black, and some light pastel blue, silver, and pale yellow
Rating : 3.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Legends of Cybertron Red Alert. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Legends of Cybertron Red Alert here .)

Having Red Alert done up as G1 Perceptor is pretty creative, and rather unexpected-- the homage to Perceptor was most likely done because of the large cannon over the shoulder in robot mode, which is similar to G1 Perceptor's telescope-cannon. The colors are spot-on Perceptor, with pretty much all the colors exactly like they were on the G1 toy. The red and pale blue go particularly well together, and the black wheels and gun serve as a nice dark color to contrast against the others. The silver chest and yellow visor in robot mode also go well with the other colors and help to give the robot mode a bit more individuality color-wise than it otherwise would have. However, Perceptor's main problem (besides the horrible mold, discussed in the LoC Red Alert review linked above) is that his vehicle mode really needs more paint apps. Really, on the bare minimum of paint is used on his vehicle mode-- he really needs something more to break up all that red, and using another paint app or two on the all-black cannon would've helped as well.
No mold changes have been made to Classics Perceptor.
Classics Perceptor is a decent repaint and a nice, creative homage to the G1 character. However, its plainness in vehicle mode and the fact that it's definitely one of the worst TF molds released recently makes it really hard to recommend to anyone but the most diehard completist.
