Divebomb (RotF)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Moderately light red, black, pasty white, and some dull gray, silver, dull metallic bronze, and metallic gunmetal gray
Rating : 7.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen Ransack. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen Ransack here .)

Believe it or not, this Divebomb is meant to be the same Divebomb from the first movie-- a bit of a "downgrade" given the vehicle mode, no? Regardless, this version of Divebomb is a bit of an homage to the famous Red Baron biplane. The paint job mimics it perfectly-- the red is just about the right shade, and the white and black both serve as excellent contrast colors against the red. However, compared to Ransack, Divebomb's colors look more "new" and less worn, which for me doesn't really fit a historic alt mode like this. For the most part Divebomb has a pretty good number of paint apps, but nearly all the paint apps he has are either black or white. I understand why this was done-- to keep it his vehicle mode a bit more accurate to the Red Baron's signature paint job-- but it means his color scheme isn't as varied as I usually like it. Most of the more unique colors are sadly only used on his robot head, like the metallic bronze and gunmetal gray.
No mold changes have been made to RotF Divebomb.
RotF Divebomb is an excellent homage to the Red Baron's aircraft, but if that by itself isn't enough to get you to buy this toy than I'd stick with its original release as Ransack. Even though Ransack's colors are a bit duller and more eye-catching, it's more varied, realistic, and "worn"-looking, which I think just looks better on an alt mode as old as this one, as opposed to Divebomb's pretty straight shade of red.


Divebomb Bio :
Entranced with the romance and daring of ancient Decepticons living on Earth, Divebomb rescanned his vehicle mode to mimic one of the most famous of those old warriors. Unfortunately, paired with Skystalker to fight an Autobot antiaircraft specialist, he's quickly learning the limitations of his new form.
