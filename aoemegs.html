Megatron (AoE)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Light milky gray, very dark milky brown, and some dull brownish tan, dark metallic gunmetal gray, silver, light red, dark milky gray, and metallic bronze
Rating : 5.5

Can't have a movie line without a Megatron, now, can we-- even if he's been upgraded to Galvatron ! This "throwback" 1-step changer is based off the RotF version of Megatron, whose alt mode is an alien tank-thing with some jet boosters in the back, though the jet aspects of him have been de-emphasized in this toy, making him look more like an alien tank instead. Most of the details and proportions are fairly accurate in this mode; he's got angled treads, rounded areas around his main gun emplacement which isn't quite a turret but looks like it almost could be-- as well as the signature odd "robot head behind a mask in between the front treads" bit. There are two noticeable downsides to this version, though. The first is that, from the back end, Megatron looks a bit weak. He's got an extra set of treads that are obviously his robot legs, and there's a noticeable gap in said back end as well. Secondly, because of the transformation and because RotF Megatron's right arm is noticeably larger than his left, the shoulder "bump" on the right tread is definitely larger than the one on the left and a fairly obvious extra. On a bit of a lesser note, if you look at him from a direct-on side view in this mode, a gap down the middle is quite noticeable, as there's some definite space in between the bottom-- which forms his robot chest, arms, and the like-- and the top of the tank mode. That said, from the vast majority of angles you can't see this, so this is a minor quibble. The color scheme doesn't really change anything about RotF Megs' typical colors, for the most part-- he's still largely that very blah light milky gray plastic that just looks boring, though the very dark brown and dark metallic gunmetal gray paint used on his treads is a pretty good shade and helps provide some contrast, albeit not much color. He's also got some darker gray for the main gun, but again, it doesn't add much. There's also a bit of tan near the front in front of his robot head, which adds perhaps a LITTLE color, but not a lot. Then again, this IS all accurate to his RotF version, so I can't complain too much about his lack of color. The most impressive part about this mode is all the mold detailing-- whether it's the incredible amount of intricate detailing on the treads, the ridges on the cannon, or the curved bits on the top, this mode is covered in excellent molded detail.
To transform Megatron to robot mode, you simply press down on the little dark gray lever on the top of his tank mode and he auto-converts. (To change him back, you just move everything back to its vehicle mode position until it clicks into place.) Some 1-step changers have pretty impressive and fun transformations, but unfortunately that's not the case for Megatron. Basically the back "tank parts" rotate back to reveal his arms and legs a bit more, while his robot head tilts forward some. It's pretty darned clear that his entire tank mode is on his back in this mode, which makes for one heck of a piece of kibble-- thankfully his feet are wide and solid, so there's no stability issues, at least. Still, this mode definitely plays second-fiddle to the tank mode. The legs and the chest are the only things that look solid (though the chest is just a tad small proportionally); the arms are a bit too stiff and wide, and suffer from being on the same "plane" as the treads on Megatron's upper shoulders and hanging out way to the sides of his butt. The robot head also has the sides of the mask stil in front of it-- albeit separated a little on the sides-- and the turret sticking straight up out of his upper back certainly isn't movie-accurate. At least Megatron's mold detailing is still quite intricate, complete with the odd dangly claws for hands and the asymmetrical shoulders. There's also plenty of the "fragmented metal" look on his chest, all of which is painted silver (along with his upper legs and knees), which is certainly a nice change from all the light milky gray plastic. The head is sculpted a little oddly, though, with the grin a bit TOO wide. The bronze face also doesn't look quite right-- there's a bit too much of said color where there shouldn't be, like around his light red eyes. As for articulation, Megatron can move back-and-forth at each shoulder-- as is the case with pretty much every one-step changer, he can't move much.
The AoE 1-step Megatron toy is one of those toys I'm just unsure why it exists. There were plenty of cool KSI Decepticon designs in the movie that never got a toy, but instead we get a 1-step toy of a Megatron design we're already gotten tons of toys of? The tank mode is fairly accurate, and the mold detailing is impressive for a 1-stepper, but the transformation-- usually the highlight for these toys-- is pretty unimpressive, and his robot mode has his entire alt mode hanging off the back, along with a few more minor oddities. If you want an easy-to-transform version of this design, I'd go for the "Autobot Alliance" Activator -- even if the latter has an inferior color scheme, it has considerably better articulation and moderately better proportions.

Review by Beastbot
