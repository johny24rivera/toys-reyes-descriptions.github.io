Decepticon Stinger (1-Step Changer; AoE) [Wal-Mart Exclusive]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Red, black, and some silver and metallic gunmetal gray
Rating : 7.1

Finally! A toy of Stinger that actually is a new mold and has the correct alt mode-- that is, a Pagani Zonda, one of the fastest cars on the planet. As you'd expect from a licensed alt mode, the proportions are pretty much perfect in this mode, and the robot mode extras are VERY minimal. The tips of the toes are a little obvious from the back end, and looking in through the open windows it's obvious there's not a driver's seat in there, but both of these are pretty darn minimal extras and easily overlooked. The red plastic serves as a nice base for Stinger, with the black diagonal stripes on the sides and the large "swoop" of black around the main windows looking great and contrasting quite well with the red-- it's a fairly basic color scheme, but it still looks pretty good. There's also a bit of silver on the front headlights, but sadly the front grill isn't painted black, nor is anything on the back end painted, either. I'm normally a fan of large faction symbols, but in this case the big Decepticon symbol on Stinger's hood actually helps to add a little extra color here, and contrasts against the black quite well. (That said, it's the wrong kind of Decepticon symbol, being the G1 symbol as opposed to the movie "narrower eyes" version.) As you'd expect from a car with this aerodynamic of a look, the mold detailing in this mode is fairly minimal, but still accurate-- and there are intricate details where required, such as on the wheels and the honeycomb-like detailing (get it?) on his front grill.
To transform Stinger to robot mode, you simply push in on the little button on his windshield, which causes the vehicle bits to separate a bit-- then you just stand him up. To get him back to vehicle mode, you simply lightly push those pieces back together. He uses the same one-step transformation gimmick originally pioneered in the line by Lockdown , and thus shares many of the same positives and negatives. Sure, it's not a very impressive transformation, but it largely gets the job done, with a fairly proportional robot mode for a one-step changer. There is some substantial kibble, though, with the doors hanging off the side arms, the entire roof and top middle section of the car hanging back behind the main body, and-- on a lesser note-- there being a noticeable gap in between the bottom of the leg pieces and the foot pieces, which are molded into the back-halves of the car mode that form Stinger's legs. From a side view Stinger looks a bit two-dimensional with a large backpack, but from the front he looks pretty okay despite the extras, which aren't as egregious as on some of the other one-step changers-- all in all, it's not that bad of a trade-off. That said, because of this transformation, the vehicle mode pieces don't really tab together at all, and the vehicle mode bits can move around a little without much force, which is kind of annoying. The mold detailing takes a MAJOR uptick in this mode, with many of the movie-accurate details appearing in this mode, such as the springs along his stomach, the stylized fake vehicle front-chest, the rather evil-looking helmeted headsculpt, and the alternating sharp points and curves on his legs, making him very much look like an "evil doppleganger" of 'Bee in this mode in terms of his general detailing. The red and black is broken up more in this mode than in vehicle mode, with more of each color on each part of the robot-- no one large part is solely one color. That said, not extending the silver paint app on the abs down to the crotch is a bit of a head-scratcher, and I'm not sure why Stinger's eyes are painted black, as opposed to the bright green they are in the movie, which looks MUCH cooler and provides a bit more color. As with all one-step changers with this transformation scheme, sadly Stinger can only move a bit back-and-forth at his elbows-- otherwise he's a statue in this mode.
Stinger's design is one of the better one-step changer designs, but it's a real shame that this was really the only option given to collectors who wanted a Stinger toy that actually LOOKED like Stinger instead of... y'know, a redeco of someone else. It took about 4 years to rectify this (which shows how late I am with this review, but whatevs), but I'd recommend this toy a lot less now that Stinger has a deluxe "Studio Series" toy. That said, for those who want a quick-to-transform version of the silent Decepticon doppleganger, it's certainly better-than-average for the subline.

Review by Beastbot
