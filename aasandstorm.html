Sandstorm (Autobot Alliance)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Legend
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Black, tan, moderately light orange, and some silver and white
Rating : 7.9

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Universe 2.0 Beachcomber. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Universe 2.0 Beachcomber here .)

Sandstorm here may be a slight homage to G1 Sandstorm, who had similar colors (but was a triple changer, with dune buggy being only one of his modes). Regardless if it is or not, Sandstorm certainly has a color scheme here befitting his name-- the light tan and orange complement each other quite well and certainly give him a bit of "sandy/desert/beach" theme. The black serves as a nice contrast color to keep Sandstorm from looking too light or one-tone. The silver and white accents go nicely against the black, but are light enough where they don't contrast enough with the tan and orange paint. The paint apps are widespread enough throughout the figure where there aren't any big patches of unpainted plastic, which is of course a big plus.
No mold changes have been made to Autobot Alliance Sandstorm.
Autobot Alliance Sandstorm is a nice redeco, with a color scheme that fits the toy quite well, G1 homage or no. I like Beachcomber's overall darker color scheme SLIGHTLY better, but they're both great deco jobs, and this is a fine "second place" toy if you can't find (or don't want to pay the prices for) Universe 2.0 Beachcomber. Mildly recommended, particularly if you're into Legends class figures.
