Deadlock (2016 E-Hobby/TFCC Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Black, dark dull purple, and some transparent cherry red, white, red, and metallic dull gold
Rating : 9.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Generations Drift. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Generations Drift here .)

Deadlock was Drift's Decepticon alter-ego/persona before he became Drift in the IDW comics, and this redeco-- shared between e-HOBBY and the Transformers Collectors' Club-- is colored like Deadlock (although in the comics Deadlock had a slightly different body, which isn't reflected in this release). Deadlock's main colors are black and purple-- certainly very Decepticon-y colors, and the transparent cherry red used for the windows makes Deadlock's alt mode in particular look VERY evil-- it's a nice color combo. That said, the purple is a bit duller than I'd like, and doesn't contrast against the black or stand out as much as I'd prefer. What really helps to make Deadlock's color scheme are his accent paint apps, which are mostly visible in robot mode. There's some dull gold on his wheel hubs, his waist, and lower legs; some red on his knees, feet, waist, chest, and shoulders; and white on his head, lower arms, and waist. The gold helps Deadlock look a bit more "royal", and contrasts against the white and black pretty well. The red carries his "eeevil" look a bit farther and is good in the amounts used, and the white contrasts against the other colors nicely, but is thankfully used sparingly so the toy doesn't look TOO much like Drift. The head does still look a bit too much like Drift, but I figure if one part should be similar between Deadlock & Drift it's the head-- the pale purple face and the large black "eyebrow" still helps differentiate Deadlock enough while still making it obvious "hey, this guy becomes Drift"!
No mold changes have been made to Deadlock, but he does come with Blurr's sniper rifle and smaller guns in addition to Drift's long sword, which makes sense since Deadlock wasn't big on swords initially.
Deadlock is a pretty good redeco, and this is definitely a recommended purchase for Drift fans who have the money to be in the market for foreign/club exclusives. He's got a lot of nice paint apps and a unique color scheme, and it's one of the best early Generations molds to boot. The dull purple is my only real issue; it needed to be lighter or something. He's not quite as well-done, as, say, Shattered Glass Drift , but he's pretty close.

Review by Beastbot
