Buzzsaw (Beast Machines)

Allegiance : Maximal
Size : Basic
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Green, black, yellow, transparent orange, transparent light orange fading into transparent green, and some red and silver
Rating : 6.9

Beast mode is a wasp. This mode is the coolest-looking wasp beast mode I've seen, both in Beast Wars and Beast Machines. Some of neatest features are the eyes on the head, the dropping, but not stiff, antennae, and the neat transparent. The detailing on this toy is pretty good for a basic, too. There are a few problems, though. The first, and most noticeable one, is the long spring between Buzzsaw's abdomen and stinger. Although the "stinging action" is kinda neat for a basic, there is no locking lever, and the continual bobbing of the stinger as you move Buzzsaw around gets a bit annoying after a while. The second is the disturbing amount of cheap, bendy plastic on this toy. While this type of plastic used on the antennae (and, in robot mode, the robot head) is useful for the twitching of the antennae, the insect legs and the green part of the stinger aren't the best place for it. The legs tend to stay in a certain position if you leave them one way for a while, which isn't cool if you want them to look right in both modes, and the stinger looks less menacing if you can bend it. The third problem is that the wings, which become the legs in the robot mode, look a bit odd in this mode. The extra prongs on the ends of them and the large black lines at the upper sides of them are some obvious indications that these wings become the feet. The final problem, which may not become apparent until you see the toy in stores, is the fact that Buzzsaw is a bit small, even for a basic. Hasbro should at least keep the size of the basics the same if they are going to up the price a bit, don't you think? Overall, the pros and cons of this beast mode seem to cancel each other out, so I'm leaving final judgement of this mode up to you.
Robot mode looks decent, but not the best. For one, the slagging bobbing stinger is still there, although it has become Buzzsaw's left claw. The idea of the wings becoming Buzzsaw's legs in robot mode was a creative one, but, in my opinion, ultimately failed. These appendages look odd in both modes. Although Buzzsaw can surprisingly stand up fairly well, they look too skinny, and Buzzsaw has "high heels" in this mode, even though he's a male. :) His right arm is just two of his insect legs, and don't really resemble anything. The only thing I can think of is maybe you can make then some sort of lighting-guns or something. The little piece of plastic that's supposed to hook into the rest of his body from his right arm doesn't fit in, and it looks odd, sticking out where it is. The robot mode head looks very nice, though, and there are not that many extras on his back or otherwise. The spark orb is on the inside of his stinger-claw. It's a bit hard to see, as it is the same color as the plastic around it.
A decent Beast Machine toy, but not the best. Looks a bit more like a Pred than a Maximal. Mildly recommended.


Buzzsaw Bio :
FUNCTION: Aerial Reconnaissance
Motto: "I float like a butterfly but sting like a bee!"
Saved by Rattrap from the effects of the conversion freezing virus, Buzzsaw is grateful to the Maximal cause. Impressive aerial abilities and tendency towards gossip make him an excellent reconnaissance scout - likes to be the buzz about town. Multi-function stinger can pierce even the most impenetrable armor and functions as a highly sensitive receiver/transmitter. Slightly proud, often heard referring to himself as "the bee's knees", a statement none of his Maximal associates can discern.
