Optimus Prime (Silver Knight First Edition) [AoE] {Target Exclusive}

Allegiance : Autobot
Price : $60 (U.S.)
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Light milky gray, silver, transparnet light blue, red, and some black, dark metallic gunmetal gray, light pale blue, light red, and dark dull blue
Rating : 7.0

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original AoE First Edition Optimus Prime toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original AoE First Edition Optimus Prime toy here .)

Optimus Prime's "Silver Knight" form gets a big, articulated toy for the Target exclusive subline-- only, it's the larger but slightly inferior First Edition form of the toy and not the AoE Leader class mold . Still, in vehicle mode this new paint scheme is a beaut-- the entire nose and body of the truck is COATED with either silver paint or rather extensive flame paint apps that are a bit more extensive than on the original First Edition release. I'm not one for big allegiance symbols, but the big Autobot symbol on the roof does actually look nice surrounded by all that silver paint. The chrome is also on pretty much every place that it was on the original First Edition toy except for the shoulders, which furthers the whole "Silver Knight" thing in robot mode. (Fair warning, though; the red paint on the chrome front grill can chip off rather easily, though I haven't had any problems with the chrome itself.) He's also got some nice metallic gunmetal gray paint on his chest, shoulders, and faceplate, and a silver-painted head with light blue eyes. Additionally, he's got some extra flame paint apps painted on his chrome chest. The transparent plastic on the original has been switched from yellow to a more Autobot-y light blue, and looks better than the yellow (in my opinion) on the weapons in particular, and it contrasts well with the silver/gray. There's also a bit of light red and dark blue paint and plastic on a few areas of the robot mode-- such as legs and parts of the ankles, elbows, and shoulders-- that helps to break up the robot mode a bit more and add some more color to make him less monochromatic. If all that I just mentioned to you was the full description of the toy, I'd actually rate this above the original-- all that silver paint, and a rare genuinely new color scheme for Prime, looks great! However, there is one big downer here, and that's that the bottom section of the vehicle mode and many of the parts of the robot mode are NOT silver-painted, but instead that blah, light milky gray plastic that's used far too much on TFs as is. And there are LARGE sections of the gray that are completely uninterrupted, such as the entire back third or so of the vehicle mode-- indeed, pick him up in that mode and look at him from the underside and you can get a sense of just how much unpainted gray plastic there is. If they didn't have the budget to completely dip the toy in silver paint for this exclusive release, at least using a more metallic shade of plastic would've worked-- but nope, there's just that blah gray there instead.
No mold changes have been made to this version of Silver Knight Optimus Prime.
The "First Edition" version of Silver Knight Optimus Prime has an impressive amount of paint on it on the front of the vehicle mode and a more original color scheme for Optimus than is usually found on redecos of him nowadays. However, despite all the silver and red and gunmetal gray paint and all the silver chrome, having such large pieces of him being uninterrupted blah light milky gray plastic-- instead of being painted or a more interesting, metallic shade of gray-- hurts the toy more than you'd think. As such, I'd recommend the original release of the mold over this version-- unless you want both the Leader class mold AND this mold, but also want this mold to look "more different" on a shelf.

Review by Beastbot
