Lockdown (AoE)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Dark milky greenish brown, transparent moderately dark greenish blue, milky gray, and some metallic silvery blue, black, pale metallic greenish gold, and red
Rating : 7.8

Lockdown's vehicle mode is a licensed Lambourghini Aventador, and as you'd expect from a licensed alt mode, he looks pretty darned good and proportional in this mode. There really aren't any robot mode extras at all in this mode, not even if you look in the windows-- granted, there aren't molded-in seats in there, but the parts that are visible in there are mostly hinges and the like and not obvious robot bits. The main color of a dark milky greenish brown really doesn't look good to me-- I would've preferred a color closer to black-- but on the other hand, the milky, semi-glossy appearance of the plastic doesn't look half bad. The transparent blue used for the front and rear windows (the side windows are mostly open) looks fantastic against the greenish brown. The silvery blue headlights, black bits on the front and rear bumpers, red taillights, and little gold Lamboughini logo on the front hood help to break up the colors a bit, but some additional paint was still needed on the figure in this mode, as there are some LARGE areas of just the greenish brown plastic. The tires also could've used painted spokes, as well. The mold detailing, as you'd expect, is minimal in this mode, given the look of the Aventador and the sleekness of it, but a couple of places like the wheels are well-detailed. Lockdown's weapon is a rather large, long gun that can fire a push-missile. It looks nice and has a ton of excellent mold detailing, but it was definitely made for the robot mode first, as it just sort of pegs along the top of this mode, which looks rather awkward on such a sleek vehicle. I wish there had been some way to store the weapon underneath the car, instead.
Lockdown's transformation to robot mode is almost exactly the same as DotM deluxe Roadbuster's , though the back end folds up a bit better on ol' Lockdown and the rear pieces of the car mode form some bulk to his shoulders. By themselves, the different parts of Lockdown look passable-to-good. The legs in particular are the best part, being quite movie-accurate (minus the wheels sticking of the hips so much), and I especially love the two-toed feet and all the wonderful shock absorbers and the like detailed on him. The legs aren't quite thick or long enough proportionally, though. The chest and head are the next-most-impressive part with the sides of the Aventador headlights forming obvious sides of the chest-- but this isn't at all how it looks in the movie, despite the panel of robot mode detailing that covers up the center of the chest. It makes him look like he has a bit too much mass near the bottom of his chest/stomach. The headsculpt is quite well-done, but not QUITE movie-accurate-- like it's going off an earlier rendition of Lockdown, when his face was slightly more skull-like than his final movie design. His scar isn't present, and the structure of his head is just a bit... different, really. The arms are the most disappointing part-- the upper part looks fine, if slightly movie inaccurate, but at deluxe scale it is NOT cool for the main villain of the movie to merely have his hands detailed on the sides of car doors-- with the car doors even at the bottom of the hands, preventing one from slotting a weapon entirely through one of his hand holes! It just looks awful, and Lockdown's robot mode proportions are a bit wonky, too-- his head's too small in proportion to his chest, and his arms are too long, reaching down to his knees easily and giving him almost a gorilla-like appearance in that respect. He's also just too darned short and small in general as a toy-- in the movie, he's nearly as tall as Optimus Prime, and having his "mainline toy" be a deluxe is just unacceptable, especially given how awesome of a character he was in the movie and a formidable threat. That said, there are definitely more visible paint apps in this mode to liven up his color scheme a bit more, with silver on his face, lower arms, stomach, and upper legs, with some additional pale metallic gold on his upper legs. Then there's the black on his chest, and the metallic silvery blue from his headlights on the sides of his chest. His eyes do have light piping, but it's the wrong color (it should be green), and it doesn't work that well anyways-- the slot for the light to come through is too small. For articulation, Lockdown can move at the neck, shoulders (at two points), elbows (at two points), hips (at two points-- one is shared among both legs, meaning if you move one forward the other will too unless you keep your hand on the other leg to keep it still), and at the knees (at two points) and slightly back-and-forth at the ankles. It's decent articulation, but not extraordinary-- some more articulation in the arms and perhaps some waist rotation would've been appreciated. Lockdown's large weapon from vehicle mode can be held in his hands but-- yes indeed-- it can be slotted over and fit on top of his head firmly to replicate the "gunface" he does in the movie! It looks awesome and is definitely the highlight of the toy, in my opinion-- I honestly didn't expect Hasbro would do that.
AoE deluxe Lockdown has a pretty good car mode and some excellent details-- and his head-gun gimmick is pretty darned awesome. As a MOLD, he's okay, with decent articulation, even though his robot mode proportions are a little out of whack. However, as a representation of LOCKDOWN, he falls far short, being far smaller, shorter, and far less imposing in his proportions and look than he does in the movie. As such, he's one of my biggest disappointments in the AoE line, for the main villain character to get a "merely okay" too-small deluxe toy that can in no way go toe-to-toe with any mainline Prime toy.


Lockdown Bio :
Lockdown captures everything he tracks. Few of his targets have outmaneuvered him, and even fewer have escaped his prisons. Now, the merciless bounty hunter has set his sights on the Autobots!
