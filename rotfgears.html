Autobot Gears (RotF)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Metallic silver, blue, dull rusty reddish brown, light pale transparent purple, and some black, dull metallic bronze, light flat blue, light milky gray, greenish gray, dull bluish green, light orange, red, and dull pale metallic copper
Rating : 9.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the Movie Stockade toy. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the Movie Stockade toy here .)

Gears is a light homage to the G1 character of the same name. Of course, you wouldn't really know it from the vehicle mode, which is colored completely different from his G1 self. He's got a very nice-looking metallic silver paint (with just a hint of purplish tint to it) covering every non-transparent piece of plastic in this mode. It's decorated on the roof and on the sides with a nice gear motif, which of course fits given his name. It's not overdone to the point where it looks loud, but it's just there enough to give him a bit more color variety. The transparent light pale purple is a decent color for the windows, but painting the rear windows a light blue is very noticeably different-- that was a bad color decision, there. All of his necessary mold details-- such as his taillights, fornt bumper, and the wheel spokes--are painted, as well. The orange and red used for his taillights is a particularly poppin' color, too. In robot mode, Gears almost completely changes his color scheme to be closer to his G1 scheme. A medium, rather nice shade of blue and some rusty reddish brown are the main new colors in this mode, and both of them go excellently with the silver car parts. A few other colors, like light milky gray and black, serve mostly as accent colors on some of the minor connector parts. Some dull bronze paint helps to round out the general color scheme-- one can hardly say Gears isn't colorful in robot mode. I think it's a rather ingenious way to give Gears his G1 colors without having them show in vehicle mode, where they'd be a bit "loud" for the generally realistically-colored movie line.
No mold changes have been made to RotF Gears.
RotF Gears is a fantastic, imaginative repaint. He's got some G1 homage colors in robot mode and is just generally very eye-catching in that mode. He's got tons of paint apps, and his entire vehicle mode is covered in a nice sheen of silver paint with some nice gear motifs that offer something different from "just" a G1 homage repaint as well. Plus, it's great mold! Really not much to complain about, here. Highly recommended, and recommended over Stockade.


Autobot Gears Bio :
If there's a negative angle to be found in any situation, Gears will find it. It's not that he's a miserable guy, or even that he's unhappy on Earth. He just really likes complaining. Nothing makes him happier than having something to gripe about. The other Autobots actually find his complaining funny, rather than annoying, which is part of the reason he keeps on doing it.
