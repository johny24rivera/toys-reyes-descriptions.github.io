Rhinox (Beast Wars 10th Anniversary Edition)

Allegiance : Maximal
Size : Deluxe ($15 U.S.-priced, however)
Comes with : Transmutate's waist and right leg; DVD containing the episode "The Spark"
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Black, pale brown, dark metallic bronzish brown, dull green, and some white, silver, dull gold, and dull red
Rating : 6.2

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Beast Wars Rhinox. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Beast Wars Rhinox here .)

10th Anniversary Rhinox's overall color layout is the same as the toy's original version, but the tones of most of the colors have changed. The brown used for the rhino mode is of a decidedly darker, duller, more "fleshy-like" tone, and there's a really nice new semi-metallic bronish brown fade on the back and the front of the legs of the rhino mode. The green used for Rhinox's robot parts is also a bit darker of a shade. (The gold and black seem to be largely the same shade as the original, however.) 10th Anniversary Rhinox does have a few different paint apps-- for one, the rhino eyes are actually painted white with pupils this time, as opposed to the more evil-looking solid red of the original. There's also a silver-and-red Maximal symbol painted on the right rhino shoulder. In addition, some of the paint apps on the robot head has been changed to make the color layout more show-accurate (and all-around better-looking), and a black stripe paint app on the Mutant head has also been added.
No mold changes have been made to 10th Anniversary Rhinox, though he does have an assembly error; his shoulders and upper arms have been assembled backwards. It's an easy fix, however-- just unscrew the screws on the shoulders, flip some of the parts around so they look right, then re-screw the shoulders back together again.
10th Anniversary Rhinox has a much more show-accurate color scheme than the original, and I love the new, more fleshy tones to to the rhino mode, plus the new robot head paint apps. It's still not different enough where I'd recommend it if you have the original Rhinox, however. Although this toy does have some pretty good nostalgia value, it's not that good of an actual Transformer when compared to today's standards especially, so if you don't have a particular attachment to Beast Wars, you're probably better off picking up one of the better-designed Cybertron toys or Alternators instead of this fella.


Rhinox Bio :
Maximal second-in-command and chief science officer, Rhinox prefers to remain aboard the Axalon to engaging in battle, but that doesn't mean he can't fight. On the contrary, his triple-plated steel skin and wide array of medium range and hand-to-hand weapons make him dangerous to even the toughest opponent. There's a good reason Rhinox is the last line of Maximal defense, after the perimeter is breached and all weapons systems are offline - no one's ever got past him.
