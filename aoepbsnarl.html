Snarl (Power Battler; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Dark jungle green, yellow, and some pale yellow, charcoal black, fuchsia, dark metallic gunmetal gray, silver, and metallic fuchsia
Rating : 7.6

Snarl's traditional dino mode of a stegosaurus makes it into the Age of Extinction line, though like the other AoE Dinobots, he's really ridged and spiky and generally more "EXTREME", to borrow a popular toy phrase from the '90s. He's also not in the movie at all, but included mostly because of the G1 Dinobots' lineup. For the Power Battler version, Snarl's stegosaurus mode has many positives, but some negatives dragging him down proportionally-- let's get to the latter first. From a front-on view, Snarl's simply too wide-- about a third or so wider in the body than he should be, really. This is primarily due to his gimmick, I'd assume (discussed later). Also due to his gimmick, his back plates are in two very odd rows-- the back plates should be alternating and very close to each other, but these are in two distinct rows rather far apart from each other on the body, with the plates NOT alternating but in fact all coming out of the back at the same place if you look at him from the side. The tail is also a bit short and stunted proportionally. All of this drags down Snarl's alt mode a bit, though not as much as it would have if he were a realistic-looking stegosaur. Other than the slightly noticeable fists on the rear legs and juuust the tip of the robot waist piece sticking out below his stomach, he doesn't have any robot extras in this mode at all, so that's a big plus. As for the mold detailing, Snarl has the usual bunch of spikes everywhere, with his mostly concentrating on the bottom of his tail and on simply making his back plates more pointy-- that said, only the middle (unpainted yellow, which are noticeably duller than the painted yellow) back plates are actually of a softer rubbery plastic. None of the spikes are nearly sharp enough to actually poke someone, though, so no worries about that. There's also quite a lot of interlocking, armor plate-like detailing on his legs and along the sides of his main body. His stegosaurus head actually looks a bit ugly, but in a good way-- it has a bit of a "pig snout" with some extra little spikes above his eyebrows and the back of his head (because clearly he didn't have enough already). He also has sharp, carnivore-style teeth, despite the fact that he's supposed to be a plant-eater-- but given the style, I don't think realism is at all the main point here. For a color scheme, Snarl largely has a dark jungle/forest green for most of his dino mode, with most of his plates painted yellow. The green/yellow combo works fairly well, given their spots on the color wheel, though the yellow paint on most of the plates and on the sides of the front part of his body is a bit lighter/brighter than I'd like. There's a hint of other colors-- some silver on the teeth, some dark metallic gunmetal gray on the neck, some black on the back, and some fuchsia on some small parts of his rear legs and gimmick trigger, but they aren't numerous/large enough in this mode to really make a substantial contribution to the overall scheme, beyond breaking up the green and yellow a bit. For articulation in this mode, Snarl can move back-and-forth at the shoulders, and his head can look downwards a bit at the neck as a side effect of his transformation-- though if you do this too much, the segmentÂ  where the neck meets the body comes up too far above the main body and it looks weird, so it's best only to move him here a little. In general that's pretty poor articulation-- I understand poseability isn't the point of Power Battlers, but I don't see why some knee articulation at the dino legs couldn't have been possible.
Snarl's transformation is a simplified version of his deluxe toy's transformation, with the front section folding down to become his legs while his tail splits in half and connects behind his rear legs and folds out to become his robot arms. The end result is a pretty unique-looking Dinobot-- in (mostly) a good way. I love how the tail halves fan out behind his shoulders-- they enhance his silhouette quite a bit. The way his feet (complete with the usual AoE Dinobot "spiky knight shoes") fold out from his neck and head is also pretty well-done. That said, I wish the dino feet on the sides of his robot feet could fold away or something-- they're REALLY darned obvious and there's not even an attempt to hide them, so at first glance it looks like Snarl actually has four feet in this mode. The arms are also eternally in a "flexing strongman" pose, which doesn't look too great. I'm also not a big fan of the headsculpt-- it's definitely the least knight-like out of all the AoE Dinobots' heads. There's a visor, sure, but it's ridged, there's obvious teeth below said visor in an odd, stretched-out grin, and the way the silver "front" of his face is painted and sculpted it looks like he's wearing some odd mask as opposed to a knight helmet. His neck is also overly bulky. That said, his other robot mode detailing is quite good, from the robotic "ab" detailing on his stomach to the rather unique "sunburst" patterns on his chest to the ridged armor on the hips. There's also a bit more color added to this mode, with the fuchsia plastic (and metallic fuchsia paint) becoming more apparent, particularly on the abs, where it contrasts wonderfully with the green and the silver on the sides of the stomach. I really wish the metallic fuchsia was used more on the toy, it looks wonderful. As for Snarl's Power Battler gimmick, if you pull down on the fuchsia handle on his back the plates there will spring forward out of and in front of his head, though I'm not quite sure what weapon they're supposed to emulate. The packaging says it's "Hammer Strike" action, but the stegosaurus plates don't look remotely like a hammer. "Upper back strike action" doesn't sound as great, I suppose. At least it doesn't cripple the toy, like some other Power Battler gimmicks. (Of note is that this action feature can't be used in beast mode, despite the trigger being present.) As for articulation, Snarl can move back-and-forth at the shoulders, and at the hips (at two points), knees, and ankles. I really wish he had some elbow and further shoulder articulation, but honestly that's pretty good articulation for a toy from this line, even if it doesn't meet the higher standards of the "Generations" mainline AoE toys.
Power Battlers Snarl may not be quite as good as the deluxe toy, but he's definitely one of the best Power Battlers toys, with a pretty nice-looking robot mode with good articulation (for the subline), a decent stegosaurus mode (width aside), and a decent color scheme with the fuchsia really making it pop in robot mode. His gimmick is rather odd, but it doesn't affect the rest of the toy much, so at least there's that. Recommended for fans who love movie Dinobots but aren't quite up to the task of more complex transformations (particularly with more-brittle translucent plastic, as the deluxe version has).

Review by Beastbot
