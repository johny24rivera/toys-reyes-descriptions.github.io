Optimus Prime (Battle Core) [Combiner Wars]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation to Robot : Medium
Difficulty of Transformation to Torso : Easy
Color Scheme : White, moderately light milky gray, and some black, metallic bluish silver, silver, metallic gold, metallic gunmetal gray, and moderately light blue
Rating : 7.6

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to the original Combiner Wars Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of the original Combiner Wars Optimus Prime here .)

One version of Optimus Prime for Combiner Wars isn't enough! This "upgraded" version of Optimus Prime is the one that actually appears in fiction (the IDW comics), and features a color scheme that seems to be a bit of a nod to Nova Prime, the evil old Autobot leader in said IDW comics. (What that portends for Optimus is... well, nothing, it's an homage that lasts a comic or two before he reverts to his old color scheme.) Regardless, Battle Core Prime's general color scheme is a bit more monochromatic than Optimus' usually is-- he's mostly white, with a good bit of light milky gray in there as well. The placement of the white honestly reminds me of Ultra Magnus , though this certainly isn't meant to be him-- though with all the sticker sets that let you "re-appropriate" this figure as someone else, you can certainly try to make him Magnus if you want to! The light milky gray is unfortunately that bland, non-metallic shade of plastic it usually is, though just a bit darker (i.e., slightly better, in my opinion). By itself, this would be a boring color scheme-- it's the metallic paint apps that really help this color scheme, the gold in particular, which is found on the legs, arms, abs, head crest, the real and "fake" truck fronts-- it's used on pretty much every part, albeit generally in small amounts on each part. I wish it had been used more liberally to give the color cheme a bit more "pop". The windows are painted a silvery blue-- an okay color for windows by itself, but I would've preferred something a bit lighter to complement Prime's color scheme. As it is, it just blends into the gray a bit too much. Same with the silver paint on places like the front bumper/headlights and portions of the torso mode upper legs. Same GENERALLY with the metallic gunmetal gray paint on things like his weapons and front grill, though it's difficult for metallic gunmetal to look outright bad, it just looks "sameish". The light blue eyes on the robot and combiner heads really pop against the white, and is another color I wish had been used more.
Surprisingly, Battle Core Optimus Prime DOES have a new head sculpt-- it's meant to be an homage to Star Convoy, complete with Prowl-esque headcrests and more details on the "helmet" around his core face & faceplate. It looks pretty good, particularly with the gold paint-- better than the original CW Optimus' headsculpt, methinks. (Unfortunately, the combined mode head is the same.) The misaligned ratchet problem has also been fixed for this release of the mold.
Battle Core Optimus Prime works okay as a "powered up" version of regular Prime-- or even can pass as Combiner Wars Nova Prime-- but generally, despite this version having a more original color scheme, I like the original CW Prime's colors better. The gold spices things up a bit, but generally the color scheme for this version of Optimus is a bit too monochromatic for my tastes, without enough other colors.
