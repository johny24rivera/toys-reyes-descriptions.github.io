Autobot Topspin (Legion; DotM)

Allegiance : Autobot
Size: Legion
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark blue, white, moderately light milky gray, and some black, metallic black, silver, red, light metallic silvery blue, and pale yellow
Rating : 5.6

This is the only version of DotM Topspin that has a non-weaponized (i.e., normal) vehicle mode, and looks more like you might find him at a racing track. The vehicle mode is robot extra-free, and the proportions are generally pretty good, though the hood and windows could stand to "poke out" a bit more from the surrounding body. Topspin's NASCAR car alt mode incorporates a pretty impressive amount of paint details onto this small of a toy, with not only the big "LOWE'S" logo on the front and the white paint along most other non-rimmed parts of this mode, but he also has his "48" number on the sides and tiny versions of his Chevrolet and Sprint/NASCAR logos. Unfortunately, the "48" yellow isn't solid enough-- when the plastic it's on goes from painted white to the dark blue plastic underneath on the bottom half of the car doors, the yellow itself gets a bit darker as well, so it's a bit "see-through" and doesn't look that great. On the other hand, all the white contrasts with the dark blue quite well in this mode, and the windows are painted not just black, but a metallic shade of black, which looks a bit better and more "glittery" under stronger light. The taillights are the only major details in this mode that aren't painted, which is unusual on a Legion-class toy. The mold detailing in this mode is unusually sparse for a movie toy, even considering the relative sleekness of the race car mode; the car door side details aren't even molded into the toy, for example.
Topspin's robot mode, however... oof. I get that they were trying to do a bit of a different transformation for a car legion-class toy, but this is NOT the way to do it. The arms are absolutely horrible, with literally halves of the front two-thirds of the alt mode hanging off each arm, which is a LOT of kibble. The gun-things above his shoulders aren't represented well, either, and the arms are rather 2-D, which only adds insult to injury. On the plus side, though, the chest, waist, and head are quite well-done for this scale, with tons of little mold detailing bits and Topspin's signature "hair" painted dark blue on his headsculpt, along with his "sunglasses" being painted a nice light metallic blue. The legs are a bit fat proportionally, and I can't exactly tell where the knees are supposed to be, but for the scale they're passable, and Topspin's flip-out feet provide a decent amount of balance for him. As for articluation, Topspin can't move much, even for the size class-- at the hips, forward-and-back at the elbows (yup, his big car-shell kibble bits pretty much completely eliminate any useful shoulder articulation), and his ankles can tilt forward a bit due to the transformation.
Legion class DotM Topspin has a pretty well-done vehicle mode and a pretty nice number of paint apps in said mode, but his robot mode is just bad. The huge shells of his car hanging off his arms are big eyesores, and add to that his overly chubby legs and 2-D arms, and you've got only the "core" part of the robot looking decent. I highly recommend just saving up the little extra money and buying the deluxe version of the character instead.


Autobot Topspin Bio :
The harder the job, the more Autobot Topspin likes it. If he doesn't return from a mission dented and leaking transmission fluid, he considers it a failure. Scratches and dings are badges of honor to him-- signs that he has lived thorugh all that life has to throw at him, and come out smiling.
