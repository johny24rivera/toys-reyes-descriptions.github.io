Transformers Titanium 6" Figures

In the summer of 2006, Hasbro/Galoob decided to expand its diecast metal Titanium line from other properties besides Star Wars, and Transformers was the second property they ventured into with the line. They made two different sizes of Transformers Titanium. The first were 3" tall figurines, which were made entirely out of diecast metal, came with a display stand that showed their allegiance, retailed for $5 U.S., and although they could move at a few points, they were unable to transform. The larger figures, roughly 6" tall, retailed for $15 U.S., came with a personalized display stand, were 40-70% diecast metal, and could transform. The 6" figures are reviewed in this section-- the 3" figurines are not reviewed on this site, since they isn't really much to them, seeing as how they're statues that can move at a few places.
The Titanium line celebrated Transformers across the ages, and many different Transformers from many different eras were represented in the line, from Generation One to Beast Wars to Robots in Disguise and beyond. Some in the line were even made from comic series redesigns that had never even been an actual toyline before, which was a real treat for fans. The line sputtered out in mid-2007 because of low sales, though the odd Titanium store exclusive still came out now and then for a while afterwards.
