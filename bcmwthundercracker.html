Thundercracker (Machine Wars) [Botcon 2013 Attendee-Only Exclusive]

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe (comes in a 2-pack with Mirage )
Difficulty of Transformation : Hard
Color Scheme : Moderately dark dull pea green, moderately dark pale reddish brown, and some white, silver, clear plastic, black, and dark blue
Rating : 6.8

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen Dirge. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen Dirge here .)

Like Skywarp in the boxset, Thundercracker doesn't resemble his "traditional" G1 self much, instead being based on the Machine Wars mold which shares pretty much no relation to what most fans think of as Thundercracker besides being a Decepticon jet. Given that the original Machine Wars Skywarp and Thundercracker both shared the same mold, it's only fitting that their Botcon exclusive versions share the same mold, as well. FunPub kept more to the "template" set by the original MW Thundercracker toy this time, though, with Thundercracker retaining the reddish brown camo on his jet mode that the original had. That said, with this being a larger mold and an exclusive, the camo is considerably more realistic and extensive than on the original MW toy, with the camo patterns looking really well-done and detailed. The white outline around the camo really helps add some additional contrast to the paint apps, and the dull pea green-- even if it SOUNDS kinda gross-- certainly works for the alt mode. There's some additional reddish brown paint apps on the launchers and tailfins, along with a Decepticon symbol in front of a triangle back there and on the sides of the nosecone. There's also the traditional military "white star on a blue circle" logos on his wings. All in all, it's a pretty fitting color scheme for a military jet. In robot mode, unfortunately, a lot of the camoflauge is relegated to only being visible from the back, which makes Thundercracker look a fair bit less interesting. That said, an increased amount of rusty reddish brown on his robot parts does help to mix things up some. This is actually not accurate to his original MW toy, which had some greenish black plastic in place of much of this brown. I much prefer this new color scheme, which keeps the same "feel" as the MW toy but looks more interesting color-wise. Thundercracker also has silver along his visor, his chest, the "rim" of his missile launchers, and his cockpit. He's also got a black head, to give him a bit more color.
Just like with Skywarp, Thundercracker has the same new headsculpt, and obviously since they were redecos of each other, it looks perfect on Thundercracker too. No issues here.
Botcon 2013 Thundercracker is one of the weakest toys out of the full set, but that is almost entirely due to the weak base mold. His color scheme is quite good, fitting for a military jet and with some good contrast between the dull green and rusty brown, along with a good deal of awesome camo paint apps in his jet mode, though I wish he had just a bit more paint in robot mode. Recommended over FunPub's update of MW Skywarp, at any rate.


Thundercracker Bio :
FUNCTION: Decepticon Rebel Warrior
MOTTO: "The best weapon is terror."
Thundercracker is loyal to the cause of unifying the Cybertronian Empire, however he does not believe in Jhiaxus' means of doing such. He remains rebellious within the ranks of the new Decepticons and does not fear displaying his contemptuousness to his superiors.
Often times Thundercracker prefers taking his clone squadron on monotonous patrols and performing menial tasks so as to distance himself from his fellow Decepticons. This also allows him to practice his latest tactics for striking fear in his enemies while acting on his disdain for anything incapable of flight, without feeling judgment by the piercing optics of the land bound Soundwave.
