Bumblebee (High Octane; Power Battler) [AoE]

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme: Black, dull milky yellowish orange, and some light milky gray, metallic moderately light blue, dark metallic gunmetal gray, glossy yellowish orange, silver, and light metallic silvery blue
Rating : 7.5

Bumblebee's alternate mode for his earlier-in-the-movie "High Octane" version is an old '70 Camaro, but in "reverse colors" from his usual color scheme. That is, most of him is black, with his usual yellowish orange "cheesy" yellow being more of an accent color. This is particularly apparent in vehicle mode, where the only yellow is on the bordered stripes around the grill and on the Autobot symbols on the front sides; almost the entire rest of this mode is black plastic, though the windows are thankfully painted a rather nice shade of metallic silvery blue that contrasts against the black very well. The rear windows aren't painted, but in Hasbro's defense these parts fold in on themselves during transformation, and to paint the back windows would have highlighted the gaps in between the plastic pieces there, so this is one of the few times I'm glad paint WASN'T added. I still would've liked paint on the headlights, taillights, or wheel rims, though. The lack of both paint detailing and a relative lack of mold detailing in most places (other than the tires and the grill) makes this mode look a little bland overall. That said, the proportions are pretty spot-on, and the only robot mode extras are little bumps on the back end that form the robot toes, so those are two huge pluses.
Although his transformation is pretty simple-- as is basically required for a toy in the Power Battler subline-- Bumblebee's robot mode looks significantly different from his vehicle mode, with a rather intuitive transformation that involves folding up much of his car mode origami-style into his lower legs, with most of his "robot parts" being hidden inside the interior of the vehicle mode. The end result is a robot mode that has some definite pluses and minuses. First, the minuses-- 'Bee's lower legs aren't all that convincing, with the "front" mold detailing and feet further out front than the upper legs and generally having a very unpleasant, unnaturally segmented look to them. The beefy car sides on the sides of the legs also look pretty weird from the side, as they're bulkier than the entire upper body (the latter of which admittedly SHOULD be a bit bulkier than it is from a side angle, but still). The front sides of the car mode also don't really have anywhere to go, and just hang unceremoniously from Bumblebee's arm pits, not really locking in anywhere or getting out of the way by any measure. That said, Bumblebee's main body is proportioned fairly well (though the chest should poke forward a bit more), and the mold detailing on his robot parts is pretty good, with the approporiate springs, armor plating, and the like all molded in where it should be. (However, the lack of significant door wings on this version is odd.) His headsculpt is also spot-on with some nice metallic blue eyes and a gunmetal gray face. The paint detailing on his chest and waist is also quite good (the grill-chest is completely fake), and all the additional orange-yellow paint and plastic helps break up this mode and make it considerably more visually interesting than the vehicle mode. The orange-yellow paint is of a noticeably lighter, glossier shade than the plastic, though, the latter of which is actually orange much more than it is yellow, in contrast to most of movie 'Bee's other toys. High Octane Bumblebee has a pretty good Power Battler gimmick-- squeeze his legs together and his entire upper body rotates to the side, with him pointing his right arm forward right as the cannon piece on it envelops the hand, like he's about to fire off a shot. His head stays in place despite the rest of his upper body rotating, and it's a very well-excecuted, fun gimmick. However, the articulation otherwise is pretty sub-par-- he can move his knees backward, his left shoulder out to the side, and TECHNICALLY his right shoulder out to the side as well, though the cannon will automatically come over his hand if you do this, and it springs back to its default position as soon as you let it go. Even for a Power Battler, that's a little disappointing.
The Power Battler version of "High Octane" Bumblebee has both some big positives and some big negatives. He has some pretty obnoxious kibble in robot mode, particularly under his arm bits and on his lower legs, and his vehicle mode could use some more paint. His articulation is also a little sub-par even for this subline. However, his robot mode DOES have a good number of paint apps, his vehicle mode is VERY solid, his transformation intuitive yet simple, and his gimmick just plain fun. Overall I'd say he's one of the better Power Battler toys, and I'd recommend him the most if you want a "simple" High Octane Bumblebee toy.

Review by Beastbot
