Ransack (RID 2015)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Mini-Con
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Very dark military milky green, transparent pale purple, and some yellow, dark red, and moderately dull forest green
Rating : 3.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to RID2015 Backtrack. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Backtrack here .)

Ransack is Backtrack's buddy, and the darker of the two. His main color is a very dark military green which-- if you look at it in the light-- has a bit of a milky, "impure" sheen to it, but which I actually think helps on this dark of a shade. (Honestly I wish the green was a bit lighter.) The main contrast color on the main toy itself is a considerably lighter forest green, though it's still a bit dull compared to your "average" shade of green-- the latter is mostly used on some internal robot parts like the upper legs and upper arms, though there's also some green curved stripes on most of the bottom half of the ball mode. As with the other versions of this mold, I wish the curved painted stripes went all around the ball mode, but it still helps give some contrast to all the dark green. Beyond simply these main two shades of green, Ransack has a yellow paint app on his belly blaster/button on the front, and his eyes in robot mode are a dark red. (Unfortunately, his face is the same dark green as the rest of that piece; having no silver on the face like there is on Backtrack doesn't look good.) Finally, for the translucent armor pieces Ransack has a pale purple shade, which looks pretty good itself though I think making the purple a bit lighter/brighter would've helped it contrast against the dark green more.
No mold changes have been made to Ransack.
Honestly, I can't really decide whether Ransack or Backtrack (the regular version) is better. Backtrack's armor looks cooler and his face is painted, but I think Ransack's secondary colors on the main toy are better and his two shades of green complement each other quite well. If you're only going to get one version of this (rather bad) mold, I'd make it the Blizzard Strike Backtrack version; beyond that version, well... Backtrack and Ransack are both on the same plane to me, just different.

Review by Beastbot
