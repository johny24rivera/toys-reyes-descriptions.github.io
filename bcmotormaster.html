The Motormaster (Botcon 2011 Exclusive)

Allegiance: Decepticon
Size : Voyager (comes in a 5-pack with Breakdown , Dead End , Drag Strip , and Wildrider )
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme : Black, dark purple, light dull bluish gray, and some silver, bright green, metallic dark purple, transparent dark purple, pinkish purple, and white
Rating : 9.4

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Animated voyager Optimus Prime. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Animated voyager Optimus Prime here .)

Like other redecos of various versions of Optimus Prime into the Stunticon leader, this Animated version shares the basic black, bluish gray, and dark purple color scheme of Motormaster's G1 version. The particular shades used for the colors on this version are pretty darned snazzy, though-- the black is a VERY black, "midnight-esque" color, and the dark purple pops in a way that's a bit hard to describe, but it goes with the other colors excellently, despite being flat like most of the colors on other Animated figures. The light bluish gray is also of a tint that makes it go well with the purple, to boot. The accent colors are really what make this toy look great, though. The silver just goes brilliantly with the black and purple, and to add that little something extra, there's some bright green. In any real amount, this would look garish, but as an accent color it gives The Motormaster that slight "professional wrestler" vibe he's supposed to have (he's a bit of an homage to the late Randy "Macho Man" Savage) while still going with the other colors quite nicely.
The Motormaster has a new headsculpt, made to look like a "head squeezed into a box", which is how the G1 Motormaster toy looked (to a less exaggerated extent, of course). The grimace on Motormaster's face and the "mustache" looks great, but I'm just not fond of the box around the head on this version-- it just looks off on this body, not fitting with the "line flow" of it at all, in my opinion. I'm also not really liking the pinkish color of his face.
The Motormaster has some excellent, eye-catching colors, and done in a way that pretty much only the Animated line could pull off. The new face sculpt isn't all that great and seems out-of-place, but ignoring that, this is a really nicely done redeco.


The Motormaster Bio :
FUNCTION: Stunticon Leader
MOTTO: "These wheels are made for crushing!"
The Motormaster brings vanity to a whole new level. He annoys the Stunticons (and everyone else) with bombastic proclamations and his habit of referring to himself in the third person. Secretly, he is insecure about his place among the much larger Decepticons. He has reinforced trauma buffers and shock absorbers, making him likely to survive any collision. His greatest weakness is his disloyalty he inspires in those under his command.
