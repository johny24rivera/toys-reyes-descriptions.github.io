(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Generations Drift. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Generations Drift here .)

Shattered Glass Drift's color scheme may at first simply seem like the amounts of red and white on him have been reversed, but he's actually an homage to the popular fourth-wall-breaking Marvel character Deadpool! (It actually makes quite a bit sense, as the opposite of a silent ninja is probably a smack-talking mercenary with no real code of honor.) Once you realize where "Driftpool's" color scheme comes from, this toy does a great job of emanating Deadpool's color scheme while still making SG Drift his own thing. The red and black are primary shades of the color that go very well together, and the black-outlined white detailing-- particularly in vehicle mode-- looks great, with the "rays" on the sides of the car looking like the Japanese "sun" symbol, with the sun being the front tires in this instance. Those are some really creative details there, though Drift's Japanese character on his front hood makes considerably less sense. Apparently it means "decisive", or summat like that. Not really sure how that applies to Driftpool, but whatever-- at least it looks cool, and fits with his Japanese drifter car alt mode. Drift's transparent plastic is so dark as to be almost black, which is fine by me-- not only does this mean it's hard to make out the robot bits on the inside, but it also goes well with the black-painted rear windows. The black lines down the sides of his car front are also great details, both looking like sword tips while hearkening back to certain aspects of Deadpool's costume. It also love how clean the white and black paint apps are on Drift's face-- they look quite striking against the red head, and, in another callback to Deadpool, Drift has completely white eyes.
No mold changes have technically been made to Shattered Glass Drift, but in addition to the large sword and two smaller swords that the mold comes with, this toy ALSO comes with the Blurr remold's two smaller guns. Due to the molding, they can't be sheathed in the car doors, but it's nice for Drift to have both guns and swords, just like Deadpool.
Shattered Glass Drift is a pretty nice nod to Deadpool, but even ignoring that it's a great mold with a color scheme that not only looks good, but does some pretty neat things (like the "rising sun" paint apps on the sides of the car mode). Highly recommended.


Drift (Shattered Glass) Bio :
FUNCTION: Dimensional Fragment Eliminator (Fancy-talk for hunting invaders from other universes)
MOTTO: "Blast 'em again to make sure they stay offline!"
Even amongst the villainous hordes of the Evil Autobots, Drift is considered the most ruthless. Laws and morality have no stay with him, only money and profit... and occasionally thrills. He'd blast his own batch initators if he were paid for the job. Or if he thought it might be fun. Typically given free rein by the Autobot leader, Optimus Prime, to complete his objectives, Drift's methods of taking out his targets are only limited by his imagination. In other words, they are almost boundless. The mercenary relishes dreaming of new ways of taking out his marks, and logging them when he reboots in the morning. Regardless of the elaborate traps he might set, he always ensures that he is there at the final move, with his finger on the trigger or his hand on the blade.
