Mol

Allegiance : Maximal
Size : Deployer Size
Difficulty of Transformation : Very Easy
Color Scheme : Yellow, transparent red, black, and bronze (original); light green, transparent dark blue, black, and dark sky blue (repaint)
Rating : 5.5 (original); 5.6 (repaint)

Beast mode is a mechanical mole. This mode looks very nice, and very fitting for such a creature as a mole. The "nose" is actually a small drill that can turn when you turn a wheel on the back of Mol's head! A pretty nice feature, there. Mol's back feet are also large treads with large toes engraved on the front of them. They are a nice touch, but I wish Hasbro would have put small wheels on the bottom of them so that they could move. You pretty much have to drag Mol on the ground in you want to move him. His front feet are rather large, but very mechanical in their detailing and very fitting for Mol's "plodding" pose. The detailing on Mol (besides the rear legs, where detailing is comparatively scant) is very nice. There are gas pipes on the back of his head, screwheads everywhere, and even some mechanical detailing on the plastic UNDER the transparent red headpiece! The nice tubing and vent detailing on the side of the rear dusted over with black looks very nice and "worn", too. He also has tail lights on the back of his back legs, too! Although Mol looks decidedly cute in this mode, this is okay for a Deployer, since they're more like little helpers than actual transformers. Sadly, Mol's articulation is very limited in this mode; he can only move his drill-nose and his front legs in one area. Thus, Mol's posability is extremely limited.
Mol's weapon mode is, well... bad. Basically you just undo a latch on Mol's beast mode, and an automatic spring puts him up into this position, and then all you have to do is turn his front legs 180 degrees. It's supposed to be some kind of boring tool, but all it really looks like is Mol is shooting sawblades out of his butt (which he is). This mode is definitely lacking in creativity. Also, another interesting note: Although not mention in the instructions, Mol, has little "handles" on the top of his back legs in mole mode. In weapon mode, these can become handles for larger transformers to use to hold him, although I honestly don't know if any Beast Machine transformer CAN hold him, which is a rather big letdown...
The original Mol's color scheme (shown on the left in each of the above pictures) is pretty neat, involving mostly a yellow and transparent red color scheme, with some black and brown thrown into the mix as well. While this color scheme certainly isn't bad, I think the Mol repaint's color scheme is slightly better (shown on the right in each of the above pictures). The Mol repaint has a very cool light green and blue color scheme, and it fits in a bit more with a creature who tunnels in the dark. Still, both are just about as good as the other.
Mol is a very cool and interesting Deployer in beast mode, but his weapon mode is very bad. Get the other Deployers before trying this guy out.


Mol Bio :
FUNCTION: Demolitions
Determined...deliberate...and often depressed, this subterranean demolitions expert can bore his way into areas where no other ally would dare tread. Recently reformatted following eons of deactivation at the hands of the Decepticons, this always dutiful Mol is usually called to search for organic life or to plant sophisticated silicon-encased explosives near Vehicon emplacements. When in bore mode, he can fire his metal tearing, serrated circular blade at attacking Vehicons with circuit slicing accuracy. He projects a negative attitude that makes his fellow Maximals a bit uneasy. Not exactly "the life of the party," but reliable to a fault.
