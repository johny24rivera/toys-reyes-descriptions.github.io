Sky Shadow (Generations)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size: Deluxe
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Black, milky gray, silver, light red, and some transparent light red, white, moderately dark bluish gray, pale metallic bronze, and light milky tan
Rating : 7.8

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Generations Thunderwing. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Generations Thunderwing here .)

"Sky Shadow" here is actually a direct homage to the Japanese character Black Shadow, who himself was a remold of G1 Thunderwing-- get it? Anyways, Black/Sky Shadow's colors really carry over well here, with some really nice red and silver stripes in vehicle mode playing off of his main color of (appropriately) black. Combined with the transparent light red cockpit and missiles, it's all quite eye-catching. In robot mode Sky Shadow ends up looking a bit less impressive, as most of the dynamic red & silver paint apps from his vehicle mode are "hidden", and gray is a much more dominant color. The light milky gray at least has a slight marbly appearance to liven it all up a BIT, though this doesn't carry over to the dull bluish gray on Sky Shadow's arms. The bits of tan plastic used here and there as accents go nicely enough with the other colors, though some paint apps on the gray to break it up a bit would've been appreciated.
Sky Shadow has two mold changes from Thunderwing-- as expected, the head has been changed, and is a pretty spot-on representation of Black Shadow's head, complete with the large "antlers". The center chest piece has also been remolded to match Black Shadow's, and has a more robotic-looking appearance than Thunderwing's chest. The white, bronze, and silver on the chest go together quite well with the surrounding colors, and the detailing in general is really crisp and accurate. No complaints here.
Generations Sky Shadow is not only the first U.S.-release of a previously Japanese-only character, but taken by itself it has a far superior color scheme to Generations Thunderwing's, particularly in vehicle mode. The remolds are also quite well-sculpted, to boot. It's still merely an okay mold, but if you're looking for which version to get, I'd go with this one.
