Razorclaw (Botcon 2009 Attendee-Only Exclusive)

Allegiance: Predacon
Size: Voyager (comes in a 2-pack with Elita-One )
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Milky golden yellow, red, black, and some dark metallic gunmetal gray, pale metallic off-white, and light orange
Rating: 7.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Cybertron Voyager Leobreaker. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Cybertron Voyager Leobreaker here .)

As part of the 5th anniversary of Fun Publications' taking over of Botcon, they released a special set of exclusives in 2009 that actually came from ideas seen in their earlier sets. Razorclaw here is one of them, briefly seen in the "Dawn of Futures Past" comic from Botcon 2006. This same idea of doing Razorclaw from Leobreaker has actually already been done from the Legends class toy of the character in Universe 2.0, but here is gets the bonafide Voyager-class treatment. I'm not usually a fan of yellow, but the G1 Predacons have always been an exception to that usual rule, and Razorclaw here is no different. The golden yellow is quite fitting for a lion, and goes really well with the black and orange/red to create a very "fiery" color scheme, but without being too loud in my opinion. The gunmetal gray used for the technorganic "muscle fiber" is a particularly nice detail, but overall he's got a pretty good amount of paint detailing, the sole exception being the lower arms, which only have the claws painted. The only color I'm not fond of on Razorclaw is the milky metallic off-white-- it looks good against the black, but the other colors are too light for it to effectively serve as a contrasting color-- particularly against the golden yellow, it can be hard to see in those cases. Also of note is that Razorclaw has a slimmed-down version of the Predacon symbol on his upper arms, which looks pretty interesting.
Razorclaw has a new head mold, based pretty heavily on his G1 self. The mane "ponytail" still looks remarkably good hanging off the G1-head, though, and the sculpting on the new head is pretty solid. I do wish Razorclaw's visor was a slightly different from his faceplate to help differentiate the two visually, however. Like most Botcon exclusives, the electronics on the toy have been gutted. The Cyber Key on the figure has also been changed to resemble the "Golden Disk Key" that came with most of the 2006 exclusives, though it's also transparent on this toy for some reason.
Botcon 2009 Razorclaw is a nice homage with great colors, but the mold leaves much to be desired. Combine this with the fact that this toy has already essentially been released at mass-retail (just at Legends class), and only the biggest collectors and/or Predacon fans should consider hunting this guy down.


Razorclaw Bio :
FUNCTION: Predacon Commander
MOTTO: "All good things do indeed succumb to those who wait!"
At the end of the Third Cybertronian War, the Decepticon army, at long last, was no more. With many Decepticons destroyed or in hiding, Razorclaw came to realize that if the Autobots were to ever be defeated, a new army must carry on the fight. A Predacon Army! In time the "Predacon" empire became the main opposition to the Autobots. They attacked Cybertron with ferocity never before seen. The Predacons appeared to have a chance at achieving what the Decepticons never could... victory! However, just as with the Decepticons, treachery and deceit existed within the Predacon ranks, and the three who would become the Tripredacus Council assassinated Razorclaw... or so it was believed. Now, many stellar cycles after the reformatting of Cybertron, the Predacons have returned, with Razorclaw once again leading the army. What happened and how he returned is a mystery. What is known is that he is stronger and more determined than ever, and he will do what is needed to take back Cybertron and destroy every last Maximal!
