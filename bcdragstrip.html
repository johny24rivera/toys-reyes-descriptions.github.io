Drag Strip (Botcon 2011 Exclusive)

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Deluxe (comes in a 5-pack with Breakdown , Dead End , The Motormaster , and Wildrider )
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Yellow, black, fuchsia, and some transparent cherry red, light sky blue, and silver
Rating: 9.2

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Animated Arcee. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Animated Arcee here .)

Drag Strip here, although an homage to her G1 self in many respects just like her other fellow Animated Stunticons, has a bit more of a drastic change; for one thing, this version's female. Part of the decision to tweak the "sex" of the character for this universe's version likely had to do with wanting to use four different deluxe sportscar molds from the Animated line for the set, but I have to say they pulled it off here quite well. She has G1 Drag Strip's colors-- yellow and black are her primary colors, along with several nice fuchsia and yellow lines running down her form. The paint apps are scattered quite well across the toy so that no one section is simply a big block of color-- definitely a good thing, as I'm personally not usually a fan of yellow as a main color. She also has the pale blue face that G1 Drag Strip has, which helps to add a bit more color to the robot mode (along with a few isolated silver paint apps). That said, even with the different colors her head still looks too much like Arcee's-- it doesn't make the transition to a new character as well as Dead End's does. Still, what really impresses me about this toy is that the stripes in robot mode, combined with the colors and swords, make her a pretty obvious and deliberate "Kill Bill" homage. That turns what would normally be an okay G1 homage into a brilliant double-homage, and is what really makes me like this toy.
No mold changes have been made to Drag Strip.
Animated Drag Strip is certainly more different from her G1 counterpart than the other Stunticons, but this certainly isn't a bad thing in her case-- the well-done paint apps and overall look that gives a nod to "Kill Bill" is ingenious and quite well-done, to the point where I'd recommend this over the original Animated Arcee, despite my general dislike of the color yellow.

Drag Strip is angry that the Autobots control Cybertron, that she has to take orders from those windbags The Motormaster and Wildrider, and that she was protoformed in a wimpy-looking Autobot shell; which fuels her already high lust for violence. New laser swords are well-suited for carving her name in anybot's armor, but those aggravating this fem-brawler-bot should expect to receive a headbutt between the optics and a swift kick to the exhaust port.
