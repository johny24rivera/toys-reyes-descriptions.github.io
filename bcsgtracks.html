Turbo Tracks (Shattered Glass) [Botcon 2012 Exclusive]

Allegiance : Autobot (Shattered Glass)
Size : Deluxe (comes in a 6-pack with Gigatron , Metalhawk , Soundwave , Treadshot , and Ultra Magnus )
Difficulty of Transformation : Medium
Color Scheme: Black, light red, and some silver, royal purple, dark metallic orange, dull yellow, metallic gunmetal gray, white, and metallic blue
Rating : 9.3

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Autobot Alliance Turbo Tracks. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Autobot Alliance Turbo Tracks here .)

Like most of the other Shattered Glass toys in the main 2012 Botcon "Invasion" boxset, Turbo Tracks here gets his main color scheme and inspiration from another toy-- namely, the eHobby toy Road Rage, which was a similar recolor of the original Tracks toy. The Autobot symbols on Shattered Glass Tracks are also a rather fuchsia shade of color, allowing one to repurpose him as a "normal" Autobot without needed to resticker his allegiance symbols, which was a nice thought. Shattered Tracks' main color scheme is a light red (obviously the opposite of dark blue!), with black serving as the main foil color in vehicle mode. He has plenty of paint apps, though, so you don't need to worry about one part of the toy being too one-tone. All of his windows are painted black, along with a nifty stripe down the sides. His bumper is painted silver, his taillights and headlights dull orange, and there's a neat flame decal on his roof, which is close to Tracks' G1 flame symbol-- certainly closer than the more stylized flames found on Autobot Alliance Turbo Tracks. In robot mode, white and a rather nice shade of royal purple make themselves known, and both look fantastic against both the red and the black-- the purple in particular. There's also some nice gunmetal gray on his legs to give him a more metallic look there.
No mold changes have been made to Shattered Glass Tracks.
Shattered Glass Tracks has a great color scheme even when taken by himself, with numerous paint apps and a color scheme that really meshes together while both being rather original and refraining from being too loud. Add in the double homage to Road Rage, and this toy has my favorite color scheme from the core boxset for the 2012 figures. Recommended if the cost of an exclusive toy doesn't bother you.


Turbo Tracks Bio :
FUNCTION: Warrior
MOTTO: "There was once a prophecy that there would emerge the greatest looking Autobot of all and his name would be Tracks."
Tracks is simply the greatest thing in the Autobot army, at least that's how he sees it. He dismisses those who think he is not the best looking Autobot of all time, calling them a bunch of "haters". If you take enough time to admire his form, you will eventually learn to appreciate him as much as he appreciates himself. However, to date, this theory has yet to be proven. One quality no Autobot will dispute is Tracks' bravery. He is always willing to be one of the first to charge into battle with the goal of destroying more enemies than anyone else, all in an effort to prove how utterly amazing he truly is. In vehicle mode he can achieve a maximum speed of 300 MPH. If he needs to move faster he can transform into a flying car, allowing him to achieve sub-sonic speeds. He is armed with two heat-seeking missile launchers and a weapon that disrupts the energy flow inside enemies causing unpredictable malfunctions, most commonly the loss of sight. Tracks' greatest weakness is his tendency to admire himself in a reflection while in the midst of battle, making him an easy target.
