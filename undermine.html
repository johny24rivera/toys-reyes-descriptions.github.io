Undermine

Allegiance : Decepticon
Size : Scout (Basic)
Homeworld : Jungle Planet
Cyber Key Code : s48a
Difficulty of Transformation: Very Easy
Color Scheme : Dark bluish green, light jungle green, moderately dark gray, and some dull metallic gold, metallic light bluish silver, metallic light blue, and dark red
Rating : 7.1

Beast mode is a spinosaurus, and is one of the best beast modes I've ever seen on ANY Transformer. Holy CRUD, this mode is awesome. The proportions are spot-on, there's great techno-organic mold detailing all over the place, and the color scheme is just right for a dinosaur transformer-- different shades of green, with some nice metallic detailing to show off Undermine's mechanical parts. His hand claws look especially wicked-- I love that splayed position they're molded in. His head is also molded just perfectly, and when you open up his mouth for him to give a "roar", it looks totally natural, not rigid like on many beast Transformers with such articulation. Undermine's other articulation in this mode is at the shoulders, hips (at two points), knees (at two points), and ankles, plus three points of articulation in the tail, so you can get some pretty mean poses out of him. The only things I don't like about this mode is that the robot head is a bit visible behind the neck, but it's not much of a robot head anyways, so I'm not as concerned about it as I would be otherwise. The part where his tail connects to his main body is also a bit loose, so his tail comes off a little easier than I'd like. When you plug in his Cyber Key to the back of his fin, a ramming sword flips out so Undermine can have another sharp edge to thrust at his opponents in this mode-- not outstandingly cool, but certainly not a bad gimmick, at least in this mode.
In robot mode, however, we find out the price for such an awesome beast mode-- a lanky robot mode that seems largely thrown together as a second thought. The "head" is almost non-existant, consisting of just the dinosaur finÂ  with a small gold helmet on the bottom of the front side with two red eye slits. There's no mouth or faceplate to speak of. Plus, activating the Cyber Key gimmick in this mode looks awkward, as he has a big sword coming out of his forehead, ick. His chest is skinny, so the dinosaur arms are hanging off the shoulders to try to give the chest the appearance of having more mass, but this totally does not work, it just looks like Undermine has a pair of extra arms on his shoulders. I wouldn't mind the arm-claws made from the halves of his dino head much, if the detailed, solid-looking halves were facing outward, but they're not-- instead, the more visible side of his arms in this mode are mostly hollow, with several pegs and a few bolts visible-- it loks rather ugly. He also doesn't really have elbows, it just goes from his uppper arms to his claws. Plus, Undermine can't hold his (rather cool) tail-mace in his hands, it fits into a slot on the top of his claws, with looks a little odd. At least his waist and legs look alright, though. His articulation in this mode, besides the same leg articulation mentioned in dinosaur mode, also consists of movement at the shoulders, two points of movement at his claws/elbows, and due to his transformation, he's able to move at the waist back-and-forth, so he can "look up" using this odd point of articulation.
Even though his robot mode looks very weird and slapped-together, I can't bring myself to give Undermine too low of a score due to his incredibly awesome beast mode that sets a high standard for future beast TFs' alt modes. There are better beast TFs in Cybertron, to be sure, but none have a better beast mode than Undermine-- just also be aware that few have WORSE robot modes than Undermine, as well.


Undermine Bio:
Cruel and predatory Undermine serves as a scout and spy for Scourge, unquestioned ruler of the Jungle Planet. He enjoys hunting, and will often toy with his prey before moving in to rip out its power source with his powerful fangs. Silent, cunning and all but impossible to sneak up on, Undermine is possibly the greatest hunter the Universe has ever seen. The only reason you haven't heard about him before now is that none of his prey have survived long enough to talk about it.
