Optimus Prime (Premier Edition; Voyager) [The Last Knight]

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Voyager
Difficulty of Transformation : Hard
Color Scheme : Dark milky semi-metallic blue, light milky gray, moderately dark red, and some silver, dark gray, dark metallic blue, black, light electric blue, and light orange
Rating : 9.3

Optimus Prime's back with a new mold for this design in the voyager class, and in vehicle mode it looks pretty accurate to the actual Western Star truck vehicle model. The proportions are pretty spot-on, though the tires are perhaps a tad small for a truck-- I mean, he does sit pretty low to the ground. The only thing I would actually qualify as an extra is a bit of Optimus "skirt" piece sticking out on the top of part of the rear section, and the edges of his lower legs, which are otherwise covered up by the trailer hitch piece above them. Still, for a design as seemingly impossible to make a reality as Optimus' AoE/TLK model, that's pretty darned good. Plus, Optimus' shield accessory can be pegged into his trailer hitch in this mode, covering up even more of these minor extras. (His sword accessory is stored underneath the bottom of this mode-- slid between two tabs.) Mold-wise, the detailing is fairly sparse on the vehicle portions, as is correctly the case for this model of a truck, at least in the front. The wheels have some pretty good tread detailing, though, and there's quite a few lines on the tanks on the sides, the trailer hitch area near the back, and the rear end, and there's the appropriate vents 'n such on the nose and front grill. Color-wise of course he's in your traditional Movie Prime colors, being predominatly blue with some nifty red flame paint apps on the front nose and sides around the cockpit. The blue plastic used for this toy is particuarly good-looking, being of a milky, semi-metallic variety with quite a lot of glitter in it-- thus making it about as close as unpainted plastic can get to an actual truck chassis. There's also a bit of a painted metallic version of this color around the front grill. The red is about half paint, half milky plastic (that on the truck nose is mostly plastic). The front grill itself is painted silver, along with the bit above the front window and the tanks along the sides. Unfortunately, that all-too-prevalent, bland, light milky gray plastic is back on this toy, used in this mode on the trailer hitch and smokestacks. *sigh* Will Hasbro every stop using this awful color for its gray plastics? Of course, there's also some black-- on the wheels, windows, and a bit on some small connector pieces behind the front wheels.
Optimus Prime's transformation to robot mode is a bit more complex than most other TLK toys (even Premier Edition ones), particularly when going from robot to vehicle as it requires placing some parts in juust the right place initially, but the end result is pretty worth it. Granted, there's still some kibble here-- the most egregious of which are the sides of his front nose sticking out the sides and back of his lower legs. There's also his truck doors sticking out of his back a little, and his roof accordioned up behind that. However, the rest of him is pretty movie-accurate and well-proportioned, which is pretty amazing given the size class and this seemingly impossible design. The mold detailing for all his robot bits is absolutely spot-on and very impressive-- there's a lot of little mechanical greebles all OVER his lower legs and chest especially, but even on the "less detailed" parts like hos arms and upper legs, there's lots of movie-accurate curved armor detailing and some circuitry here and there, with movie-accurate shoulder caps and smokestacks behind his upper back. His headsculpt is also spot-on accurate. Optimus' color scheme loses most of the red in this mode, behind the bit on his chest and on the sides of his legs. The blue is still pretty apparent, but a dark gray is now present on his arms, upper legs, and the sides of his chest, making him a bit darker in color. There's still some of that blah light gray on his feet and sword, but it's relatively minor in this mode. There is a nice amount of silver paint in this mode-- on the waist, lower legs, biceps, and much of the chest-- but having it abruptly disappear on some of the dark gray parts doesn't look right. Optimus' color doesn't change suddenly there on the model, I'm not sure why they wouldn't want a closer paint-plastic transition on this toy. Looks kinda weird, especially on the chest. This is also apparent on his blue "collar" pieces, where the front two are painted metallic blue but the last isn't, for some reason. (There is some more metallic blue on his pecs and lower arms, which looks nice.) It unfortunately makes Optimus look slightly unfinished, albeit still with a lot more paint than most other TLK toys. Optimus' head also has silver paint where appropriate, but the light blue paint apps on his eyes are cartoonishly oversized, which unfortunately is a big deal since this is right where your eyes normally go-- why didn't they get this right and make those apps smaller to match the actual molded-in eye details? Such an odd decision. There's also some light electric blue on the front of much of his shield piece, which can either be held in his fists like a gun or pegged into the side of either of his lower arms. His sword, which has the blade painted a spiffy light orange, can either be held in a hand or sheathed in a special slot behind his back. For articulation in this mode, Optimus can move at the neck, shoulders (at two points, and two more points on each outer "shoulder armor" piece), elbows, rotation at the waist, and movement at the hips (at three points) and the knees. Some ankle movement would've been appreciated, but otherwise he's very articulated and quite poseable in this mode.
TLK voyager Optimus Prime still has some kibble issues-- such as the truck nose-bits behind his lower legs and roof behind his back-- but overall, it's a much better try at the design than his various AoE toys. His proportions are great in both modes, his mold detailing fantastic when it needs to be, his articulation is very nice, and the plastic used for his blue and red are of a particularly awesome shade, even if he could've used a bit more silver paint in his robot mode and better eye paint apps. A definite upgrade, and if you get no other toy of this design, I'd make it this one (so long as you're old/patient enough to handle the more complex transformation).

Review by Beastbot
