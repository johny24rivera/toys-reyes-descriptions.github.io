Bumblebee (Power Battler; AoE)

Allegiance : Autobot
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : "Cheese" orangish yellow, blac, and some light metallic silver blue, silver, and moderately light semi-metallic greenish blue
Rating : 6.3

Bumblebee's vehicle mode is pretty much what you'd expect at this point-- he's a orangish-yellow-and-black Chevrolet Camaro, of the newest model (as of the time of the movie), with the most noticeable difference for this version being the more slanted headlights. The vents on the front hood are also a bit more pronounced, as well. Generally the proportions in this mode are very good-- as you'd expect from a TF with a licensed alt mode. You can see little bits of the robot mode inside the side windows (which are "open" and not filled up with transparent plastic or anything), and there's some rather ugly lines across his windshield due to his transformation, but the rest of this mode is devoid of robot extras. (That said, due to the mold breakup the middle sections of the front grill are black but not the sides, which is a bit odd). The paint apps are what are absolutely needed, and basically no more-- 'Bees got the one thick stripe going down the top of this mode with the little stripes right beside it, and the headlights and rear and front windshield are painted a nice metallic silvery blue, which is pretty typical paint used for windows and contrasts nicely with the orange-yellow; there's also a bit of black on the rear bumper, though the taillights are unpainted, which is a bummer. The rear spoiler also should've been painted as well. Overall there's enough to make for a decent color scheme, but only just; I wish just a few more paint apps/colors were visible in this mode to liven things up a bit.
Bumblebee's Power Battler gimmick is a "Power Punch"; in robot mode his right fist is hugely oversized. When you rotate 'Bee at the waist to the right, his right arm extends forward a pretty good amount for said "punch"; the waist's default position is facing straightforward, though, so as soon as you let go of the waist the fist will go back to its neutral position, shown above. The core robot body, upper legs, and robot head are the highlights of this mode; all three are quite excellently proportioned, and have oodles of that really nice mold detailing we've come to expect from movieverse toys. The headsculpt is spot-on, complete with a black stripe down the middle of the head, a silver face, and blue eyes. The silver paint on the piston "abs" and black on the faux car chest looks good, as well. The waist and upper legs also look pretty good and proportional, fitting in with 'Bee's more angular appearance for this movie. Unfortunately, his feet and arms are where this mode fall apart. The left arm is okay, but more what I'd expect from a Legion-sized figure; the arm is merely molded onto the inside of a big part of the car mode (in this case, most of one of the rear sides of the car mode). It generally doesn't look good. The biggest issue is with 'Bee's "Power Punch" arm, which simply sacrifices too much for its gimmick. It can't be posed at all, and is stuck in its "collapsed" configuration where Bumblebee basically has a third of the car mode hanging off his arm, with a fair amount of extending behind his back as well. (Even when "punching" forward, it still looks off.) At least there's some rather cool semi-metallic greenish blue paint on the top of the first, though; it goes quite well with the other colors and is quite a unique shade, to the point where I wish it was used a bit more on the toy. The legs are also pretty weak, being simply the halves of the vehicle front with little panels of detailing folded onto the front that look like hollow detailing of Bumblebee's leg details on the movie model. First off, the detailing panels are way too small, and don't cover up the car halves much at all, making them look doubly weird; and second, the hollow detailing just doesn't look good, not like more "normal" mold detailing such as that on 'Bee's chest and upper legs does. At least he's got little protrusions at the rear of his car half-feet, though, so he's pretty stable. For articulation-- ignoring the "Power Punch" gimmick-- Bumblebee can move at the right shoulder (at two points), and backwards at each knee. Thus he's not poseable at all; granted this isn't the focus of the Power Battler toys, but I don't see why head rotation or hip movement wasn't added.
Power Battler Bumblebee has a pretty decent vehicle mode, but his "Power Punch" gimmick in robot mode is simply too much of a sacrifice; there's no way you can make his robot mode appear naturally posed, and having one huge oversized fist constantly reared back in comparison to one normal-sized arm just looks odd. His legs are also very weak-looking and a disappointment; one of the worst Power Battler molds, and not recommended. If you want a simpler AoE Bumblebee toy, go for the Flip 'n Change figure instead.

Review by Beastbot
