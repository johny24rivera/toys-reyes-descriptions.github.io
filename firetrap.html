Firetrap

Allegiance : Autobot
Size : Scout
Difficulty of Transformation : Easy
Color Scheme : Dark forest green, dull light tannish orange, dark gray, and some light metallic silvery blue, pale metallic bronze, silver, dark brownish gray, and metallic copper
Rating : 8.7

(NOTE: Because this is a repaint, this is not a full-blown review. This mainly covers any changes made to the mold and the color scheme, and merely compares it to Revenge of the Fallen Scattorshot. For a review on the mold itself, read the review of Revenge of the Fallen Scattorshot here .)

Firetrap has a considerably more military-esque color scheme than his predecessor Scattorshot, which is certainly appropriate for the mold. A dark forest green is the main color, and certainly is a military shade. The main "foil" color for the green is a tannish orange (mostly visible in robot mode), which contrasts quite well against said green-- though it's not a color combo I'd write home about, on the other hand. There's also a few shades of gray here and there as well, which serve to give a bit more color variation to the plastic I suppose, but are otherwise pretty unremarkable. The highlight of Firetrap are his paint apps-- mostly of the gold and silver variety. Both contrast quite well with the green, and the gold complements the brownish tan in robot mode fairly well, to boot. The gold stripe running along the sides of the vehicle mode really helps the color scheme there in particular, and keeps Firetrap from looking too one-tone in that mode. The windows are also all painted a nice silvery blue, which goes well with the dark green as well. I also quite like the Autobot symbols on the sides with outline stripes around them-- it sort of makes Firetrap look like he's part of some kind of special Autobot defense team or something.
No mold changes have been made to Firetrap.
Firetrap is a pretty appropriate, nice recolor of a great mold. The dark green and brownish tan/gold color combo works quite well, and the paint apps only help that, helping to keep him from looking boring in either mode. Recommended a little over RotF Scattorshot.


Firetrap Bio :
To say that Firetrap is enthusiastic about specialized ammunition is an understatement. He takes every chance he can to try out unusual shells for his cannons. He's espeecially happy whenever he can spring strange munitions on an unsuspecting Decepticon target. It's just bad luck for Sunspot that he happened to be flying over right after Firetrap secured some brand-new anti-aircraft ammo.
